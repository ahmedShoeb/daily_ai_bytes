## Key Points

Modal has optimized AI inference server replica scaling from multiple kiloseconds to just tens of seconds through four key techniques
Four key ingredients: cloud buffers, custom filesystem, checkpoint/restore for CPU initialization, and CUDA checkpoint/restore for GPU initialization
Cloud buffers maintain healthy idle GPUs to take on new load quickly, removing tens of minutes of latency from instance allocation
Custom filesystem (ImageFS) serves container images lazily from a multi-tier cloud-native cache, cutting container start times by about a minute
CPU checkpoint/restore fast-forwards through host-side initialization, reducing time by about 10x for loading host-side components
GPU memory snapshotting speeds up device-side initialization by 4-10x, reducing container start time from several minutes to tens of seconds
Modal's system enables 40x faster inference spin-up: 50 seconds instead of 2,000 seconds (33+ minutes)
The platform has handled tens of millions of replicas across CPU and GPU snapshots for hundreds of organizations
GPU Allocation Utilization is critical - most organizations achieve less than 70% utilization, often closer to 10-20% with traditional approaches
Modal supports a variety of AI workloads including document processing, voice applications, and smaller LLMs that can scale to thousands of GPUs as needed
