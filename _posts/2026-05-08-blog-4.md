## Key Points

- Subquadratic, a Miami-based startup, has launched a model with a 12-million-token context window - far exceeding the current industry standard of about 1 million tokens
- The company claims its Subquadratic Selective Attention (SSA) architecture scales linearly in both compute and memory with respect to context length, unlike traditional transformers that scale quadratically
- SubQ's model reportedly runs 52 times faster than dense attention at 1 million tokens and achieves 92.1% on needle-in-a-haystack retrieval at 12 million tokens
- The model scores 83 on MRCR v2 benchmark, beating OpenAI's GPT-5.5 by nine points, and 82.4% on SWE-bench, edging out Anthropic's Opus 4.6 and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro
- Subquadratic plans to offer a model with a 50-million-context window soon, targeting Q4 release
- The company is launching two beta products: an API with the 12M-token window and SubQ Code, a CLI agent built on the same model
- Unlike other approaches that sacrifice performance for efficiency, SubQ claims SSA maintains content-dependent selection without quadratic scaling costs
- The company has raised $29 million at a $500 million valuation from investors including former SoftBank Vision Fund partner Javier Villamizar and Tinder co-founder Justin Mateen
- Previous attempts to solve the quadratic attention problem include fixed-pattern sparse attention (Longformer), state-space models (Mamba, RWKV), hybrid architectures, and learning-based approaches like DeepSeek's Native Sparse Attention
- While impressive, the article notes cautionary tales in this space, like Magic.dev's 2024 announcement of a 100M-token model that hasn't seen widespread adoption
