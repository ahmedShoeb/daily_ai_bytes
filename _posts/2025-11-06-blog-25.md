## Key Points

- Microsoft is investing $15.2 billion USD in the UAE between 2023 and 2029, focusing on AI and cloud infrastructure.
- The investment includes a $1.5 billion equity stake in G42 and significant capital expenses for AI and cloud datacenters.
- Microsoft has secured export licenses from the U.S. Commerce Department to ship advanced GPUs to the UAE, enabling access to cutting-edge AI models.
- The UAE leads globally in per capita AI usage, with nearly 60% of its population utilizing generative AI.
- Microsoft aims to skill one million people in the UAE by the end of 2027, with initiatives targeting government employees, students, and teachers.
- New centers, including a Global Engineering Development Center and an AI for Good Lab, have been established in Abu Dhabi to foster talent and address regional challenges.
- Microsoft, G42, and MBZUAI co-founded the Responsible AI Future Foundation (RAIFF) to promote ethical AI standards.
- An Intergovernmental Assurance Agreement (IGAA) between Microsoft and G42 ensures compliance with U.S. standards in areas like cybersecurity and data protection.

## Key Topics Discussed

Hey podcast listeners! Get ready for some big news from Microsoft about their massive commitment to the United Arab Emirates. Microsoft is pouring a whopping $15.2 billion USD into the UAE between 2023 and 2029, a move that's all about boosting technology, nurturing talent, and strengthening trust. This isn't money they're raising in the UAE; it's money they're spending there, aiming to not just grow their business but also contribute significantly to the local economy and the relationship between the U.S. and the UAE.

A huge chunk of this investment, over $7.3 billion by the end of 2024 and another $7.9 billion by 2029, is going into cutting-edge AI and cloud infrastructure. This includes a $1.5 billion equity investment in G42, the UAE's very own sovereign AI company, and substantial capital for expanding their datacenter capabilities. They're even bringing world-leading AI tech from the U.S. to the UAE, having secured export licenses for powerful GPUs, which means access to advanced AI models from big names like OpenAI and Anthropic.

And it's paying off! The UAE is actually a global leader in AI adoption, with nearly 60% of its population using generative AI. Microsoft isn't just about the tech; they're also heavily invested in people. They're on a mission to skill one million people in the UAE by the end of 2027, partnering with government entities and educational institutions to upskill employees, students, and teachers. Plus, they've set up a Global Engineering Development Center and an AI for Good Lab in Abu Dhabi, which are all about fostering local talent and using AI to tackle humanitarian challenges across the Middle East and Africa.

Finally, and super importantly, this investment is built on a foundation of trust. Microsoft, G42, and the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence have launched the Responsible AI Future Foundation to promote ethical AI standards across the Middle East and the Global South. There's also a unique Intergovernmental Assurance Agreement between Microsoft and G42, developed with input from both the U.S. and UAE governments, to ensure top-notch cybersecurity, data protection, and responsible AI practices. It's a comprehensive strategy designed to ensure that AI is developed and deployed responsibly, strengthening international ties and fostering mutual confidence. Microsoft is clearly in it for the long haul, aiming to empower others and generate lasting local opportunities and growth.
