## Key Points

Anthropic embedded about six engineers inside the NSA to deploy its Mythos cyber model for offensive operations, according to Financial Times reporting.
This arrangement occurs while Anthropic is simultaneously suing the Pentagon, which oversees the NSA, for designating the company as a 'supply-chain risk' and setting an August deadline to remove Claude from military systems.
Mythos Preview can find and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in every major operating system and browser, including discovering a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD, with exploit pipelines costing under $2,000 to run.
Anthropic presents Mythos as too dangerous for public release but has expanded access from 50 to about 150 organizations across more than 15 countries including NATO, Samsung, Okta, and EU cybersecurity agencies.
The company faces a paradoxical position: restricting Claude's military use in some contexts while actively supporting the NSA with offensive cyber capabilities.
