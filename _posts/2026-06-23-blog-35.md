## Key Points

The AI industry faces a massive financial challenge, needing to generate $2-3 trillion in annual revenue by 2030 to justify the trillions being spent on data center infrastructure
OpenAI and Anthropic represent 70-90% of AI compute demand and must grow exponentially to meet their compute commitments, requiring them to double in size every year until 2029
190GW of planned data centers will cost $9.5-15 trillion to build, requiring massive debt issuance of $500 billion to $1 trillion annually
NVIDIA's trillion-dollar revenue projection depends heavily on just three major clients building servers for Microsoft, Google and Meta
Companies are starting to cap AI token spending as they can't measure ROI - Uber, T-Mobile, and Brex have implemented monthly spending limits
AI companies are transitioning to token-based billing which is revealing true costs, with CFOs reporting no visibility into spending until after billing
The author argues AI services are too expensive, unreliable, and lack measurable ROI despite massive investments
The AI industry operates as a circular economy where companies feed money to hyperscalers who reinvest in them, creating artificial demand
A forthcoming investigative story promises to reveal damaging information that could potentially burst the AI bubble
