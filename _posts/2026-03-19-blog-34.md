## Key Points

- After one year at Anthropic, key reflections include: breakthrough successes often start as 1-2 people's side projects (like Claude Code, Cowork, MCP, Artifacts)
- Being 'AGI-pilled' (believing in artificial general intelligence) is a skill that can be developed over time
- Software engineers today look very different than a year ago, showing rapid adaptation to AI technologies
- Roles are becoming both more manager-like (directing AI agents) and more individual-contributor-like (everyone becoming builders)
- Work-life balance has gotten worse across Anthropic as progress accelerates along the exponential curve
- Anthropic released new research analyzing ~74,000 conversations from professors using Claude AI in collaboration with Northeastern University
- Key finding #1: Educators are builders, not just users of AI - creating interactive simulations, grading rubrics, and data dashboards with Claude Artifacts
- Key finding #2: 77% of teaching and classroom instruction uses are collaborative, while 65% of financial/fundraising tasks are fully delegated to AI
- Key finding #3: 49% of grading conversations showed automation patterns, but faculty rated this as AI's least effective application, revealing a disconnect
- Claude Code now helps programmers learn while coding through lite mode (explaining best practices) and learn-by-doing mode (pausing for human input at critical points)
