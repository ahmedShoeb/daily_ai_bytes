## Key Points

The post analyzes Pope Leo XIV's encyclical 'Magnifica Humanitas' on AI ethics released by the Vatican in May 2026
The document addresses the social ethics of integrating AI into society, continuing the tradition of Pope Leo XIII's 'Rerum novarum' on industrial revolution ethics
The encyclical notes that AI systems are more 'cultivated' than 'built', with developers having limited understanding of their internal workings and representations
It warns about cultural biases in AI responses that reflect the assumptions of designers rather than true objectivity
The document highlights environmental concerns about AI's energy consumption and its heavy demands on natural resources
It cautions against delegating sensitive human decisions to automated systems lacking compassion, mercy, and forgiveness
The encyclical emphasizes the need for clear accountability in AI systems and regulation of data as a public good
It warns that AI tends to amplify existing power imbalances, benefiting those with economic resources and data access
Includes a J.R.R. Tolkien quote about doing what we can in our time to help future generations
Author Simon Willison notes he had previously predicted the Pope weighing in on AI during a 2026 predictions podcast
