## Key Points

- Anthropic has launched Claude Science, an AI workbench designed specifically for scientific researchers
- Claude Science integrates over 60 curated skills and connectors for genomics, single-cell analysis, proteomics, structural biology, and cheminformatics
- The platform generates fully reproducible scientific artifacts with auditable history, including figures, manuscripts, and the code that created them
- It manages compute resources automatically, scaling from single GPU to hundreds as needed, and works on existing lab infrastructure (laptops, Linux boxes, or HPC clusters)
- The system uses specialist agents to query and synthesize data from hundreds of specialized scientific sources like UniProt, PDB, Ensembl, and scientific journals
- Claude Science includes a reviewer agent that checks citations and calculations, flagging and correcting errors automatically
- Early beta users have used it for tasks like single-cell RNA sequencing analysis, CRISPR screen design, protein structure prediction, and cheminformatics
- Manifold Bio used Claude Science to nominate tissue-targeting medicine targets by assessing surface expression, trafficking, and safety criteria
- Neuroscientist Jérôme Lecoq at the Allen Institute used it to create a multi-agent system that writes long-form scientific reviews, reducing what used to take 2 years down to much shorter timelines
- Epidemiologist Stephen Francis at UCSF Brain Tumor Center used Claude Science to accelerate germline variant analysis, completing work in roughly one-tenth the previous time
- Claude Science is available in beta for Claude Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise users on macOS and Linux
- Anthropic is offering up to $30,000 in credits and Modal is providing up to $2,000 in compute for select AI for Science projects through July 15, 2026
