## Key Points

- NVIDIA Cosmos Predict 2.5 is a large-scale world model that generates physically plausible videos conditioned on text, images, or video clips
- The blog provides a comprehensive guide for parameter-efficient fine-tuning of Cosmos Predict 2.5 using LoRA and DoRA techniques
- Fine-tuning helps adapt the model to specific domains like robot manipulation without expensive full model training
- LoRA and DoRA inject small trainable adapter modules into the frozen base model, reducing memory requirements while keeping adapters small and portable
- The approach enables fine-tuning on a single GPU and allows flexible swapping of adapters for different domains at inference time
- Training uses a dataset of 92 robot manipulation videos with text prompts describing pick-and-place tasks
- The method addresses issues where the base model hallucinates human hands, fails to use the correct hand, and exhibits jitter in videos
- Both LoRA and DoRA achieve similar performance improvements, with DoRA offering potential stability benefits at very low ranks
- The guide includes practical implementation details including data preparation, training scripts, and inference workflows
- Results show that training for 100 epochs (about 2.5 hours on 8x H100 GPUs) substantially improves geometric consistency and instruction following
