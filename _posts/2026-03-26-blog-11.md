## Key Points

- METR conducted a tabletop exercise simulating access to AI systems with 200-hour time horizons (predicted to be available in 12-18 months) to understand future AI-augmented workflows
- Researchers simulated 2 days of work and estimated 3-5x productivity uplift compared to current 12-hour time horizon AI systems
- Key observations: No time to develop ideas before implementation (agents implement ideas immediately), overnight work sequencing becomes critical, and project prioritization becomes a major bottleneck
- New workflows emerged: declarative workflows (writing design docs for agents), speculative execution (starting experiments before knowing if needed), and proofs of correctness (agents proving their work meets specifications)
- Serial bottlenecks become dominant: human data review, ML experiments, and feedback cycles could consume 100+ times more time than actual AI execution work
- Participants noted human feedback becomes the primary bottleneck, but agents could potentially predict feedback and data to optimize workflow
- Junior researchers might struggle in this AI-augmented world compared to more experienced colleagues who can better direct AI systems
- The exercise revealed complex organizational challenges around decision-making consistency and storing feedback at appropriate levels of generality
