## Key Points

- Anthropic is turning its February 2026 research project into a consumer-facing product called AI Fluency Scorecard
- Users will be able to generate a personal AI fluency report that analyzes their activity across Chat, Cowork, and Claude Code sessions
- The scorecard evaluates 11 observable behaviors based on the 4D AI Fluency Framework developed with academics Rick Dakan and Joseph Feller
- Scoring covers three main pillars: Delegation, Description, and Discernment
- The system presents results as a fraction (e.g., 7.5 out of 11) with guidance on areas to strengthen
- This builds on Anthropic's AI Fluency Index study from February 2026 that analyzed 9,830 anonymized Claude conversations
- The study found iteration and refinement to be the strongest predictor of good AI use
- The scorecard creates a personal feedback loop to nudge users toward behaviors that lead to safer outcomes
- This is part of a broader push to position Claude as a skill people can develop through Anthrophic Academy, AI Fluency course series, and partnerships
- Rollout timeline and tier availability are currently unclear
