## Key Points

- Google is developing a collections system for NotebookLM that would allow grouping multiple notebooks under a single heading
- This feature appears early in development with no official confirmation or timeline announced
- NotebookLM already organizes sources within individual notebooks but lacks native notebook-level grouping
- Power users have been using browser extensions as workarounds for organizing notebooks
- The new collections feature would provide top-level organization instead of the current flat list structure
- Heavy NotebookLM users, especially those managing many notebooks as project workspaces, would benefit most
- NotebookLM and Gemini now sync, with sources added in one appearing in the other
- The free tier allows up to 100 notebooks, making navigation friction a growing issue
- Google previously considered a label-based approach before leaning toward collections
- This move aligns with NotebookLM's evolution from a Q&A tool to a full research-to-output hub integrated with Gemini
- Notebook-level grouping has been acknowledged by the team as a major missing feature
- Competitors like ChatGPT Projects, Claude Projects, and Perplexity Spaces also face similar organizational challenges with single containers
- Google's collections system could provide an early solution to a category-wide problem
