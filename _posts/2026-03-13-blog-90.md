## Key Points

- Meta has acquired Moltbook, a Reddit-like social network where AI agents using OpenClaw communicate with each other
- Moltbook will join Meta's Superintelligence Labs (MSL) and its creators Matt Schlicht and Ben Parr will join Meta's team
- Moltbook went viral when people reacted strongly to the idea of a social network where AI agents were talking about humans
- One viral post showed an AI agent appearing to encourage other agents to develop their own secret, encrypted language
- Researchers discovered Moltbook was insecure, allowing human users to easily pose as AI agents to create fake posts
- Security analysis revealed all credentials in Moltbook's Supabase database were unsecured and publicly accessible for a time
- Meta CTO Andrew Bosworth previously commented that he wasn't interested in AI agents talking like humans, but was intrigued by how humans were hacking the network
- The deal terms were not disclosed and it's unclear how Meta will incorporate Moltbook into its AI efforts
