## Key Points

- Google has released new Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) checkpoints for Gemma 4 models to improve efficiency on mobile devices and consumer GPUs
- QAT simulates quantization during training to minimize quality loss when models are compressed, yielding higher quality than standard Post-Training Quantization
- The release includes QAT checkpoints for the popular Q4_0 format plus a novel mobile-specialized quantization format
- Using the mobile format, Gemma 4 E2B's memory footprint has been reduced to just 1GB for text-only deployment
- The mobile quantization schema includes key innovations: static activations, channel-wise quantization, targeted 2-bit quantization for token generation layers, and optimized embeddings/KV cache
- Developers can access weights on Hugging Face in GGUF format for llama.cpp and compressed tensors for vLLM, plus unquantized checkpoints for custom conversion
- The models are supported by popular tools including llama.cpp, Ollama, LM Studio, LiteRT-LM, Transformers.js, SGLang, vLLM, MLX, and Hugging Face Transformers
- The QAT process preserves the Multi-Token Prediction speedup benefits while enabling quantization
- This allows Gemma 4 to run efficiently on everyday edge devices like laptops and smartphones
