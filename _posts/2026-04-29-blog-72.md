## Key Points

Video understanding has evolved from simple action recognition to hour-long reasoning and on-device tracking at 16 FPS on phones
Video presents unique challenges: massive token volume (352K tokens per minute), high information sparsity, and intrinsic multimodality with audio
EUPE (Efficient Universal Perception Encoder) distills multiple vision models into <100M parameter backbones, handling classification, dense prediction, and vision-language tasks
Long-form video understanding requires adaptive token compression, with LongVU achieving 60.6% on VideoMME by pruning 45.9% of redundant frames
Efficient tracking solutions like EdgeTAM achieve foundation-model-grade tracking at 16 FPS on iPhone 15 Pro Max
DepthLM shows vision-language models can match dedicated depth specialists while handling multiple reasoning tasks
VideoAuto-R1 introduces 'reason-when-necessary' approach, reducing average response length by 3.3x while maintaining accuracy
Deployment splits into cloud, edge servers, and on-device tiers, with hybrid architectures becoming production defaults
Quantization recipes like W4A16 are standard for video models, with ParetoQ showing 2-bit models can outperform 4-bit models for fixed memory budgets
Open problems include streaming mode understanding, sparse-event detection, cross-camera reasoning, and sub-watt inference for AR glasses
