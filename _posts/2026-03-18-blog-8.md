## Key Points

- ByteDance has paused plans to globally launch its new AI video model Seedance 2.0, according to a report in The Information.
- The Chinese company (TikTok's parent organization) launched Seedance 2.0 in China back in February.
- Brief videos generated by the model went viral, including a clip featuring Tom Cruise fighting Brad Pitt.
- The viral videos drew intense criticism from Hollywood and studios.
- One screenwriter declared the footage meant 'It's likely over for us.'
- Studios sent ByteDance cease-and-desist letters, with Disney's lawyers accusing the company of a 'virtual smash-and-grab of Disney's IP.'
- ByteDance responded by promising to introduce stronger safeguards for intellectual property.
- The company had planned to make Seedance 2.0 available globally in mid-March but is delaying those plans.
- Engineers and lawyers are working to avert further legal issues before proceeding with global launch.
