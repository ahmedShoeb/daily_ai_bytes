## Key Points

Microsoft has leased 30,000 NVIDIA Vera Rubin GPUs at a data center in Narvik, Norway that was originally intended for OpenAI's "Stargate" project
The Narvik facility is located within the Arctic Circle and leverages Norway's abundant hydropower and cool climate for efficient AI computing
The deal with neocloud provider Nscale builds upon Microsoft's prior $6.2 billion, five-year commitment at the same site from late 2025
OpenAI had previously negotiated for the site but didn't reach an agreement, following the company's pause of its "Stargate UK" project due to high energy costs and regulatory uncertainty
The campus is a joint venture between Nscale and Norwegian industrial investment firm Aker, targeting 100,000 NVIDIA GPUs total
Microsoft's expansion aims to provide scalable AI infrastructure for European customers and support Europe's "sovereign AI" ambitions
The facility offers a zero carbon footprint due to Norway's renewable energy supply, addressing sustainability concerns
The Narvik site represents a strategic move for hyperscalers to secure long-term compute capacity in resource-rich regions
