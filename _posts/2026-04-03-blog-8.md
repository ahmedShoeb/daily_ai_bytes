## Key Points

The AI value chain remains inverted, with semiconductor manufacturers capturing ~70% of all AI revenues, compared to ~8% in the traditional cloud stack.
NVIDIA dominates the semiconductor layer with ~80% share and added $175B in incremental revenue over two years, roughly 3x the size of the entire app layer today.
The AI ecosystem grew 5x from ~$90B to ~$435B in annualized revenue over two years, but the profit distribution remains highly skewed toward hardware.
Semiconductors capture 79% of all gross profit dollars in AI, with semi layer gross margins at ~73%, infra at ~55%, and apps at ~33%.
App layer revenue grew fastest in percentage terms (12x) but only added ~$55B vs ~$225B for semis on absolute dollars.
Hyperscalers are spending massive capex on AI infrastructure (~$450B of projected $600B total in 2026), with executives defending the ROI despite 'elements of irrationality'.
Major tech companies are developing custom silicon (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, OpenAI/Broadcom, Microsoft Maia) to reduce dependency on NVIDIA, with Google's TPU being the most mature alternative.
The author predicts the stack will eventually flip as in previous platform shifts, but estimates it may take over a decade rather than the next few years.
The most profitable strategy in AI remains 'selling the shovels' - providing infrastructure rather than building applications.
Application layer ($60B annualized) remains extremely concentrated, with OpenAI and Anthropic together making up ~75% of the layer.
