## Key Points

- The author compares using open LLMs today to using Linux in the past - both came with professional penalties and compatibility issues
- Currently, proprietary models like Claude and GPT top performance leaderboards and have better APIs and compatibility
- Open models face issues with privacy/data sharing concerns when using third-party APIs, or are expensive/complicated/slow when run locally
- The author had mostly used open models as a hobby but stuck with proprietary models for professional work
- Claude's recent ID verification rollout is pushing the author to reconsider using proprietary models
- The author believes the professional penalty for switching to open models will be minimal compared to past technology transitions
- Open models are now very close in performance to leaders, trailing only by a few months
- The author expects a short-term productivity hit but doesn't think it will be a deal-breaker like switching from Matlab to GNU Octave would have been
