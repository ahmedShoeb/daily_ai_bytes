## Key Points

- Modern Transformers are poor at compositional generalization - solving unseen problems by composing familiar concepts - which limits scaling efficiency
- A 'harness' is a program that sits between the environment and the neural network, encoding states into inputs for the LLM
- A good harness makes complex problems 'locally in-distribution' by breaking them down into familiar sub-tasks that individual LM calls can handle
- The Recursive Language Model (RLM) harness uses context offloading and programmatic sub-calls to abstract away domain-specific information
- RLMs trained only on short tasks can generalize to tasks 8-32x longer, with roughly 10x evaluation improvement over training the Transformer directly
- RLMs also show strong domain generalization, performing well on completely different domains than they were trained on
- The architecture enables similar tasks to be viewed as isomorphic, sharing nearly identical token-level trajectories in the root LM's view
- While RLMs have 1.5-3x runtime overhead, this scales better than training Transformers on longer context/horizon tasks
- The research suggests that better returns on scaling AI require compositional generalization, which may need to live in the harness design rather than just the neural architecture
