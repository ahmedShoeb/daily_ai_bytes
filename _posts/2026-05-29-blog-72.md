## Key Points

- Felix Rieseberg, engineering lead for Claude Cowork and Claude Code Desktop at Anthropic, shares how he personally uses Claude AI
- Built a 3D floor planner from 2D house plans by having Claude parse furniture data from years of email receipts and confirmations
- Created a $20 hardware 'Claude buddy' physical AI device
- Developed live artifacts dashboards that auto-update with real-time data from connected services like Spotify, Gmail, and Calendar
- Key philosophy: Go one abstraction layer up when solving problems with AI, then do it again
- Biggest barrier to AI adoption is psychological: people don't realize AI can solve almost any non-creative problem
- Model selection heuristic: Use Sonnet for well-scoped tasks, Opus when you need Claude to interpret what you actually want
- Kids are the best AI users because they're not limited by preconceived notions of what computers can't do
@- When Claude makes mistakes, debug your workflow and prompts rather than blaming the model
- Treat AI like a collaborator who needs better instructions, not a broken tool
