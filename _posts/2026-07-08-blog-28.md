## Key Points

- The article introduces Stewart Brand's Pace Layers framework to understand the AI ecosystem's dizzying pace
- Pace Layers organizes different components by how fast they change, with each layer influencing adjacent layers
- The framework has six layers in a healthy civilization: nature, culture, governance, infrastructure, commerce, and fashion
- Fast layers learn and propose, while slow layers remember and dispose; fast gets attention, slow has power
- Current AI backlash results from massive investment forcing lower layers (like data centers) to move faster than normal
- Data centers are moving from 'decades' to 'years' in development time, causing friction with slower cultural layers
- AI model advancements are now driven by hired and synthetic data since organic human data is largely tapped out
- The speed imbalance causes upper AI layers to race ahead while organizations and universities can't support their pace
- This explains why AI insiders see rapid automation while outsiders question infrastructure needs like data centers
