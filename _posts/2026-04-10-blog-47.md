## Key Points

- Cursor introduced 'warp decode' - a new approach to MoE (Mixture of Experts) model inference that reorganizes parallelism around outputs rather than experts
- This technique achieves 1.84x throughput improvement and produces outputs 1.4x closer to full FP32 reference accuracy
- Traditional MoE inference organizes token generation around experts, which works well for large batches but inefficient for single-token decode
- Warp decode eliminates five 'bookkeeping' stages by making each warp independent and assigned to a single output value
- Each warp streams weight data directly from memory, aggregates totals across all routed experts in registers, avoiding intermediate buffers
- Technique eliminates padding, scatter-and-combine operations, and intermediate memory buffers that traditional path requires
- Warp decode sustains 58% of B200's peak memory bandwidth (3.95 TB/s vs 6.8 TB/s peak)
- Removes intermediate activation quantization step, keeping activations in BF16 and accumulators in FP32 throughout
- Improves Composer research and training pipeline by speeding up inference and improving accuracy
- Not a general replacement for expert-centric execution - still beneficial for prefill and large-batch inference
