## Key Points

- A researcher had Claude AI read and analyze approximately 4,000 AI safety papers published since 2020
- The project started with around 350 known AI safety papers and expanded by collecting papers that referenced at least three of these
- A citation-based approach was used which resulted in blog posts and non-arXiv content being underrepresented
- The researcher notes that the majority of papers appear to be insubstantial, driven by academic publishing incentives rather than direct safety impact
- The database helps find relevant papers, techniques, and datasets for AI safety research projects
- Papers were scored on Novelty, Applicability, and Compute Requirements by Claude, though the reliability of these scores is acknowledged as limited
- The researcher uses a mix of Claude Sonnet (70%) and Opus (30%) for the analysis due to cost considerations
- The project is biased toward more recent papers (2020 onward) due to recency bias in the collection methodology
