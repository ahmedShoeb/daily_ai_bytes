## Key Points

The author shares a journey of trying to post-train the Kimi-K2-Thinking 1 trillion parameter open-source model using existing tools like HuggingFace and LLaMA-Factory.
Despite being an open-source model, there was no readily available training code, and existing solutions either had bugs or were inefficient for large-scale training.
Several major technical hurdles were encountered: compression taking too long, CUDA memory management issues requiring special environment settings, uneven weight distribution across GPUs, quantized weights not working with LoRA training, and MoE (Mixture of Experts) architecture compatibility problems.
The author had to manually fix issues in the codebase including modifying the HuggingFace transformers library to skip unnecessary compression, implementing custom GPU device mapping, patching MoE gate training constraints, and fixing memory management in quantized weight handling.
Even after getting training to work, the solution was 6-9x more expensive per token than using a training API, and experts couldn't be trained due to the quantized architecture.
The article reveals how deep technical debt exists in open-source ML infrastructure, with problems often hiding several layers down the stack.
The author concludes that while open-source models promise broad accessibility, the infrastructure challenges mean it's not as easy as it seems, sometimes requiring building custom solutions rather than patching existing ones.
