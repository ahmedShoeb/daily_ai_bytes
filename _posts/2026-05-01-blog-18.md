## Key Points

- Google has granted the U.S. Department of Defense unrestricted access to its AI for classified networks, allowing all lawful uses
- This deal follows Anthropic's refusal to grant the Pentagon similar terms due to concerns about domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons
- Anthropic wanted guardrails to prevent its AI from being used for domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons, while the Pentagon wanted unrestricted access
- After refusing, the DoD designated Anthropic as a 'supply-chain risk' - a designation normally reserved for foreign adversaries
- Anthropic and the DoD are now in a lawsuit, with a recent injunction granted to Anthropic against the designation
- Google becomes the third AI company (following OpenAI and xAI) to sign deals with the Pentagon after Anthropic's refusal
- Google's agreement includes language about not intending its AI to be used for domestic surveillance or autonomous weapons, similar to OpenAI's contract
- It's unclear whether these provisions are legally binding or enforceable
- 950 Google employees signed an open letter asking the company to follow Anthropic's lead and not sell AI to the DoD without similar guardrails
- Google did not respond to requests for comment about the deal
