## Key Points

- The last two remaining co-founders of Elon Musk's AI startup xAI, Manuel Kroiss and Ross Nordeen, have reportedly left the company
- This follows earlier departures of 9 other co-founders, leaving xAI with no original co-founders
- Kroiss led the company's pretraining team and Nordeen was Musk's 'right-hand operator' who reported directly to him
- Nordeen previously came from Tesla and was involved in planning major layoffs at Twitter after Musk's acquisition
- Musk recently stated that xAI 'was not built right [the] first time around' and is now 'being rebuilt from the foundations up'
- xAI was recently acquired by Musk's SpaceX, bringing SpaceX, xAI, and X (formerly Twitter) together under one corporate umbrella
- The restructuring comes as SpaceX is reportedly planning to go public
- TechCrunch has reached out to xAI for comment on the departures
