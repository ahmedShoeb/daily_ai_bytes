## Key Points


The article discusses the challenge of applying reinforcement learning to domains that aren't easily verifiable, unlike math and code where answers can be objectively checked.

Dario Amodei of Anthropic highlights that tasks like planning missions to Mars, scientific discovery, and writing novels are harder because they lack clear verification mechanisms.

RL with verifiable rewards (RLVR) has driven progress in domains like math and code where answers can be programmatically checked, leading to models achieving IMO gold-medal level performance.

Most valuable work in the real world - like writing memos, design, or building businesses - isn't easily verifiable, creating the fundamental challenge.

The article explores three main techniques for tackling unverifiable domains: rubrics as rewards, generative reward models, and process reward models.

Companies are approaching the problem in three ways: selling verifiers and data to labs, formalizing domains into checkable systems, and owning the full experimental loop for physical validation.

Examples include Mercor, Surge, and Micro1 (selling verifiers), Pramaana Labs (formalizing regulated fields), and Periodic Labs/Lila Sciences (owning experimental loops).

The author argues that the next big leap in AI will come from applying RL techniques to the 'rest of the economy' that's harder to verify than math and code.
