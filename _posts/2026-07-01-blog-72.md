## Key Points

- Google has developed a method to retrofit Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) onto frozen Gemini Nano v3 models for on-device inference acceleration
- MTP addresses the bottleneck of standard autoregressive text generation that processes one token at a time by predicting multiple tokens simultaneously
- Unlike traditional speculative decoding that uses separate draft models, MTP attaches a lightweight Transformer head to existing models without retraining the backbone
- This approach eliminates the need for memory-heavy separate drafting models, saving approximately 130MB of RAM compared to standalone drafters
- The zero-copy architecture leverages the main model's key-value cache, eliminating drafter prefill latency and reducing runtime memory footprint
- On Pixel 9 devices, MTP achieves speedups of 50% or more in production workloads like AI Notification Summaries and Proofread
- The system correctly predicts an average of nearly two additional tokens per inference pass, reducing energy consumption and improving battery life
- MTP maintains bit-for-bit identical output to the main model due to verification steps, ensuring full backward compatibility
- This technology has been rolled out to Pixel 9 and 10 series devices for on-device AI features
- Google continues to explore parallel decoding and techniques for handling language ambiguity to further optimize on-device LLM efficiency
