## Key Points

VOID is a video inpainting model developed by Netflix researchers that removes objects from videos along with all their physical interactions on the scene
It goes beyond simple removal by also eliminating secondary effects like shadows, reflections, and physical interactions (e.g., objects falling when a person is removed)
The system is built on top of CogVideoX and fine-tuned for video inpainting with interaction-aware mask conditioning
VOID uses a two-pass inference process: Pass 1 for base inpainting and Pass 2 for warped-noise refinement to improve temporal consistency
The model requires quadmasks (four-value semantic masks) that distinguish between primary objects to remove, overlap regions, interaction-affected regions, and background
A pipeline called VLM-MASK-REASONER automatically generates these masks using SAM2 segmentation and a Vision-Language Model (Gemini) for reasoning about interactions
Training data is generated from two sources: HUMOTO motion capture dataset with Blender physics simulation and Kubric with Google Scanned Objects
The system requires significant computational resources (GPU with 40GB+ VRAM, like A100) for inference
Researchers provide both a Gradio demo and complete training/inference code, though training requires manual data generation due to licensing constraints
The work was published in a paper titled 'VOID: Video Object and Interaction Deletion' (arXiv:2604.02296)
