## Key Points

- Meta and Broadcom announced a partnership extension through 2029 for designing Meta's in-house AI accelerator chips
- Meta committed to an initial deployment of 1 gigawatt of its Training and Inference Accelerators (MTIA chips)
- The deal could scale to "multiple gigawatts" of chips based on Broadcom technology by 2027 and beyond
- Broadcom CEO Hock Tan informed Meta last week he won't stand for reelection to Meta's board (joined in 2024)
- The MTIA chips will be the first AI silicon to use a 2 nanometer manufacturing process
- Meta is spending heavily on AI with plans to deploy up to $135 billion this year across various chip deals
- The deal comes just two weeks after Broadcom announced a similar agreement with Google for its TPU chips
- Meta's AI strategy includes commitments to 6 gigawatts of AMD GPUs, millions of Nvidia chips, and Arm Holdings custom chips
- Meta is building 31 data centers (27 in the U.S.) to support its AI infrastructure needs
- Meta unveiled four new versions of its in-house MTIA chips in March 2026
