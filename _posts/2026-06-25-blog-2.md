## Key Points

- Anthropic has updated its privacy policy to potentially require users to verify their age and identity by uploading government-issued documents
- The policy change takes effect July 8 and applies to users whose accounts are flagged but not outright banned
- Users may need to upload photos of passports or driver's licenses, plus selfie photos/videos and face geometry templates
- Anthropic says this is part of an improved appeals process for flagged accounts rather than affecting all users
- The company uses San Francisco-based Persona as its identity verification provider
- This move comes amid tensions with the Trump administration over AI tool access and regulatory compliance
- Persona is backed by Trump supporter Peter Thiel's Founders Fund, which has caused controversy for other platforms
- Anthropic says it will determine how long Persona retains user identity documents but didn't specify deletion timelines
