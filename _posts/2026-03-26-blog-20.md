## Key Points

- An AI model (GPT-5.4 Pro) solved a complex Ramsey hypergraph problem that had stumped mathematicians
- The problem involved improving lower bounds on H(n), a sequence related to simultaneous convergence of infinite series
- The solution was first elicited by Kevin Barreto and Liam Price using GPT-5.4 Pro and confirmed by problem contributor Will Brian
- Multiple AI models including Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and GPT-5.4 were eventually able to solve the problem under testing conditions
- The problem was considered moderately difficult, with experts estimating 1-3 months for a human mathematician to solve
- The AI's approach eliminated inefficiencies in previous lower-bound constructions and matched upper-bound constructions
- Brian plans to write up the solution for publication, with Barreto and Price having the option to be coauthors
- The solution involves constructing hypergraphs with specific partition properties to improve known bounds by a constant factor
- Problem author Will Brian noted the AI's approach worked out 'perfectly' and was excited by the solution
- The solution demonstrates AI's capability to tackle complex mathematical problems that require creative construction
