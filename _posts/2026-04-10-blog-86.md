## Key Points

- The paper introduces SandMLE, a framework for training machine learning engineering agents using synthetic environments
- Traditional verification of ML engineering tasks is expensive because it requires running full ML pipelines on large datasets
- While software engineering tasks can be verified with fast-executing unit tests, ML engineering requires data preprocessing, model training, and metric evaluation
- Current approaches use supervised fine-tuning or offline proxy rewards, sacrificing the benefits of on-policy reinforcement learning
- SandMLE generates diverse synthetic ML environments from seed tasks with micro-scale datasets (50-200 training samples)
- This approach reduces execution time by over 13 times, enabling large-scale, on-policy trajectory-wise RL for ML engineering
- The framework achieves significant gains over SFT baselines on MLE-bench-lite with Qwen3 models, with relative medal rate improvements from 20.3% to 66.9%
- The trained policy generalizes across unseen agentic scaffolds, achieving up to 32.4% better HumanRank score on MLE-Dojo
- The paper addresses the bottleneck in training ML engineering agents by focusing on sandbox data size as the primary constraint
- Submitted on April 6, 2026, this research represents an advance in ML agent training methodologies
