## Key Points

- China's National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) has blocked Meta's $2 billion acquisition of AI startup Manus
- The NDRC ordered both parties to unwind the deal entirely without providing any explanation
- Manus was founded in 2022 by Chinese engineers and relocated its headquarters from China to Singapore in mid-2025
- Meta announced the acquisition in December 2025 for $2-3 billion, planning to integrate Manus' agent technology into Meta AI
- Approximately 100 Manus employees had already moved into Meta's Singapore offices as of March
- Manus CEO Xiao Hong and Chief Scientist Yichao Ji are reportedly under exit bans preventing them from leaving mainland China
- The company's origins trace back to China, where founders established parent company Butterfly Effect in Beijing in 2022 before moving to Singapore
- Senator John Cornyn raised concerns about American capital flowing to a Chinese-linked firm
- Meta stated the transaction complied fully with applicable law and anticipates an appropriate resolution
