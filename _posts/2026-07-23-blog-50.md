## Key Points

- NVIDIA has released Cosmos 3 Edge, a 4-billion-parameter open world model designed for edge AI systems
- The model enables robots and vision AI agents to understand surroundings, reason in real-time, and generate actions directly on edge devices
- It's optimized for NVIDIA edge computers including RTX PRO GPUs, DGX systems, GeForce RTX GPUs, and Jetson modules including new T2000/T3000
- Cosmos 3 Edge operates at robot-control resolution (640×360) and can generate 32 actions per inference while achieving real-time control at 15 Hz on Jetson Thor
- The model ranks #1 among similar-sized (4B parameter) models on VANTAGE-Bench for vision analytics and sets state-of-the-art for robot policy learning
- Cosmos 3 Edge features two transformer towers: an autoregressive tower for reasoning and a diffusion tower for prediction/generation
- The model uses a common action representation that maps different robot embodiments (vehicles, cameras, robot arms) into compact geometric vectors
- It includes a policy mode that predicts both actions and their expected visual consequences, connecting world modeling directly to robot policy training
- NVIDIA is also releasing Cosmos 3 Edge Policy (DROID) specifically trained for pick-and-place tasks with accompanying post-training scripts
- The release includes reference post-trained checkpoints and training recipes for developers to customize the model for their applications
