## Key Points

- Anthropic is in talks with Samsung about developing a custom AI chip, according to a report from The Information
- This follows reports from April that Anthropic was considering producing its own AI chips to address chip shortages
- The company hasn't yet decided on the chip's specific use, how it will fit into servers, or its power specifications
- Anthropic maintains that a diversified hardware strategy using chips from Google, Amazon, and Nvidia remains central to its compute approach
- Many AI companies are pursuing custom chips to create specialized hardware and reduce dependence on Nvidia
- OpenAI recently announced its own custom inference processor called 'Jalapeño' in partnership with Broadcom
- Samsung is already a major Nvidia partner and produces chips needed for AI training and model running
- Samsung and Nvidia are collaborating on an AI chip factory in South Korea, and Samsung has also discussed chip-making partnerships with Google
