## Key Points

- NVIDIA has introduced the NVIDIA Agent Toolkit, an open modular foundation for building specialized AI agents
- The toolkit comprises three main components: NVIDIA Nemotron open models, NVIDIA NemoClaw blueprints for safer agent behavior, and NVIDIA OpenShell runtime for secure operations
- Companies are moving from experimental AI to specialized agents that can reason, use tools, and take action in complex workflows
- The toolkit enables digital AI coworkers that enterprises can customize, specialize, control, and trust
- NVIDIA technologies accelerate turning frontier models into fully functional digital coworkers that work with third-party agent harnesses
- Specialized agents are already being deployed in life sciences for drug discovery, in cybersecurity for vulnerability investigations, and in operations for supply chain coordination
- Examples include Cadence and Synopsys building agents for chip design, CrowdStrike running security agents with 98.5% accuracy, and healthcare applications in clinical documentation and surgical assistance
- The NVIDIA BioNeMo Toolkit enables life sciences research that previously took months to be completed in days
- The approach allows agents to combine models, tools, skills, runtime, and infrastructure in ways companies can adapt to their own workflows
