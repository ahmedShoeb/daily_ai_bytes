## Key Points

- Gemma 4 is a new generation of open-weight, natively multimodal language models with dense and Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architectures ranging from 2.3B to 31B parameters.
- The models feature a thinking mode that generates reasoning traces before responses, improving performance on math, coding, and other complex tasks.
- Gemma 4 includes a unified encoder-free architecture for the 12B model, which processes raw audio and image patches directly without separate encoders.
- The model family demonstrates improved efficiency through better local-to-global attention ratios, KV cache sharing, and reduced memory footprint.
- Gemma 4 31B ranks as the top open dense model in Arena human evaluations, performing comparably to much larger open models while being significantly smaller.
- The models show major performance improvements over Gemma 3 across STEM, multimodal, and long-context benchmarks, with the E2B model matching Gemma 3 27B performance with 10x fewer parameters.
- Training infrastructure uses TPUv5p and TPUv6e with advanced scaling techniques like Slice-Granularity Elasticity to handle failures efficiently.
- The models undergo rigorous safety evaluations and filtering, with significant improvements in safety over previous Gemma versions while minimizing unjustified refusals.
- Quantization-aware training provides efficient mobile quantization (mix of int2 and int4) and Q4_0 quantization options for different deployment scenarios.
- Audio and vision encoders are significantly optimized, with the audio encoder reduced by 55% and encoder-free architecture eliminating separate vision/audio encoders for the 12B model.
