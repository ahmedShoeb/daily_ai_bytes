## Key Points

AI represents a fundamental shift where the 'how' (execution) is fully outsourced to technology, similar to a genie granting wishes
The primary challenge now becomes knowing 'what' to ask for or create, rather than 'how' to do it
This inversion makes previously 'invisible' skills like taste, judgment, agency, curiosity, and imagination critically important
Most people haven't developed these 'what' skills because they were never necessary when execution was the main bottleneck
Different 'what' skills aren't interchangeable - someone can have great taste but no agency, or strong agency with poor judgment
AI accelerates execution so dramatically that organizational differentiation will shift from speed to knowing what to build
Designers are positioned as experts in understanding outcomes people truly need and identifying the core problems to solve
AI is ultimately just a powerful implementation tool - the real value lies in what humans choose to create with it
