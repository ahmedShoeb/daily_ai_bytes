## Key Points

- Anthropic and Amazon have signed a new agreement to deepen their partnership and secure up to 5 gigawatts (GW) of capacity for training and deploying Claude
- Anthropic is committing more than $100 billion over the next ten years to AWS technologies for this expansion
- The collaboration includes using Trainium2, Trainium3, and future Trainium4 chips, with significant Trainium2 capacity coming online in Q2 2025
- Amazon is investing $5 billion in Anthropic today, with potential for up to an additional $20 billion, building on previous $8 billion investments
- The full Claude Platform will soon be available directly within AWS, integrating with existing accounts, controls, and billing
- Anthropic's run-rate revenue has surpassed $30 billion, up from approximately $9 billion at the end of 2025
- Over 100,000 customers now run Claude on Amazon Bedrock, and Anthropic currently uses over one million Trainium2 chips
- The partnership will help address infrastructure strain from rapid growth, with nearly 1GW of total capacity expected by the end of 2026
- The agreement includes expansion of inference capabilities in Asia and Europe to serve Claude's growing international customer base
- Claude remains the only frontier AI model available on all three major cloud platforms: AWS, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure
