## Key Points

- The post presents an informal model called "belief webs" that unifies beliefs, goals, and actions as facets of a single phenomenon
- Belief webs suggest that agents' beliefs are locally consistent but not necessarily globally consistent, unlike frameworks that require single probability distributions
- Actions are conceptualized as self-fulfilling beliefs under a "self-predictive model" where holding a belief can make it come true via external actuators
- Goals are framed as beliefs that have "drives" (forces pulling credences upward) rather than fixed artificial credences
- The model draws from active inference, agent foundations, and machine learning, connecting ideas from frameworks like probabilistic dependency graphs and Garrabrant induction
- The self-predictive model helps explain psychological phenomena like internal conflict, procrastination, and identity formation
- Belief webs aim to treat individual agents as emergent phenomena from densely-connected regions of a larger belief web
- The framework attempts to handle hierarchical concept formation and internal inconsistency while avoiding problems like the 5-and-10 problem
