## Key Points

Google is preparing a credit-based system for the Gemini app where users get a monthly allowance to spend across models and features
This system will replace current fixed prompt quotas and time-bound caps tied to subscription tiers
The credit mechanics would bring Google closer to flexible consumption models used by OpenAI, Anthropic, and Notion
Power users would benefit from more predictable budgeting for heavy workloads like agentic tasks, Deep Research, and multimodal sessions
A new dedicated images section labeled 'NEW' has appeared in the web UI, suggesting potential image generation updates or integration
Google may be developing a proper in-app image editor within Gemini with canvas-style tools, reviving previous Labs projects
This fits Google's broader consolidation strategy, folding developer perks into AI Pro and Ultra, and unifying billing systems
The timing suggests these features could be unveiled at Google I/O on May 19-20, alongside other announcements like Stitch redesign and Skills rollout
