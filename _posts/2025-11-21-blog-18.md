## Key Points

Soumith Chintala, the influential creator of PyTorch and former Meta AI leader, has joined Mira Murati's startup, Thinking Machines Lab.
Chintala's departure from Meta followed an 11-year tenure and coincides with significant restructuring within Meta's AI divisions.
Thinking Machines Lab, founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, is actively recruiting top AI talent from major companies and academia.
The startup has already secured substantial funding, including a $2 billion seed round, and is reportedly seeking further investment at a $50 billion valuation.
Thinking Machines Lab focuses on "human-AI collaboration" and has launched its first tool, Tinker, for fine-tuning large language models.

## Key Topics Discussed

Hey everyone, let's dive into some fascinating news in the AI world! Soumith Chintala, a really influential figure who created PyTorch and was an AI leader at Meta, has made a big move. He's now joined Thinking Machines Lab, the fast-growing startup founded by Mira Murati, formerly the CTO of OpenAI. Chintala even updated his social media to confirm his new role, expressing excitement about the incredible people he's working with. This move comes after Chintala's remarkable 11-year run at Meta, where he helped build PyTorch into an open-source AI framework that's widely used across tech and academia. His departure is set against the backdrop of significant changes within Meta's AI ranks, as the company has been reorganizing its AI efforts and bringing in new talent. Thinking Machines Lab, which Murati unveiled in February, is making serious waves. The startup positions itself as an AI research and product lab, dedicated to "human-AI collaboration." They've been on an aggressive hiring spree, snatching up top-tier talent from places like Meta, OpenAI, and Anthropic, offering salaries as high as $500,000 for technical roles. Financially, Thinking Machines Lab is also making headlines. They raised a massive $2 billion seed round earlier this year at a $10 billion valuation, and according to Bloomberg, they're now in talks to raise even more funding at an astounding $50 billion valuation. This shows just how intensely competitive the AI talent and funding wars are right now. Chintala had previously indicated he was ready for "something small… something new… something uncomfortable" after his long and impactful career at Meta. He believed PyTorch was stable enough to thrive without him. While Thinking Machines hasn't rolled out its full suite of products yet, their first tool, Tinker, which helps users fine-tune large language models, is already being utilized by researchers at prestigious universities like Princeton and Stanford, along with early business customers. It's an exciting time to see what innovations will come from this new collaboration!
