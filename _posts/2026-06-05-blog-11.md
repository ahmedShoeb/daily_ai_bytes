## Key Points

- The fundamental debate between open and closed AI models centers on economics: whether users will continue paying large premiums for top closed models
- Early 2026 is pivotal for the AI industry, as coding agents demonstrate where users will pay substantial premiums for better intelligence
- Closed labs (like OpenAI, Anthropic, and potentially Google) will likely dominate the high-end market by creating the most efficient models for intelligence at given costs
- These labs have massive returns on integration that open models can't match due to their diverse serving requirements
- The author believes OpenAI and Anthropic could reach $2-10T valuations in 5-10 years, forming an oligopoly similar to today's cloud market
- While closed models will dominate premium applications, the open model economy will capture more collective value overall
- Open models will achieve commodity pricing through multiple coordinated layers, making them accessible for enterprise in-house tools
- Different use cases will emerge: closed models for premium knowledge work, open models for commodity tasks and specific niches
- Intelligent model routing will become critical - using frontier models for complex logic while routing routine tasks to open ecosystems
- Both ecosystems are on different growth trajectories: closed models monetize top-end knowledge work immediately, while open models enable broader AI diffusion
