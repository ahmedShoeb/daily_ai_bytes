## Key Points

- Perplexity Computer shifts AI from conversational assistants to autonomous agents that plan and execute complete tasks rather than just provide information
- Computer performs 48× more machine work per session than Search, with average execution times of 26 minutes vs 33 seconds
- Computer reduces task time by 87% and task cost by 94% compared to traditional Search + human execution workflows
- Users shift from manual execution to supervision, enabling work at higher levels of abstraction and across disciplinary boundaries
- 59% of Computer usage involves tasks outside users' primary occupations, compared to 50% for Search
- Computer tasks are more cognitively complex: 76% require higher-order cognition vs 55% for Search, with 50% being Create-level tasks vs 26%
- Computer users engage with 38% more knowledge domains and 40% more work activities per task compared to Search users
- The study found 55% reduction in user dissatisfaction with Computer compared to Search
- Programming tasks show the most dramatic improvements: 92% time reduction and 96% cost reduction
- Early adopters show 84× growth in Computer queries compared to conventional Search usage patterns
