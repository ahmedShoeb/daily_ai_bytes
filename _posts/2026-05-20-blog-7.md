## Key Points

- A M5 MacBook Pro running local LLM inference at 50-100 watts costs around $0.02 per hour in electricity at $0.20 per kWh
- Hardware costs dominate local inference - a $4,299 M5 Max MacBook Pro depreciates at $0.05-$0.16 per hour depending on estimated lifespan
- Local inference speeds range from 10-40 tokens per second with models like Gemma 2:31b, costing $0.40-$4.79 per million tokens depending on configuration
- OpenRouter's cloud inference for comparable models costs 1/3 to 2x less at $0.38-$0.50 per million tokens
- Local inference is typically slower than cloud, with OpenRouter achieving 60-70 tokens per second vs local's 10-20 tokens per second
- For human employees, their salary costs are ~1000x higher than token generation costs, making cloud services like Anthropic more cost-effective in business contexts
- Apple Silicon's biggest advantage is enabling consumer devices to run models approaching Anthropic Sonnet's performance level
