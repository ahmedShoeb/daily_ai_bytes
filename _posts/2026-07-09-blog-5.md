## Key Points

- PACE is a framework that predicts expensive agentic LLM benchmark performance using a small subset of atomic evaluation instances, dramatically reducing costs
- Evaluating LLM agents on benchmarks like SWE-Bench and GAIA can cost thousands of dollars and take days, while PACE reduces this to a fraction of the cost
- The framework constructs proxy benchmarks by selecting instances from existing non-agentic evaluations that reliably predict performance on agentic benchmarks
- PACE uses two complementary instance-selection strategies: target-relevance local selection and globally informative global selection
- Experiments across 14 models, 4 agentic benchmarks, and 19 non-agentic benchmarks show impressive results
- PACE-Bench achieves leave-one-out cross-validation mean absolute error under 4% and Spearman correlation above 0.80
- The framework enables reliable estimation of agentic performance during model development, selection, and routing without full agent evaluation overhead
- Analysis of selected proxy instances reveals which skills each agentic benchmark uniquely demands
- This approach reduces agentic evaluation costs to less than 1% of the full benchmark cost
- PACE provides practical value for practitioners who need to estimate agentic performance efficiently during development cycles
