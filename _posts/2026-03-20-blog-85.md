## Key Points

OpenAI is gearing up for a potential IPO by the end of 2026, focusing heavily on its enterprise business ahead of going public
CEO of Applications Fidji Simo told employees OpenAI is 'orienting aggressively' towards high-productivity use cases to turn ChatGPT into a productivity tool
ChatGPT now supports more than 900 million weekly active users, but OpenAI aims to convert these into 'high-compute users' for enterprise applications
OpenAI's IPO could happen as early as the fourth quarter of 2026, though timing is still subject to change
CFO Sarah Friar is building out the finance team, hiring former Block and DocuSign executives to prepare for market debut
OpenAI declared a 'code red' effort in December 2025 to improve ChatGPT against competition from Google and Anthropic
The company revised its compute spending target from $1.4 trillion to roughly $600 billion by 2030, more closely tied to expected revenue growth
OpenAI projects total revenue of more than $280 billion by 2030, with nearly equal contributions from consumer and enterprise businesses
