## Key Points

- Google has released Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) drafters for the Gemma 4 family, providing up to 3x speedup without quality degradation
- The MTP drafters use speculative decoding architecture where a lightweight drafter model predicts multiple tokens ahead, then the main Gemma 4 model verifies them in parallel
- This addresses the memory-bandwidth bottleneck in standard LLM inference where processors spend most time moving parameters from VRAM
- Developers can achieve improved responsiveness for real-time applications, faster local development on personal computers, enhanced on-device performance for edge devices, and preserved battery life
- The system maintains identical reasoning and accuracy since the primary Gemma 4 model retains final verification
- MTP drafters share the target model's KV cache and activations, avoiding recalculation of already-processed context
- For edge models (E2B and E4B), Google implemented efficient clustering techniques in the embedder to accelerate generation
- Performance optimizations show ~2.2x speedup locally on Apple Silicon with batch processing and similar gains on Nvidia A100
- The MTP drafters are available today under Apache 2.0 license on Hugging Face, Kaggle, and Google AI Edge Gallery
- The technology supports various frameworks including transformers, MLX, vLLM, SGLang, and Ollama
