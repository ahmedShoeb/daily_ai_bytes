## Key Points

- There's a major wealth divide emerging in the AI boom that's creating bad vibes even in the tech industry
- Menlo Ventures partner Deedy Das describes San Francisco as particularly frenetic right now with the 'worst divide in outcomes' he's ever seen
- Das estimates around 10,000 people (founders and employees at companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Nvidia) have achieved retirement wealth above $20M
- Meanwhile, many others worry they can work their well-paying jobs (under $500k) their whole lives and never reach such wealth
- Layoffs are currently in full swing
- Many software engineers feel their lifelong skills are no longer useful, causing confusion about career paths
- This situation has created deep malaise about work and its future
- The dynamic has sparked debate on social media, with some arguing those complaining are still incredibly fortunate
- Another perspective notes the irony that AI is both the path to wealth and potentially eliminating backup career options
