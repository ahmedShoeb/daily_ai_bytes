## Key Points

- xAI (now part of SpaceX) has formed significant rental partnerships with Anthropic and Google, providing massive GPU capacity to these companies
- Anthropic was struggling with serious capacity shortages, forcing them to implement peak-hour usage restrictions before xAI's intervention
- xAI's deal with Anthropic involves $1.25 billion/month for 300MW capacity (approximately 220k GPUs) from their Colossus 1 datacenter in Memphis
- Google's partnership involves $920 million/month for 110k GPUs, with 90-day cancellation clauses available after initial lock-in periods
- These rental deals are enormously profitable for xAI, potentially allowing them to recoup all capital expenditure within 18 months
- xAI/SpaceX has a competitive advantage in rapid datacenter construction, building Colossus 1 in just 122 days compared to competitors' multi-year timelines
- The partnerships put Grok (xAI's own AI model) in an odd position, with capacity originally intended for Grok now being leased to competitors
- The article suggests xAI is evolving to resemble more of a datacenter REIT with an AI lab attached rather than primarily a frontier AI research company
- There's speculation that financial engineering for SpaceX's upcoming IPO and pressure on OpenAI might also be factors behind these deals
