## Key Points

- Researchers performed a transparency audit of DiffusionGemma, Google's new text diffusion model, finding it's not significantly less transparent than the autoregressive Gemma model
- The study distinguishes between 'variable transparency' (understanding model computation snapshots) and 'algorithmic transparency' (reconstructing the decision-making process)
- Text diffusion models like DiffusionGemma have lower algorithmic transparency by default because they generate all tokens at once, unlike autoregressive models that reason token-by-token
- Researchers observed unique phenomena in text diffusion models including non-chronological reasoning, token and sequence smearing, and intermediate-context reasoning
- One key phenomenon is 'retroactive self-correction' where the model can correct earlier mistakes in subsequent denoising steps
- Another is 'token smearing' where the model maintains probability distributions over adjacent positions when uncertain about token placement
- The opaque serial depth for DiffusionGemma is 28.6x larger than Gemma, but could drop to 1.1x if intermediates were fully interpretable
- Monitorability (a key safety application of transparency) is similar between Gemma and DiffusionGemma
- The work sets a precedent for transparency audits of new model architectures, which is important for AI safety as more reasoning may occur in latent spaces
- The paper includes 24 open problems for the community to investigate further
