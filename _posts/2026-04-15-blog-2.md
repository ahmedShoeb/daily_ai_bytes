## Key Points

- Missions is Factory's system for breaking large AI projects into focused units handled by fresh agents with narrow goals, shared state, and explicit validation
- The architecture addresses agent context issues: as agents see more information, they become less focused and reliable due to accumulating irrelevant or adversarial context
- Key design principles include separation of concerns, test-driven development at two levels (worker and mission), and externalized state management
- The system uses an orchestrator to plan work, workers to implement features, and validators to independently review work for correctness
- Different AI models are specialized for different roles based on their strengths: planning for orchestrators, execution for workers, skepticism for validators
- A mission workflow starts with a user request, creates a validation contract, decomposes work into features, and runs implementation-validation loops until milestones pass
- Example mission: built a Slack clone with 38.8k lines of code (52.5% tests) and 89.25% statement coverage across six milestones
- Validation accounted for 37.2% of total runtime, with fix features making up 34.4% of implementation work
- The system uses fresh agents for validation to avoid self-evaluation bias and ensure objective review of completed work
- The architecture is designed to improve as AI models get better at reasoning, planning, execution, and validation
