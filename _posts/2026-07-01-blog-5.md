## Key Points

- 87% of digital workers use AI at work, saving an average of 11 hours per week
- Only 13% say their organization is performing significantly better despite AI adoption
- Workers spend 6.4 hours weekly 'botsitting' (feeding AI context, checking outputs, fixing mistakes) - more than time spent using AI to produce work
- 69% of AI users admit to 'botshitting' - shipping work they haven't verified, don't fully understand, or can't confidently stand behind
- The hidden work includes cleaning up confident-but-wrong AI outputs
- High AI achievers protect their judgment, learn from tools, and know when not to use AI
- Organizations need better context, meaningful metrics, practical governance, and redesigned workflows
- The report features insights from 100+ leaders across companies like Time, Workday, Deloitte, Zendesk, Oracle, Uber, and Instacart
- AI doesn't fix broken systems - it amplifies them
- Nine themes are reshaping work, including Division of Labor and Leadership
