## Key Points

Anthropic's latest ad campaign titled "There's hope in hard questions" is unsettling viewers with disturbing imagery and a doomsday tone
The ad shows a burning house, facial recognition surveillance of crowds, homeless people, cemetery tombstones, and mining laborers
Voiceovers feature people asking questions like "Can AI be trusted?" and "Who's gonna hit the brakes if we need to?"
The marketing follows Anthropic's pattern of positioning itself as an ethical alternative to other AI companies
OpenAI CEO Sam Altman criticized the ad, thinking it was satire and looking for parody handles like "c1audeai"
Tech industry critics called it the "worst corporate communications ever" and evidence of effective altruists living in "AI psychosis"
The inclusion of Arlington National Cemetery imagery drew particular criticism for being insensitive and disturbing
This marks a contrast from Anthropic's successful Super Bowl ads in February that humorously targeted OpenAI's ChatGPT ads
The ad backfired despite following a common marketing strategy where companies acknowledge industry harms to position themselves as solutions
Viewers described the imagery as "sinister" and compared it to 1970s conspiracy thriller "The Parallax View"
