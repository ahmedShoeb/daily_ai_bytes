## Key Points

-Hey everyone, today we're diving into autoresearch - a fascinating concept where agents build 'outer loops' to maintain and improve their own systems using feedback signals, evaluations, and human input
-Roland Gavrilescu, co-founder of Introspection, explains how we've moved from focusing on models to harnesses, and now to loops - where the loop itself becomes the product
-They're proposing a concept called 'agent recipes' - portable formats that capture human expertise, evals, judges, and signal processing so agents can iterate on them like a research lab
-There's an important distinction between inner loops (primary system working with users) and outer loops (systems that study and maintain the primary system through autoresearch)
-Human involvement remains crucial - agents can be trained to ask questions like an 'ask a human' tool, gradually learning preferences and becoming more autonomous over time
-Introspection focuses on vertical agents, especially in SaaS companies who want secure, provider-agnostic infrastructure that doesn't lock them into OpenAI or Anthropic
-Everything is Git-based, with Git serving as the audit log, making the environment agent-friendly for software engineers in vertical SaaS companies
-The approach is to design humans as core components of the factory, extracting tacit knowledge gradually rather than trying to automate everything immediately
-For engineers starting with autoresearch, the key is to invest in signals, control costs, and follow research patterns used by AI labs
-The ultimate goal is turning your product organization into a miniature research lab with agents acting as miniature researchers
