## Key Points

- OpenAI disclosed substantial risks in investor documents ahead of potential IPO, highlighting heavy reliance on Microsoft for financing and compute resources
- Microsoft has invested $13 billion in OpenAI and holds a 27% diluted stake worth $135 billion in the for-profit part of the organization
- The company warned that if Microsoft modifies or terminates their partnership, it could adversely affect OpenAI's business, though a spokesperson called this 'standard legal risk factor disclosure'
- OpenAI faces $665 billion in estimated compute spend commitments through 2030 with partners including Microsoft, Nvidia, AMD, and Broadcom
- The company expressed concerns about potential supply chain disruptions if chip supplier TSMC is affected by China-Taiwan tensions
- OpenAI is involved in multiple lawsuits, including three from co-founder Elon Musk's xAI (now part of SpaceX), with the first case expected to go to trial next month
- At least 14 lawsuits have been filed by ChatGPT users or family members blaming the company's products for mental illness leading to suicide or injury, including a wrongful death case from parents of a 16-year-old
- OpenAI had $13.1 billion in 2025 revenue, was valued at $730 billion last month, and recently announced $110 billion in funding from partners including Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank
- Despite Sam Altman's controversies (including 2023 ouster and reinstatement), the risk factors document doesn't specifically name him as a risk
- The disclosures offer a preview of what to expect in OpenAI's upcoming IPO filing as the company prepares for a potential public market debut this year
