## Key Points

ByteDance unveiled Seedance 2.5, its latest AI video generation model, during a Beijing conference
The new model can accept up to 50 reference pieces (images, videos, or audio files), a significant increase from 12 in Seedance 2.0
Seedance 2.5 can generate 30-second, 4K videos with a single prompt
ByteDance is set to release the model in China next month, with no timeline mentioned for other countries
The company's AI video models rival those of OpenAI's Sora and Google's Veo 3
Seedance 2.0's US rollout was delayed earlier this year due to Hollywood pressure over copyright concerns
The new model faces potential similar backlash if it can't address legal and copyright issues
Increasing reference inputs gives users greater control over the video creation process
