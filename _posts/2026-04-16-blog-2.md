## Key Points

- AI2 researchers developed two benchmarks to test AI science agents: ScienceWorld (released 2022) and DiscoveryWorld (released 2024)
- ScienceWorld tests if agents can carry out elementary-school level science experiments in a virtual environment
- DiscoveryWorld evaluates if agents can design and execute end-to-end scientific investigations from scratch across 8 topics at college/PhD level
- When ScienceWorld launched in 2022, top AI models scored below 10% despite excelling on multiple-choice exams
- By early 2025, leading models scored in the low 80s on ScienceWorld but still can't fully solve 4th-grade science curriculum
- On DiscoveryWorld, best systems complete only ~20% of tasks at higher difficulty, while human scientists solve ~70%
- DiscoveryWorld contains 120 challenge tasks across different difficulty levels with parametric variations to prevent memorization
- ScienceWorld includes 30 task types across categories like changing states of matter and genetics, with hundreds of randomized configurations
- Both benchmarks show that 'knowing what a concept is' differs from 'being able to apply it' in scientific contexts
- The benchmarks are open and freely available to help turn promising ideas into proven results in AI scientific discovery
