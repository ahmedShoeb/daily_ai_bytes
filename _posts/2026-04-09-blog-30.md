## Key Points

- Private equity and venture capital investors are shifting their focus from "What is your AI strategy?" to "How far along are you in rebuilding the company around AI?"
- Companies must treat AI as an operating system layer across all business functions (product development, service delivery, sales/marketing, customer success, finance/operations)
- AI should not just be added as features or experiments - workflows need to be redesigned from the start with AI built into processes
- Most companies are still treating AI as a feature layer (adding copilots, automating tasks) rather than as foundational infrastructure
- Companies' core operating logic (vision, strategy, priorities) needs to be embedded into the AI layer for daily interaction and alignment
- Investors now look for evidence that operating models are changing: faster shipping, better service leverage, improved team linking, AI-human orchestration
- Companies that rebuild around AI will outperform those that bolt AI onto old operating models
- This is becoming a funding imperative - slow adopters will face competitive disadvantages
- AI as an operating layer makes companies more scalable, durable, and investable
