## Key Points

- Anthropic published a study showing Claude can match or beat specialized chemistry software in NMR analysis without any chemistry-specific training
- The key insight is that the bottleneck in AI for science has shifted from model capability to workflow and operationalization
- Claude (Opus 4.7) performed forward prediction of NMR spectra more accurately on hydrogen and competitively on carbon compared to ChemDraw and MestReNova
- Claude successfully solved inverse problems (determining structure from spectra and formula alone) for simpler targets and could solve harder ones with hints
- This pattern is appearing across multiple scientific domains including biology, suggesting it's a general trend, not just chemistry-specific
- The main challenge now is turning AI answers into reproducible research results, which requires integration with real data, code execution, and verification
- Real-world AI science applications need systems that can access databases, run code, verify answers against evidence, and produce auditable outputs
- Better base models actually raise the value of workflow layers rather than making them redundant
- K-Dense advocates for collaboration between model developers and workflow builders to operationalize AI for scientific research
