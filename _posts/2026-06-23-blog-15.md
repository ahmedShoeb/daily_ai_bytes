## Key Points

- OpenAI has confidentially filed for an IPO with the SEC, following similar moves by Anthropic and ahead of SpaceX's upcoming public offering
- The company is valued at over $850 billion and is preparing for a possible fourth-quarter 2026 IPO
- OpenAI plans a tender offer allowing employees to sell shares at the latest $852 billion valuation to address liquidity needs
- The company has been working with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley on the filing process
- OpenAI faces stiff competition from rivals like Anthropic (valued at $965 billion), Google, and SpaceX's merged xAI entity
- CEO Sam Altman introduced "the third phase of OpenAI" focused on making advanced AI abundant, affordable, and widely accessible
- The company is emphasizing focus by shuttering fringe projects like Sora and investing in enterprise business and coding assistant Codex
- OpenAI's IPO follows a recent court battle where Elon Musk's claims against the company were dismissed on procedural grounds
- The company has raised over $180 billion in funding but continues to burn cash for AI infrastructure and compute resources
- ChatGPT now supports more than 900 million weekly active users, making OpenAI one of the most valuable private companies worldwide
