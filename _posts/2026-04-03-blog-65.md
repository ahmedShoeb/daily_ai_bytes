## Key Points

Mercor, an AI recruiting startup, confirmed a security incident linked to a supply chain attack involving the open source project LiteLLM
The company stated it was 'one of thousands of companies' affected by a recent compromise of LiteLLM's project, linked to hacking group TeamPCP
Extortion hacking group Lapsus$ claimed responsibility for targeting Mercor and gaining access to its data
Founded in 2023, Mercor works with companies including OpenAI and Anthropic to train AI models by contracting specialized domain experts
The startup is valued at $10 billion following a $350 million Series C round led by Felicis Ventures in October 2025
Mercor spokesperson confirmed the company moved promptly to contain and remediate the security incident and is conducting a thorough investigation with third-party forensics experts
Lapsus$ shared a sample of allegedly stolen data including Slack data, ticketing data, and videos showing conversations between Mercor's AI systems and contractors
The LiteLLM compromise involved malicious code discovered in a package associated with the Y Combinator-backed startup's open source project
LiteLLM's library is downloaded millions of times per day and the incident prompted the company to change compliance processes
It remains unclear how many companies were affected or whether any data exposure occurred as investigations continue
