## Key Points

- AI inference is extremely expensive - a single prompt to a frontier model can cost $2, making AI a million times more expensive than regular HTTP requests
- Inference theft involves attackers using stolen tokens to run AI calls for free and resell them at a discount, creating a high-margin business
- AI playgrounds and endpoints with maximum prompt control are the most vulnerable targets for inference theft attacks
- Traditional web defenses like IP rate limits and auth walls fail against inference theft because attackers can use residential proxies and throwaway accounts at scale
- Sophisticated attackers wrap custom AI endpoints in OpenAI/Anthropic-compatible adapters to resell stolen inference
- Vercel experienced a real attack on April 12, 2026, where traffic spiked to 10x normal volume with 1,300 requests/minute, costing over $10,000/day
- Protection must run on every AI request, not just at session start, to prevent attackers from amortizing bypass costs across thousands of calls
- Vercel uses BotID with deep analysis for invisible CAPTCHA protection that can verify every request without user friction
- The cost asymmetry works in defenders' favor: verification is cheap per call while inference is expensive to steal
- Developers should audit AI endpoints, prioritize protection by attack likelihood, and implement per-request gating with tools like BotID
