## Key Points

- Base44, a vibe-coding platform acquired by Wix for $80 million a year ago, is launching its own AI model called Base1
- The move addresses questions about defensibility for AI startups built on top of external models and whether frontier models suit all use cases
- Base44's founder says owning their own model allows for optimization on latency, cost, and efficiency
- The model was trained on tens of millions of real user interactions from the platform
- VC expert Jonathan Userovici identifies data, distribution, and tech stack as three key ingredients for AI startup defensibility
- Competition may come not just from other vibe-coding startups like Lovable, but from frontier AI labs like Anthropic that are moving into coding
- Cost pressure is driving enterprises to demand optimization for different use cases rather than always using the latest frontier models
- Base44 has grown rapidly, reaching $150M in annual recurring revenue in May after crossing $100M in March
- Despite Lovable having higher ARR ($500M), Base44 bets vertical integration (owning distribution, data, and infrastructure) will create competitive advantage
