## Key Points

Google Chrome is entering the final phase of deprecating Manifest V2 (MV2) extensions
Chrome has removed the kExtensionManifestV2Disabled feature flag, making MV2 extensions including uBlock Origin unavailable in supported versions
All bypass methods and tricks that kept MV2 extensions like uBlock Origin working will cease to function, especially after Chromium version 151
Google cites technical difficulties, implementation complexities, and security risks as reasons for completely removing MV2 support
Microsoft Edge and Opera are expected to follow Chrome's lead, though Opera initially claimed it would maintain MV2 support longer
Brave and Vivaldi appear to be the only Chromium browsers currently committed to continuing MV2 support
Firefox remains an alternative that supports both MV2 and MV3 extensions
Users are encouraged to switch to uBlock Origin Lite (MV3-based) for Chrome, though it's reportedly not as effective as the original
Opera clarified it still intends to support MV2 extensions for now but acknowledges eventual transition to MV3 is inevitable
MV3 extensions in general are perceived as less effective for ad-blocking capabilities compared to MV2 versions
