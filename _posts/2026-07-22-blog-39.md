## Key Points

- Moonshot AI, the Beijing-based startup behind the Kimi chatbot, plans to list on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange within six months.
- The company is circulating a shareholder resolution to secure investor approval for the offering.
- Moonshot recently released its Kimi K3 model on July 16 - an open-weight model built on roughly 2.8 trillion parameters using a mixture-of-experts architecture.
- The Kimi K3 launch triggered market volatility with Taiwan's benchmark index falling more than 6%, Japanese equities dropping 4%, and the Nasdaq sliding 1.5%.
- Hong Kong-listed rival Z.ai lost as much as 30% of its value, marking its steepest single-day decline since its January listing.
- Moonshot is simultaneously finalizing a funding round that could value the firm at more than $30 billion - a nearly sevenfold jump from its $4.3 billion valuation in December.
- The startup's annual recurring revenue reportedly doubled to about $200 million by April, up from roughly $100 million in early March.
- To qualify for a Hong Kong listing, Moonshot is dismantling its offshore VIE structure and replacing it with a joint venture model following regulatory guidance.
- Rival DeepSeek is also weighing an IPO after closing its first external funding round.
- The market reaction shows how closely AI headlines now move traditional risk assets, challenging assumptions about who leads the global AI race.
