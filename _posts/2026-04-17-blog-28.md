## Key Points

✅ Cursor's multi-agent system autonomously optimized 235 CUDA kernels for NVIDIA Blackwell 200 GPUs, achieving a 38% geometric mean speedup over baselines
✅ The system accomplished in just 3 weeks what typically takes experienced kernel engineers months or years of work
✅ NVIDIA collaborated with Cursor to generate optimization problems from 124 production open-source models including Deepseek, Qwen, Gemma, Kimi, and Stable Diffusion
✅ The multi-agent system outperformed baselines on 149 out of 235 problems (63%), with 19% of optimizations delivering greater than 2x improvements
✅ The system was tested with two different programming approaches: CUDA C with inline PTX (low-level hardware access) and CuTe DSL (high-level abstractions)
✅ On specific problems like BF16 Grouped Query Attention with Paged Prefill, the system achieved an 84% speedup over baseline and near-hardware-limit performance
✅ For NVFP4 MoE Linear with Gating, the system correctly identified quantization bottlenecks and delivered a 39% speedup
✅ On notoriously difficult BF16 matrix multiplication kernels, the system came remarkably close (86%) to NVIDIA's highly optimized cuBLAS library, and outperformed it by up to 9% on small-M cases
✅ The system operated autonomously using a planner agent that distributed work across autonomous workers and continuously tested, debugged, and optimized kernels without developer intervention
✅ This experiment demonstrates that multi-agent systems can tackle novel, open-ended problems that fall outside training data distribution and outperform single-agent approaches
