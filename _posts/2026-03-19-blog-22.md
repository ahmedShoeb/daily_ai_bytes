## Key Points

- Open-H-Embodiment is introduced as the first healthcare robotics open dataset, created by a collaboration of 35 organizations including universities, hospitals, and companies like NVIDIA
- The dataset contains 778 hours of healthcare robotics training data covering surgical robotics, ultrasound, and colonoscopy autonomy data across simulation, benchtop exercises, and real clinical procedures
- Two new foundational AI models are released: GR00T-H (a Vision-Language-Action model for surgical robotics) and Cosmos-H-Surgical-Simulator (a World Foundation Model for surgical simulation)
- GR00T-H is trained on about 600 hours of Open-H-Embodiment data and uses four key design choices to handle the precision requirements and hardware variability in surgical robotics
- Cosmos-H-Surgical-Simulator addresses the sim-to-real gap by generating physically plausible surgical video directly from kinematic actions, reducing simulation time dramatically compared to real-world methods
- The project involves major healthcare robotics players including CMR Surgical, Johns Hopkins University, Stanford, and several leading universities worldwide
- Future goals include developing reasoning-capable autonomy systems that can explain, plan, and adapt across long surgical procedures
- All resources (dataset and models) are open source and available on Hugging Face and GitHub for community use and contribution
