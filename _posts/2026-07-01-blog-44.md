## Key Points

- Anthropic's Claude Code has made engineering teams roughly three times more productive, creating a bottleneck shift from coding to decision-making about what to build
- The traditional 1:8 ratio of product managers to engineers has effectively become 1:20 as AI tools dramatically increase engineering output
- The five-phase evolution of engineering workflows has progressed from Stack Overflow era (2014-2022) to browser-tab era, IDE-native era, spec-driven era, and now routines era (2026)
- Fundamentals like operating systems, networks, and concurrency are now leverage skills rather than hygiene skills, with greater importance in catching subtle AI errors
- Review has become the new writing as engineers generate more code than they can carefully read, with 84% of developers using AI tools but 46% not trusting the output
- Engineers now need product thinking skills including talking to customers, generating ideas, and working backwards from customer needs
- The engineer who matters in 2026 is the one who knows what to build, can prove it's worth building, and can ship it with an agent fleet while maintaining system integrity
- The bottleneck has moved from typing code to deciding what to type, requiring engineers to participate more in the product funnel
