## Key Points

- DeepSeek announced a 75% promotional discount on its newly released DeepSeek-V4-Pro model until May 5, 2026
- The company also cut the price of input cache hits across its entire API suite to one-tenth of previous levels, effective immediately
- Even at full price before discount, V4-Pro costs $0.145 per million input tokens and $3.48 per million output tokens, undercutting GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Claude Opus 4.7
- The promotional discount reduces V4-Pro input price to approximately $0.036 per million tokens
- V4-Pro is a mixture-of-experts model with 1.6 trillion total parameters and 49 billion active parameters per task, making it the largest open-weight model available
- The model is designed to run on Huawei's Ascend 950 chips and Cambricon hardware rather than Nvidia GPUs, reducing reliance on US technology
- This pricing move comes amidst geopolitical tensions, with the US accusing Chinese firms of 'industrial-scale' AI model distillation campaigns
- DeepSeek's strategy combines open-source availability, aggressive pricing, and 1 million-token context windows to lower barriers for developers
- The model natively integrates with popular coding frameworks like Claude Code, OpenClaw, and OpenCode used by Western developers
- The price cuts position DeepSeek as a low-cost challenger in the same week OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5 and the US restricted Chinese AI activities
