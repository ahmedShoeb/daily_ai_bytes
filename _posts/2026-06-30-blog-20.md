## Key Points

- Researchers propose MatText, a comprehensive benchmark suite for evaluating language models' ability to model materials using text representations
- MatText includes nine distinct text-based representations for materials, including five novel representations that incorporate various inductive biases
- Current language models consistently struggle to capture crucial geometric information needed for accurate materials property prediction
- Models tend to leverage local information (composition and bonding) rather than geometric positional data across all representations
- Adding more geometric information (like explicit 3D coordinates) doesn't improve performance and can sometimes degrade it
- Scaling pretraining doesn't necessarily lead to better performance on downstream materials property tasks, unlike natural language modeling
- Locality (local environment information) emerges as the strongest inductive bias for materials modeling
- The findings suggest conventional wisdom from natural language modeling may not apply to materials science problems
- The study used both BERT-style encoder models and Llama-3 8B decoder models, with consistent findings across architectures
- The work provides tools for systematically converting crystal structures into text and benchmarking language model performance
