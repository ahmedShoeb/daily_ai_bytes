## Key Points

- OpenAI is losing two key leaders: Kevin Weil, who led OpenAI for Science, and Bill Peebles, the researcher behind AI video tool Sora
- The departures follow OpenAI's decision to cut back on 'side quests' and consolidate around enterprise AI and its forthcoming 'superapp'
- Sora, the AI video tool, was shut down last month after reportedly losing an estimated $1 million per day in compute costs
- OpenAI for Science, the research group behind Prism AI platform, is being absorbed into 'other research teams'
- The team had controversy in October 2025 when Weil claimed GPT-5 solved 10 unsolved Erdős problems, but had to retract the claim after mathematical scrutiny
- Weil's departure comes just a day after his team released GPT-Rosalind, a new model for life sciences research and drug discovery
- Peebles argued in his departure announcement that research like Sora requires space away from mainline company roadmaps
- OpenAI is also losing Srinivas Narayanan, chief technology officer of enterprise applications, who's reportedly leaving to spend more time with family
- The departures signal a strategic shift as OpenAI focuses on enterprise AI solutions and streamlines its research initiatives
