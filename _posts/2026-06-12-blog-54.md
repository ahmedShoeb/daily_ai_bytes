## Key Points

- The AI industry has operated on the assumption that bigger models are more powerful and better, but this assumption may be starting to break due to mounting costs
- Coinbase co-founder Brian Armstrong predicts that 80% of AI workloads will shift to 99% cheaper models within 12-18 months, while only 20% will use the latest models where maximum intelligence is critical
- This shift could dramatically change AI economics, with savings coming out of big AI labs like OpenAI and Anthropic's pockets just as they're heading for IPOs
- Initial tests show cheaper models can substitute without quality loss: Harvey's legal AI tool reduced inference costs by 3x using a combination of Claude Opus and cheaper Fireworks GLM 5.1
- The real divide isn't between proprietary vs. open models but between large vs. small models - switching to smaller proprietary models works just as well for cost savings
- This represents a major shift from the industry's previous 'scaling-first' approach where companies defaulted to most advanced models
- With token prices rising and investor subsidies slowing, users are facing cost pressure for the first time, prompting reevaluation of model choices
- It's unclear if companies will switch to smaller models or simply economize through fewer API calls, less context usage, or abandoning less promising deployments
