## Key Points

- Research shows most organizations see modest 10-15% PR throughput gains from AI adoption - far less than the 10x increases often promised in headlines
- The median improvement was closer to 8%, showing incremental rather than transformational impact on productivity
- Developers spend only about 14% of their time writing code, limiting AI's impact when focused solely on coding acceleration
- Faster code generation can create new bottlenecks in reviews, QA, and technical oversight, offsetting potential gains
- 'False velocity' occurs when increased PR counts don't translate to meaningful business value acceleration
- AI can create 'cognitive debt' by reducing developers' understanding while increasing code output, potentially harming long-term maintainability
- The biggest opportunities for AI lie beyond coding - in planning, documentation, incident response, and other parts of the SDLC
- Autonomous agents can augment human capacity by working in parallel, not just speeding up individual developers
- Measurement frameworks need to evolve to distinguish between human productivity gains and work performed autonomously by agents
- Engineering leaders are closely monitoring technical debt, token spend, and long-term maintainability alongside productivity metrics
