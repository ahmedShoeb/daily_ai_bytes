## Key Points

- Alibaba will ban its employees from using Anthropic's programming tool Claude Code starting July 10, 2026
- Anthropic already prohibits Chinese companies and their foreign subsidiaries from using its AI models
- Anthropic has been working to close loopholes that allow Chinese users to access Claude
- The company reportedly implemented a version of Claude Code that could secretly identify Chinese users
- Anthropic's Thariq Shihipar described this as 'an experiment launched in March to prevent account abuse from unauthorized resellers and protect against distillation'
- Distillation refers to training AI models on the outputs of other models
- Shihipar said stronger mitigations have been implemented since then and they planned to remove the feature
- Alibaba has classified Claude Code as high-risk software and is directing employees to use its own Qoder tool instead
