## Key Points

82% of developers now find GenAI useful, up from 76% in 2025
54% of developers would lose significant productivity if AI tools were removed, showing AI has become essential infrastructure
Companies leading in AI are hiring MORE engineers, not fewer, with 48% growth in global technical assessments and 90% growth in US technical hiring
34% of developers feel less secure about job security despite AI productivity gains
AI is transforming what skills matter most - debugging, system design, and fine-tuning are rising in importance while writing new code declines
Hiring practices are lagging reality: 34% of hiring teams ban AI in assessments, while 46% allow it
Top cheating concern is now AI agent-generated solutions (52%), surpassing traditional concerns like plagiarism
Most effective assessment formats: technical discussions (56% developers, 60% recruiters) and live coding interviews (43% developers, 60% recruiters)
Hiring budgets expected to increase for 53% of companies, up from 37% in 2025
AI/ML specialists are the most in-demand role (35% hiring) and also the hardest to fill (26% struggle to recruit)
