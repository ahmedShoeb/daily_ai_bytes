## Key Points

- Meta has paused all work with data contracting firm Mercor while investigating a major security breach that impacted the startup
- The breach is indefinite and other major AI labs are also reevaluating their work with Mercor as they assess the scope of the incident
- Mercor is one of few firms that OpenAI, Anthropic and other AI labs rely on to generate proprietary training data for their models
- AI labs are highly sensitive about this data because it can reveal key details about how they train AI models to competitors including those in the US and China
- OpenAI is investigating Mercor's security incident but hasn't stopped current projects, confirming user data wasn't affected
- Mercor contractors working on Meta projects cannot log hours until/if projects resume, potentially leaving them out of work
- The breach appears to be connected to TeamPCP's compromise of two versions of the AI API tool LiteLLM, potentially affecting thousands of organizations
- A group claiming to be Lapsus$ also claimed responsibility for the breach, but researchers believe TeamPCP is the likely culprit
- TeamPCP has been involved in a larger supply chain hacking spree and appears to be financially motivated, possibly with some geopolitical elements
- The breach illustrates the sensitivity of AI training data and the security risks associated with third-party data contractors in the AI industry
