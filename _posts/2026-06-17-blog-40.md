## Key Points

+ The US government has issued an export control directive requiring Anthropic to suspend all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for foreign nationals, including foreign national Anthropic employees
+ The directive was received today at 5:21pm ET, citing national security concerns about a potential jailbreaking method
+ Anthropic must abruptly disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all customers to ensure compliance
+ Access to all other Anthropic models will not be affected
+ The government believes there's a method to bypass or "jailbreak" Fable 5, but Anthropic reviewed the technique and found it identifies minor vulnerabilities that other publicly-available models can also discover
+ Anthropic states their safeguards are "substantially more effective than those of any previously deployed model" and no testers have found a universal jailbreak
+ Anthropic disagrees that finding a narrow potential jailbreak should cause recalling a commercial model deployed to hundreds of millions of people
+ Anthropic believes this standard would essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers if applied across the industry
+ Anthropic is complying with the directive but working to restore access as soon as possible
+ The company believes the government should have transparent, fair, and technically-grounded processes for blocking unsafe deployments
