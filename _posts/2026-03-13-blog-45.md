## Key Points

- A federal judge temporarily blocked Perplexity from accessing Amazon's site with its Comet AI browser
- Amazon sued Perplexity in November, alleging the startup concealed its AI agents to continue scraping Amazon's website without authorization
- Perplexity's Comet allows shoppers to ask the assistant to find items on Amazon and make purchases
- Judge Maxine Chesney found Amazon provided strong evidence that Perplexity accessed its site without authorization and caused over $5,000 in response costs
- Amazon claims Perplexity's agents pose security risks to customer data by accessing private customer accounts
- The AI agents also created challenges for Amazon's advertising business by generating automated traffic that needs to be filtered
- Amazon has broadly blocked AI agents from its shopping sites, including OpenAI's ChatGPT, while promoting its own AI assistant Rufus
- Perplexity called the lawsuit a "bully tactic" and said it will continue fighting for users' right to choose their AI
