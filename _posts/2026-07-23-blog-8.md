## Key Points

- Google's parent company Alphabet is designing a new server chip called 'Frozen v2' to make its Gemini AI models more efficient
- The chip is slated for release in 2028 and could be 6-10 times more efficient than Google's existing AI chips
- Google neither confirmed nor denied the report but emphasized its 'full stack approach' of co-designing hardware and software
- AI companies are increasingly making their own chips to address efficiency concerns and reduce dependence on Nvidia
- OpenAI recently announced its first custom chip called Jalapeño, and Anthropic is reportedly discussing chip partnerships with Samsung
- Google plans to spend $180-190 billion on its AI strategy, making efficiency crucial for proving investment returns
- The news boosted Google's stock by about 3% on Monday morning ahead of its earnings report
