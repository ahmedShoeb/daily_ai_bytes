## Key Points

Anthropic announced a joint venture for enterprise AI services backed by Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, Goldman Sachs, and other major investment firms
The Anthropic venture is valued at $1.5 billion with $300 million commitments each from Anthropic, Blackstone, and Hellman & Friedman
OpenAI is preparing a similar venture called The Development Company, raising $4 billion from 19 investors at a $10 billion valuation
OpenAI's investors include TPG, Brookfield Asset Management, Advent, and Bain Capital - with no apparent overlap with Anthropic's investors
Both ventures aim to create channels for enterprise AI deals, giving preferred sales access to their investors' portfolio companies
The capital will support more engineering resources using the forward-deployed engineer (FDE) model popularized by Palantir
Anthropic's approach involves engineering teams working directly with clients to build tools that fit existing workflows
Both AI companies are fundraising aggressively - OpenAI recently announced $122 billion in new funding at an $852 billion valuation
Anthropic is in final stages of its own funding round seeking $50 billion at a $900 billion valuation
The ventures represent a strategic shift to capture more value from enterprise contracts while providing dedicated resources to client implementations
