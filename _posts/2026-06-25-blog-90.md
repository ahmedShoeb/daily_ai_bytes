## Key Points

SpaceX has signed a major computing power agreement with Reflection AI, an open-source AI startup
Under the deal, Reflection AI gets immediate access to Nvidia GB300s AI chips through SpaceX's Colossus data center infrastructure
Reflection AI has agreed to pay SpaceX $150 million per month from July 2026 through 2029, totaling about $6.3 billion if the full term is completed
Either company can end the contract with 90 days' notice after the first three months
The deal shows SpaceX expanding its AI infrastructure business beyond powering its own Grok AI chatbot
SpaceX previously struck computing power deals with Anthropic, Google, and Cursor (which SpaceX is now acquiring)
Reflection AI is an open-source AI startup valued at $25 billion, focused on creating American open-source AI models to compete with closed systems from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google
The timing is notable as open-source AI gains momentum after Anthropic cut off access to certain models
Reflection works with government and national security customers including the Department of Energy's Genesis Mission and Pentagon AI efforts
The deal positions SpaceX as a player in the AI infrastructure race, selling scarce GPU capacity alongside cloud providers
