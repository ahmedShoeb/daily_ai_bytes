## Key Points

Google has restricted Meta's use of its Gemini AI models because it cannot provide the full computing capacity Meta requested
The capacity limitations disrupted and delayed some of Meta's internal AI projects
Google informed Meta around March 2026 that it couldn't meet the full Gemini capacity the company sought to purchase
Meta has been particularly impacted due to its exceptionally high demand for Google's AI models
Several other Google clients have also been affected, though to a lesser extent than Meta
Meta has encouraged staff to be more efficient with AI tokens (units measuring AI usage) due to the restrictions
Companies continue to struggle securing enough computing power for AI services despite billions spent on chips and data centers
Google Cloud's revenue grew to $20 billion in Q1 ending March, but computing constraints prevented even higher growth
The cloud unit's backlog nearly doubled quarter-over-quarter due to computing power limitations
