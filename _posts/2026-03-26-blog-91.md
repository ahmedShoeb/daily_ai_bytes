## Key Points

- The author expected fine-tuning of AI models to be more common by now, but it hasn't become widespread as predicted
- Anthropic doesn't even have a generally available fine-tuning API
- Several overlapping reasons why fine-tuning isn't more popular: good prompts can do similar work more easily and cheaply, models are already good enough without fine-tuning, improved surrounding software like Claude Code makes fine-tuning unnecessary, development overhead for curating examples and updating systems isn't worth it
- The author acknowledges having an inflated sense of fine-tuning's value because it's particularly good for making flashcards (their specific use case)
- Current view: was wrong about how important fine-tuning would be for average engineers, but still worth knowing about
- Even without fine-tuning, there are situations where curating reference inputs and outputs is valuable
- Author still plans to use fine-tuning for their specific application (Zippyflash)
