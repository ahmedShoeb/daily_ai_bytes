## Key Points

- Factory announces Factory 2.0, the next phase in autonomous software engineering moving from individual coding agents to full software factories
- Software factories are interconnected, agent-native systems that span the entire software development lifecycle from bug reports to monitoring
- The system operates as a continuous feedback loop where monitoring deployed software generates signals that feed back into the development process
- Factory identifies three key requirements for robust software factories: Model Independence, Sovereign Intelligence, and Continual Learning & Self-Improvement
- Factory is already building software factories with major customers including NVIDIA, EY, Adobe, Palo Alto Networks, and others
- Autonomy is described as a gradual maturation process specific to each organization's readiness and comfort level
- Factory enables a spectrum of autonomy levels from simple Droid agents to complex multi-agent missions solving tasks over hours or days
- In this new era, engineers will be responsible for building the factories that build software rather than being sole custodians of software development
- The role of engineers expands to include governance, safety, and ownership of business outcomes
- Factory has expanded functionality with visibility to manage software factories directly in their desktop app
