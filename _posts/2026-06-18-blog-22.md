## Key Points

- DFlash is a novel speculative decoding method that uses a lightweight block diffusion draft model to generate entire blocks of tokens in parallel, making it more hardware-friendly than traditional autoregressive methods.
- The system employs KV injection, which extracts hidden representations from the target model's context tokens and injects them directly into the draft model's KV cache, allowing the draft model to focus purely on predicting the next block of tokens.
- DFlash achieves significant speed improvements: at concurrency 1 on HumanEval coding dataset, it achieves >4.3x throughput of baseline and 1.5x throughput of native MTP speculation.
- The integration with SGLang's Spec V2 engine reduces host-device synchronization overhead, improving performance by over 33% through overlap scheduling optimizations.
- Compared to existing methods like EAGLE-3, DFlash delivers higher end-to-end speedups: 3.3x vs 2.1x on GSM8K, 3.2x vs 2.2x on HumanEval, and 2.2x vs 1.4x on MT-Bench.
- The method avoids the linear cost scaling problem of autoregressive drafters by generating tokens in parallel, allowing deeper draft models without latency penalties.
- DFlash draft models are now available on Hugging Face, with a newly released model for Qwen 3.5 397B-A17B that outperforms native MTP speculation across all tested scenarios.
- The technology enables Xiaomi's new MiMo v2.5-Pro-UltraSpeed to achieve over 1,000 output tokens per second.
- The implementation includes optimizations like immediate materialization of KV projections and fused Triton kernels for norm+RoPE post-processing to maximize performance.
- This collaboration between Z Lab, SGLang, and Modal makes state-of-the-art inference acceleration available as open-source technology that anyone can use or build upon.
