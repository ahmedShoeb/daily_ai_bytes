## Key Points

- Thinking Machines argues today's AI systems have a ceiling because they use turn-based language models wrapped in helper components (the 'harness' approach)
- Their research proposes an 'interaction model' architecture with time-aligned micro-turns using 200-millisecond chunks instead of discrete conversation turns
- The system uses two coordinated models: a fast interaction model for real-time response and a slower background model for deep reasoning and tool use
- This architecture enables capabilities turn-based systems struggle with: speaking while listening (live translation), visual reaction during speech, and proactive interjections
- Thinking Machines built custom benchmarks (TimeSpeak, CueSpeak, RepCount-A, ProactiveVideoQA) showing their approach unlocks new capability classes
- The company plans to open a limited research preview soon, with wider release later this year and research grants for interaction model research
- Current limitations include challenges with long sessions, connectivity requirements for streaming audio/video, and scaling model size due to latency constraints
