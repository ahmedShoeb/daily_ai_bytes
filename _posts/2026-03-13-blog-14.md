## Key Points

- NVIDIA emphasizes that AI progress depends heavily on training data quality and accessibility, not just model capabilities
- Open data access reduces development costs and time, enabling faster, higher-quality model building with easier evaluation
- NVIDIA has released over 2 petabytes of AI-ready training data across 180+ datasets and 650+ open models
- Key datasets include Physical AI Collection with robotics/autonomous systems data, Nemotron Personas Collection for sovereign AI development, and La Proteina for protein modeling
- Nemotron training datasets have evolved to include higher-signal domains like math, code, and STEM knowledge for improved reasoning capabilities
- NVIDIA's 'extreme co-design' approach involves collaborative development between data strategists, researchers, and engineers
- Open datasets have demonstrated real-world impact: CrowdStrike improved NL→CQL translation accuracy from 50.7% to 90.4%, and Japanese models saw legal QA accuracy jump from 15.3% to 79.3%
- SPEED-Bench provides standardized evaluation for speculative decoding performance across different text categories and sequence lengths
- Retrieval-Synthetic-NVDocs-v1 enables rapid fine-tuning of embedding models with an 11% NDCG@10 improvement in just 3-4 days generation time
- Nemotron-ClimbMix achieved top performance on Time-to-GPT-2 leaderboards, reducing H100 compute time by ~33% compared to previous setups
