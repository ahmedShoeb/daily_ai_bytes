## Key Points

The article explores why OpenAI and Anthropic have stayed ahead of tech giants despite having infinite resources
It questions why technological progress seems to have a consistent rate rather than accelerating unpredictably
The author proposes there's a 'technological speed limit' - a maximum learning rate for the entire system of people, machines, and the world
Once enough funding and talent are gathered, this maximum speed can be reached, and adding more resources doesn't increase progress
OpenAI and Anthropic reached this speed limit early on, making them impossible to surpass technologically unless they make mistakes
The economy as a whole may have a similar speed limit on economic growth that we've been at for 50 years
Sustaining exponential growth requires increasingly Herculean effort over time
AI might simply keep us at the current speed limit rather than dramatically exceeding it
It's an open question whether AI will usher in dramatically faster growth or just maintain current exponential growth
