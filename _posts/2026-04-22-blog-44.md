## Key Points

The article contrasts two perspectives on the OpenClaw project presented at separate tech conferences: an inspiring TED talk for the general public versus a more sober engineering-focused AIE talk
OpenClaw faces unprecedented security challenges with 60x more security reports than curl and at least 20% of skill contributions being malicious
The project is dealing with major scaling issues as the fastest growing open source project in history
Anthropic launched Claude Design as its first design/prototyping tool powered by Claude Opus 4.7, which was seen as a direct competitor to design tools like Figma
Claude Opus 4.7 showed significant improvements, ranking #1 in multiple benchmarks and offering ~35% fewer output tokens with higher performance than its predecessor
OpenAI's Codex computer-use updates were highlighted as becoming a mainstream product category, with some users describing the experience as 'pretty close' to AGI in practical feel
The AI industry is converging on 'simple harness, strong evals, model-agnostic scaffolding' approaches rather than just chasing larger models
Emerging research includes agent monitoring systems, web skill extraction techniques, and open-world evaluations for messy real environments
Local inference capabilities continue to improve with Qwen3.6 workflows showing viability for local agent stacks
Large-scale compute infrastructure projects like Stargate are on track to consume massive power resources comparable to New York City's peak demand
