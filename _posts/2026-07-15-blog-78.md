## Key Points

- Greg Brockman takes over as OpenAI's second-in-command after Fidji Simo stepped down due to chronic illness
- Brockman will oversee ChatGPT product business, go-to-market teams, enterprise teams, and compute initiatives
- Simo, the former product chief who was diagnosed with POTS, will transition to a part-time advisor role
- Brockman is under pressure to justify OpenAI's $852 billion valuation ahead of an expected historic IPO
- OpenAI confidentially filed its IPO prospectus in June, but reportedly delaying the debut until next year
- OpenAI faces stiff competition from Anthropic, Google, SpaceX, and cheaper Chinese open-weight models
- ChatGPT's market share fell below 50% for the first time in March, according to Sensor Tower
- Sarah Friar (CFO) and Jason Kwon (strategy chief) will now report directly to CEO Sam Altman
- Brockman and Altman have been close allies since co-founding OpenAI in 2015 and both briefly left in 2023
- Brockman recently testified in Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI, which Musk ultimately lost
