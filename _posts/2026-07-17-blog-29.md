## Key Points

- Elon Musk has quietly purchased APR Energy, a Jacksonville-based company operating mobile gas and diesel turbines totaling over 1 GW of generation capacity
- The acquisition was discovered through an FTC notice (transaction #20261350) dated May 14, 2026, not through any public announcement
- The deal is valued at over $1 billion based on minority stake disclosures
- APR Energy deploys trailer-mounted gas turbines that can reach full power in under 10 minutes and be installed in days rather than years
- Musk is using this fossil fuel power to feed electricity-hungry data centers running xAI's Grok chatbot
- xAI's supercomputers in Memphis have been running on unpermitted gas turbines that environmental groups say emit over 2,000 tons of smog-forming nitrogen oxides
- The Department of Justice intervened to keep xAI's turbines running, arguing they're a matter of "national, economic, and energy security"
- Well over half of Grok's traffic is now driven by adult content according to reports from former xAI employees
- Musk has abandoned his "solar electric economy" vision and recently dropped "sustainable" from Tesla's mission statement
- This represents a major contradiction for Musk who previously called fossil fuels "the dumbest experiment in history" and championed solar energy
