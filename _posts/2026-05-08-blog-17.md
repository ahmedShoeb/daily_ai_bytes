## Key Points

- The article examines how efficiently LLM weights store information using Shannon entropy measurements
- BF16 weights carry about 10.6 bits of entropy per element (out of 16 bits allocated), wasting about 1/3 of the budget
- The waste is concentrated in the exponent field, which carries only about 2.6 bits of entropy out of 8 allocated
- Weight magnitudes across trained models are clustered in a narrow unimodal distribution with a long left tail
- FP8 weights carry about 6.5 bits of entropy out of 8 bits allocated, using ~80% of the budget (vs BF16's 66%)
- At FP4 precision, models must adapt their weight distributions as constraints tighten below the 2.6-bit entropy floor
- Sub-byte formats (FP4, INT4, NVFP4) push per-element bits closer to saturation with ~93% budget utilization
- The author argues that 7-30% of bits remain wasted across formats, suggesting opportunities for more efficient compression
- Fixed-length formats are hardware-friendly but may not be optimal for eliminating the remaining waste
