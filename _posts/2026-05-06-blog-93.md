## Key Points

- OpenAI, Anthropic, and Microsoft have each signed agreements with the Pentagon to do classified work on artificial intelligence
- These deals will allow the companies to work with the Defense Department on some of its most sensitive AI projects
- The agreements mark a significant shift for tech companies that had ethical concerns about military applications of AI
- OpenAI previously had policies rejecting military use of its technology, but has since revised those
- The Pentagon's Joint AI Center is now able to work with commercial AI tools for classified projects
- Microsoft has established a dedicated team to work with U.S. intelligence agencies on AI projects
- The military aims to use AI for intelligence analysis, targeting systems, and battlefield decision-making
- These partnerships demonstrate how cutting-edge commercial AI technology is becoming integrated into national security
- The deals come amid growing competition between the U.S. and China in AI development
- Tech companies are navigating complex ethical and business considerations as they work with government security agencies
