## Key Points

Liam Price, a 23-year-old with no advanced math training, used ChatGPT Pro to solve a 60-year-old Erdős problem that had eluded prominent mathematicians.
The problem concerns 'primitive sets' of whole numbers where no number can be evenly divided by any other, with an Erdős sum that needed verification.
The AI's solution was different from human approaches - it used a well-known formula from related math fields that no human mathematician had thought to apply to this specific problem.
Terence Tao and Jared Duker Lichtman reviewed and refined the AI's proof, recognizing its novelty and potential for broader applications.
The AI's 'cognitive leap' suggests a new way to think about large numbers and their anatomy, potentially unifying previously separate mathematical problems.
Price entered the problem randomly while 'vibe mathing' with Erdős problems, and the solution came from a single prompt to GPT-5.4 Pro.
This represents a significant AI achievement in mathematics, different from recent AI math solutions that often rehash known methods or solve easier problems.
