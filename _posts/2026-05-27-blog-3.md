## Key Points

DeepSeek has made its 75% price discount on the flagship V4 Pro model permanent, reducing pricing from $0.0145-$3.48 to $0.003625-$0.87 per million tokens
The new pricing undercuts major competitors: GPT-5 ($2.50 input/$10 output per million tokens), Claude Opus ($5/$25), and Gemini 3.5 Flash ($0.15/$0.60)
The model supports a one-million-token context window at the discounted rate, targeting document analysis, legal review, and codebase applications
DeepSeek's strategy prioritizes market share over per-unit revenue, accelerating industry-wide margin compression and commoditization of AI tokens
The price advantage faces challenges including Anthropic's accusation of 'distillation attacks' (improper training on Claude's responses) and geopolitical concerns about using Chinese AI providers
Enterprise adoption faces a tradeoff between significant cost savings and concerns about data sensitivity, compliance posture, and intellectual property controversies
The pricing pressure threatens revenue-per-token economics supporting competitors' valuations, with Anthropic's annualized revenue surging from $9B to $30B in recent months
DeepSeek's bet is that price will attract volume regardless of geopolitical complexities, potentially bifurcating the market into Western and Chinese tiers with different economics
The permanent price cut was announced one month after launching V4 models, described as welcoming the 'era of cost-effective 1M context length'
