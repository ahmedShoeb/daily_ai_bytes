## Key Points

Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol were tested on an NP-hard optimization problem (KIRO fiber-network design) with and without their native /goal mode
Fable 5 produced the best overall solution and showed remarkable consistency, described as 'pure raw intelligence'
The /goal feature is not a generic 'try harder' switch - it changes the control loop and search path, sometimes finding better solutions and sometimes allowing bad ideas to mature
Fable 5's plain mean score beat GPT-5.6 Sol's by 1,875 points, showing clear performance superiority
/goal helped win 4 out of 6 individual trials but made average performance worse for both models
The benchmark reveals that persistence features can win most individual trials while making observed average performance worse on hard optimization problems
Claude Code and Codex implement /goal differently - Claude uses a separate evaluator model, while Codex uses persisted thread state with lifecycle tools
The quality of the optimization loop matters less than the quality of what the loop keeps doing on hard problems
