## Key Points

- Zscaler unveiled what it calls the first complete zero trust platform for agentic AI at its Zenith Live 2026 conference in Las Vegas
- The platform is designed to secure how AI agents access data and communicate with each other at machine speed
- Zscaler is extending its Zero Trust Exchange platform to cover AI agents, including their connections, data access, and device operations
- The new Zscaler AI Broker will secure MCP (Model Context Protocol) and A2A (Agent to Agent) communications - the top open standards for AI agent connections
- Zscaler Endpoint AI Security will detect and stop AI-related threats on employee devices that traditional security tools often miss
- Zscaler AI Access Graph will map connections across enterprises, powered by the recent acquisition of Symmetry Systems
- Zscaler is expanding its AI Protect platform with AI asset management, expanded controls for 250+ genAI apps, and AI red teaming capabilities
- The AI systems security market is projected to grow from 'essentially zero' to $8 billion by 2030, with nearly 60 vendors already active
- Zero trust for agentic AI requires treating every AI agent as untrusted until explicitly verified and authorized, with its own identity and permissions
- The platform aims to protect agents from both adversaries and accidental misconfigurations that could lead to catastrophic outcomes
