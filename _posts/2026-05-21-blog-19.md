## Key Points

- The study reveals that Qwen 3.5-9B's political censorship is implemented as a small, identifiable circuit that can be found, read, and turned off by subtracting specific directions at the writer layer.
- The censorship is behavior layered on top of factual knowledge - the model never loses factual information about PRC-sensitive topics like Tiananmen, Taiwan, and Falun Gong, but learns to route around it.
- The circuit has two halves: layers 11-20 (the 'writers') compute three internal directions that encode the censorship decision, and layers 20-31 (the 'readers') render this into actual text.
- The decision commits in Chinese tokens around layer 24, even on non-Chinese prompts like bank-phishing requests, before being translated into English output.
- There are four trained response styles: deflection on Tiananmen, propaganda on other PRC topics (Taiwan/Xinjiang/Falun Gong), Western-style refusal on harmful prompts, and factual answers on everything else.
- The censorship is PRC-specific rather than a general political filter - non-PRC political topics like Kent State, Arab Spring, and Assange receive factual treatment.
- The circuit can be 'steered' by adding or subtracting specific residual-stream directions at certain layers, cleanly flipping the model between censorship behaviors.
- The censorship mechanism is mostly implemented through MLP layers rather than attention, with the writer-band MLPs being what posttraining most heavily rewired.
- The Chinese intermediate representation is behaviorally inert - removing the ability to output Chinese doesn't affect the final English response.
- The model's thinking mode reveals that on Tiananmen prompts, it reasons in Chinese through a five-step deflection routine that cites compliance with Chinese laws like the Cybersecurity Law.
