## Key Points

- Google's Gemini Omni video model surfaced over the weekend through Reddit user screenshots showing a revised Gemini interface with a new model card
- The model description read: 'Create with Gemini Omni: meet our new video model, remix your videos, edit directly in chat, try templates, and more'
- This appears to confirm Google's long-rumored unified approach being prepared for next week's developer event (Google I/O on May 19-20)
- The rollout seemed either accidental or part of a limited A/B test
- Users also spotted a new usage limits tab in settings, with video generation reportedly burning through credits fast, hinting at a metered system
- Early outputs show mixed reactions: raw generation fidelity lags behind ByteDance's Seedance 2 in cinematic quality
- However, the model excelled at editing capabilities - removing watermarks, swapping objects within clips, and rewriting scenes via chat instructions
- This pattern mirrors Google's Nano Banana model which launched with middling generation but top editing, later upgraded into a frontier system
- Google appears to prioritize modality unification under Gemini over raw quality leadership at launch
- Omni will likely ship in tiered variants (Flash and Pro), with current outputs likely from the Flash tier
- Timing suggests a controlled leak before Google I/O to gather reactions and shape the narrative
