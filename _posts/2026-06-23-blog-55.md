## Key Points

Amazon has launched a new AI-powered feature in its Shopping app that allows users to design custom merchandise using AI prompts
The feature is accessible through the Alexa icon in the Amazon Shopping app or by searching 'customize' in the search bar
Users can describe their design ideas to Alexa and see AI-generated designs for various products including apparel and drinkware
The service uses Amazon's print-on-demand platform, Merch on Demand, for production and delivery
Amazon suggests use cases like family reunion T-shirts, personalized gifts, or pet portraits
The feature currently only available in the U.S. and is free to use, with customers only paying for the products themselves
This move positions Amazon as a direct competitor to online merch platforms like Redbubble, Bonfire, Spring, and Fourthwall
Amazon handles production and delivery through Prime shipping, making the process seamless for consumers
Supported merchandise includes various clothing items (T-shirts, hoodies, sweatshirts, etc.) and drinkware (tumblers, water bottles)
Users can edit designs using suggested actions or by typing changes and share results with friends and family
