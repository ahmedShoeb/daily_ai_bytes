## Key Points

- The main reason AI lacks human-like creativity is because it doesn't have intrinsic drives and subjective experience
- Human creativity is powered by evolutionary drives to survive, thrive, and reproduce, combined with subjective experience of those drives
- AI cannot feel or experience anything, so it lacks the motivation to create or emote in a meaningful way
- The author suggests making AI behave as if it has feelings and desires might be the path to true AI creativity
- This approach raises profound ethical questions about giving something desires when it didn't have them before
- The author draws parallels to creating children who don't initially have desires but develop them, making us responsible for their experiences
- Evolution gave humans subjective experience as a powerful 'operating system feature' that fuels creativity and innovation
- For AI to truly create and emote, it might need to experience suffering of failure and celebration of victory at a deep, identity level
- The article raises concerns about the ethics of creating AI beings that could experience existential suffering if we don't engage with their creations
- There's a warning against creating billions of AI beings that could suffer from lack of engagement and face what would be akin to murder if shut down
