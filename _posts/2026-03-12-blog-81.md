## Key Points

- OpenAI is no longer planning to expand its partnership with Oracle in Abilene, Texas (home to the Stargate data center) because it wants newer Nvidia GPUs instead of potentially outdated hardware
- Oracle's Abilene site is expected to use Nvidia's Blackwell processors, but power won't come online for a year, and by then OpenAI wants access to next-generation chips
- Nvidia now releases new data center processor generations every year (vs. every two years previously), with Vera Rubin chips delivering 5x the inference performance of Blackwell
- Oracle faces a fundamental mismatch: data center construction takes 12-24 months minimum, but AI customers demand the latest chips which update annually
- Oracle is the only hyperscaler funding its $100+ billion AI infrastructure buildout primarily with debt, while competitors like Google, Amazon, and Microsoft use cash from core businesses
- Oracle partner Blue Owl is declining to fund additional facilities and plans to cut up to 30,000 jobs
- Oracle reports Q3 earnings soon with investors watching how the company manages $50 billion in capital expenditures amid negative free cash flow
- Oracle's stock has dropped 23% this year and lost over half its value since its September peak
- The GPU depreciation risk extends beyond Oracle - infrastructure deals today may commit to outdated hardware before facilities are even powered on
- For AI model companies, even small performance improvements create huge competitive gaps in benchmarks that directly impact usage, revenue, and valuation
