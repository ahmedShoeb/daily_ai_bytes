## Key Points

- Cursor introduces self-summarization as a reinforcement learning technique for their Composer AI model to handle long-horizon coding tasks
- Traditional compaction methods (text summarization, sliding windows, latent space) have limitations as they can cause models to forget critical information
- Composer is trained to self-summarize at fixed token-length triggers, learning to preserve essential context efficiently
- The self-summarization process involves generating until trigger length, summarizing context, then continuing with condensed context plus conversation state
- Self-summarization uses only 1,000 tokens on average versus baseline methods requiring 5,000+ tokens and complex prompts
- Testing shows self-summarization reduces compaction errors by 50% while using one-fifth of the tokens compared to baseline methods
- Composer successfully solved the challenging 'make-doom-for-mips' problem from Terminal-Bench 2.0 in 170 turns, summarizing 100,000+ tokens down to 1,000
- The technique enables solving complex software engineering tasks that require hundreds of actions and extensive codebase exploration
- Self-summarization is a step toward Cursor's goal of training models over even longer, more complex processes including multi-agent coordination
- Better model training through techniques like self-summarization aims to improve the scope and intelligence of agentic coding systems
