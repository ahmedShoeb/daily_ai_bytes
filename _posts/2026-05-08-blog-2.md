## Key Points

Allen AI has launched MolmoAct 2, a substantial upgrade to their Action Reasoning Model (ARM) that outperforms proprietary robotics models and runs up to 37x faster than its predecessor
The model handles various real-world tasks out of the box without per-task fine-tuning and includes baked-in bimanual capabilities
MolmoAct 2 uses Molmo 2-ER, a specialized embodied-reasoning variant that scores ahead of systems like GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro, and Qwen3-VL-8B on embodied-reasoning benchmarks
The release includes the MolmoAct 2-Bimanual YAM dataset, the largest open-source bimanual tabletop manipulation robotics dataset ever published with over 720 hours of training demonstrations
Inference speed is dramatically improved: a single action call takes about 180 ms in the base model versus 6,700 ms in MolmoAct
MolmoAct 2 introduces an adaptive-depth mechanism for 3D reasoning that predicts depth only when it improves task performance, achieving a 17% speedup
In real-world zero-shot tests on a Franka arm, MolmoAct 2 averaged 87.1% success rate compared to 48.4% for its predecessor and 45.2% for Physical Intelligence's π0.5
The model has been pilot tested in real-world applications including with Stanford researchers for CRISPR gene-editing experiments in wetlab environments
MolmoAct 2 achieved the highest average score in third-party benchmarking by Cortex AI, ranking first on 7 of 8 bimanual tasks
The release includes full open access to model weights, datasets, and a reference robot hardware setup to lower the barrier to deployment for researchers
