## Key Points

- The article focuses on optimizing inference speed for coding agents using open models like Qwen, GLM, DeepSeek, and MiniMax
- Three key optimizations: training specialized speculators, automatically researching kernels, and custom interconnect design
- Trained speculators achieve 3.07x speedup vs 1.93x for generic drafts by training on the model's own coding output
- Automated kernel research on affordable GPUs ($7K RTX PRO 6000) achieves 162 tok/s vs 97 tok/s baseline
- Custom PCIe-based all-reduce kernels and TCP-based prefix caching overcome NVLink limitations
- Coding workloads benefit most from speculation with 97% token reuse across agent turns
- The approach enables running on hardware typically written off by the general ML stack
- Focus on small, overtrained models optimized for specific distributions rather than general scaling
- All optimizations target the coding agent as the highest-volume AI workload
