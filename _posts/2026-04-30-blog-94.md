## Key Points

- NVIDIA's B200 GPU rental prices surged 114% in six weeks, from $2.31 to $4.95 per hour on the Ornn Compute Price Index
- The price spread between B200 (Blackwell) and H200 (Hopper) chips more than doubled from $0.28 to $1.80 per hour
- Major AI model releases (like GPT-5.5) correlate with B200 price spikes, as newer models require newer chip architectures for memory and inference needs
- The provider price spread for B200s has more than doubled since September 2025, indicating an opaque market with significant supply/demand shocks
- The B200-over-H200 price gap initially collapsed to near-parity by November 2025 but has since widened back to near-launch levels, especially after GPT-5.3-Codex's release
- This widening gap signals depreciation of older chips when new AI models demand newer architectures
- Cloud providers are regaining pricing power after six months of margin compression
- For AI startups, spot market prices typically lead contract pricing by about 90 days, suggesting B200 rates will likely stay above $5.00 for the summer
- Frontier AI model inference is becoming more expensive as inflationary demand outpaces efficiencies from algorithmic and chip improvements
