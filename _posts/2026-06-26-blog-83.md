## Key Points

NVIDIA and AWS announce three major AI infrastructure advancements to support production-scale AI deployment
New Amazon EC2 G7 instances powered by NVIDIA RTX PRO 4500 Blackwell Server Edition GPUs offer up to 4.6x AI inference performance and 2.1x graphics performance compared to G6 instances
Amazon OpenSearch Serverless now uses NVIDIA cuVS as default for GPU-accelerated vector indexing, making vector search up to 10x faster at a quarter of the cost
AWS achieves NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud status for NVIDIA GB300 training workloads, indicating consistent high-performance cloud infrastructure for large-scale training
G7 instances support up to eight GPUs with 256GB total GPU memory, 700 Gbps networking, and up to 7.6TB local NVMe SSD storage across multiple configurations
The platform serves multiple workloads including AI inference, graphics, spatial computing, video workflows, and GPU-accelerated data analytics
AWS customers now have production-grade AI infrastructure that scales without adding operational complexity
Vector indexing improvements make billion-scale vector databases practical to build in under an hour
