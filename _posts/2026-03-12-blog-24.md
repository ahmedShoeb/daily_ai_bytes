## Key Points

Copilot Cowork represents Microsoft's next evolution of AI assistance, moving beyond just answering questions to actually taking action and completing tasks on your behalf.
The system uses Work IQ technology to understand your work context across Outlook, Teams, Excel, and other Microsoft 365 apps, allowing it to act with the same understanding you bring to your job.
When you delegate work to Cowork, it creates a plan with clear checkpoints, allowing you to approve actions, make changes, or pause execution at any time while maintaining control.
Cowork can clean up your calendar by reviewing Outlook schedules, flagging conflicts, and automatically rescheduling meetings while protecting focus time.
The system can prepare entire meeting packets by pulling information from emails, meetings, and files to generate briefing documents, analysis, and client-ready decks.
Cowork handles deep research tasks by gathering earnings reports, SEC filings, analyst commentary, and news to create executive summaries, research memos, and Excel workbooks.
For product launches, Cowork can build competitive comparisons, create value proposition documents, generate pitch decks, and outline project milestones and owners.
Microsoft has integrated Anthropic's Claude Cowork technology into Microsoft 365 Copilot, creating a multi-model advantage that selects the best model for each specific task.
Copilot Cowork operates within Microsoft 365's enterprise security and governance boundaries, with identity, permissions, and compliance policies applying by default.
The system is currently in Research Preview with limited customers and will be more broadly available through the Frontier program in late March 2026.
