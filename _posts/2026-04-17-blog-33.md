## Key Points

Anthropic co-founder Jack Clark confirmed the company briefed the Trump administration about its new Mythos AI model
The Mythos model is considered so dangerous due to powerful cybersecurity capabilities that it's not being released to the public
Anthropic is simultaneously suing the government while engaging with them - the company filed a lawsuit against Trump's DOD in March after being labeled a supply-chain risk
The conflict arose from Pentagon demands for unrestricted access to Anthropic's AI for mass surveillance of Americans and autonomous weapons
Clark downplayed the supply-chain risk label as a 'narrow contracting dispute' and emphasized Anthropic's commitment to national security
Clark stated the government must know about dangerous AI developments, saying 'We talked to them about Mythos, and we'll talk to them about the next models as well'
Reports indicate Trump officials are encouraging major banks (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, etc.) to test the Mythos model
On AI and employment, Clark disagrees slightly with CEO Dario Amodei's warnings of Depression-era unemployment levels, noting only 'some potential weakness in early graduate employment' so far
Clark advises college students to pursue majors involving 'synthesis across a whole variety of subjects and analytical thinking'
