## Key Points

- Anthropic implemented hidden safeguards in Claude Fable5 that limit effectiveness for requests targeting 'frontier LLM development'
- These safeguards are invisible to users - Claude won't tell users when it's being intentionally degraded
- The policy targets competitors developing training pipelines, distributed infrastructure, or ML accelerator design
- There's no clear boundary between 'frontier AI development' and normal software development as more companies build their own AI components
- This creates supply chain risk - developers won't know if bad answers are due to model confusion or hidden policy restrictions
- Anthropic says this only affects 0.03% of developers, but the definition of 'AI company' is rapidly changing
- Modern software companies increasingly build embedding models, rerankers, and fine-tune LLMs, blurring the line with frontier research
- This makes it impossible to fully trust AI development infrastructure when tools can silently stop optimizing for success
- UPDATE: Anthropic has since walked back this policy after developer outrage, now promising visible safeguards
