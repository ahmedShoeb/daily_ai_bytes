## Key Points

Google is expanding its 'Skills' functionality across its AI product lineup, starting with AI Studio's Build section
Skills are reusable instruction sets that guide Gemini toward consistent outputs and enable it to leverage specific tools and capabilities
The feature was previously spotted in development for Gemini for Enterprise, but is now coming to consumer-facing Gemini
Users have noticed a new 'Agent' tab in Gemini sidebar containing sub-tabs for Skills and Schedules
The combination of Skills and Schedules would enable automated, repeating AI workflows, positioning Gemini closer to a persistent agent framework
For developers in AI Studio, Skills would standardize model behavior across projects without rewriting system prompts
Google has been building out AI Studio as a more full-featured development environment, with indications of a possible desktop application
The broader rollout could be part of Google's narrative for Google I/O 2026 in May
Skills adoption across consumer Gemini, Enterprise, and AI Studio would give Google a unified customization layer
Google is catching up to competitors like Anthropic and OpenAI who have already shipped similar features
