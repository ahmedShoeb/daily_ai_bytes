## Key Points

- Meta abruptly removed a new AI image generation feature called Muse Image that allowed users to create fake images from Instagram user content
- The feature allowed Meta AI chatbot users to tag public Instagram accounts and use content from those accounts to create AI-generated content
- Users were automatically opted in by default, meaning anyone with a public account could have their likeness used without knowledge or permission
- The feature sparked significant privacy concerns and backlash from users and privacy advocates
- Meta admitted it 'missed the mark' and that the feature is 'no longer available'
- Hollywood union Sag-Aftra described Meta's reversal as a 'win' for protecting creative likenesses
- Privacy International criticized the feature, calling it another example of AI companies exploiting people's images and data as 'raw material'
- Meta stated its intent was to provide a useful creative tool with user control over their public content
- Meta had announced Muse Image as part of a broader rollout of AI tools across its platforms including WhatsApp, Facebook, and Messenger
- Meta also has an AI video tool currently in development
