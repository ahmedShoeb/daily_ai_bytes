## Key Points

Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 introduced a new tokenizer that improves understanding but increases input costs by inflating token counts
OpenRouter's analysis found Opus 4.7 produces 32-45% more native tokens than Opus 4.6 for the same text, with smaller prompts seeing higher inflation
Despite significant token inflation, prompt caching absorbs most of the cost impact - 64% of extra tokens land in cache for 25K-50K prompts, and 93% for 128K+ prompts
Opus 4.7 generates 62% fewer tokens for short prompts under 2K, but produces 13-30% more tokens for longer context prompts (10K+)
Actual costs increased 12-27% for prompts above 2K tokens, while short prompts under 2K became slightly more cost efficient (-1.6%) due to shorter completions
The analysis used OpenRouter's consistent 'QuadChars' tokenizer as a baseline to isolate tokenizer changes from content differences
Data came from over one million requests by users who switched from Opus 4.6 to Opus 4.7 as their primary model
Completion lengths diverge based on prompt size: Opus 4.7 is more concise for short queries but generates longer responses for complex prompts
