## Key Points

- GPT-5.6 Sol ranks 1st overall on Design Arena's Web Design (Non-Agentic) Arena, which is 18 places higher than its predecessor GPT-5.5
- The model has learned to recognize and actively suppress common AI design anti-patterns like purple gradients, bento-box layouts, oversized hero text, and offset compositions
- Analysis shows GPT-5.6 Sol's design space contains clear gaps where previous models produced these anti-patterns, suggesting it consciously avoids generating them
- GPT-5.6 Sol combines strong templates with unusually high personalization, striking a better balance between consistency and variety than other models
- The model establishes new Pareto frontiers for both preference vs speed and preference vs price, being over 2.44x faster than GLM 5.2 and 36% faster than Claude Fable 5
- Unlike other models that simply avoid anti-patterns, GPT-5.6 appears to have learned specific design anti-patterns exist but refuses to produce them
- The model still has some limitations, such as overusing confetti in over 26.5% of generations and not excelling at creating charts and data visualizations
- GPT-5.6 Sol's approach involves customized templates that create variance within each cluster, similar to how bacteria evolve into different genetic strains
- This combination of selective suppression and adaptive templating gives GPT-5.6 Sol its design advantage on the leaderboard
