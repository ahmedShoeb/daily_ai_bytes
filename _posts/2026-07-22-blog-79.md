## Key Points

- General Compute, an AI inference cloud startup, secured a $400 million loan from Upper90, marking what may be the first deal using inference-specific chips as collateral
- The deal signals a market shift toward infrastructure that runs open source AI models more cheaply than expensive frontier lab LLMs
- General Compute's SN50 chips from SambaNova are designed specifically for inference, offering 16x faster performance than GPU-based clouds and not requiring expensive water-cooling systems
- Upper90 previously pioneered GPU-backed loans in 2021 with Crusoe, but now sees inference chips as the next wave as GPU markets become oversaturated
- The company's CEO believes open source models will be increasingly important, stating 'Everyone doesn't need a supercomputer, but they do need inference and AI'
- This financing represents capital organizing around alternatives to Nvidia's dominance as more chipmakers like SambaNova, Groq, and Cerebras emerge
- General Compute raised $15 million in seed funding in May to build an inference neocloud specifically for AI workloads
- The deal highlights a growing market trend where compute providers not locked into Nvidia deals may have advantages in providing cost-efficient inference
- Upper90 sees this as analogous to their earlier GPU financing strategy - getting in early on an inefficient market to get compensated for risk
- As more alternatives to Nvidia emerge, the investment represents a significant shift in AI infrastructure financing patterns
