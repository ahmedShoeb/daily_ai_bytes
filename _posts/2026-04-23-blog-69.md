## Key Points

Moonshot AI has released Kimi K2.6, positioning it as open-source state-of-the-art for coding and agentic workloads
The model is available on kimi.com in both chat and agent modes, with weights published on Hugging Face and API access through platform.moonshot.ai
Four variants are offered: K2.6 Instant (quick responses), K2.6 Thinking (deeper reasoning), K2.6 Agent (research, slides, websites, docs, sheets), and K2.6 Agent Swarm (large-scale search, batch tasks)
Moonshot claims open-source leadership on multiple benchmarks: Humanity's Last Exam with tools at 54.0, SWE-Bench Pro at 58.6, SWE-bench Multilingual at 76.7
Additional benchmark results: BrowseComp at 83.2, Toolathlon at 50.0, Charxiv with Python at 86.7, and Math Vision with Python at 93.2
Comparison charts show Kimi K2.6 competing against GPT-5.4 xhigh, Claude Opus 4.6 at max effort, and Gemini 3.1 Pro thinking high
The model visually leads on SWE-bench Multilingual and BrowseComp in benchmark comparisons
This release follows a K2.6 Code Preview beta that launched on April 13, and comes after K2.5's hybrid reasoning debut earlier this year
Moonshot is positioning open weights and aggressive agent scaling as its differentiators in a competitive AI field dominated by Claude Opus 4.6, GPT-5.4, and Gemini 3.1 Pro
