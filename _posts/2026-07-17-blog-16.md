## Key Points

- Google Images is celebrating its 25th anniversary this week, marking the evolution of visual search on the web
- Google is introducing a new browseable home for Google Images with a dynamic, immersive gallery of web images updated in real time and tailored to user interests
- Image generation is coming directly to AI Overviews in Search using the Nano Banana model to create custom visuals from text prompts
- The article traces Google's 25-year visual search history starting with the 2001 launch of Google Images, inspired by Jennifer Lopez's green Versace dress viral moment
- Key innovations highlighted include Similar Images (2009), Search by Image (2011), Google Lens in Search (2018), and Multisearch in Lens (2022)
- Recent developments include Circle to Search (2024), Lens + AI Mode (2025), Search Live (2025), Visual Results in AI Mode (2025), and Circle to Search Multi-Object Recognition (2026)
- The new intelligent search box allows users to upload multiple images and ask detailed questions about them directly in Search
- The new browseable home for Google Images will roll out over coming weeks on desktop in the U.S. in English, requiring signing in to Google Account
- Image generation in AI Overviews will roll out over coming weeks in English for all regions that currently support image creation in AI Mode
- Google Images originally launched because text-only search results weren't enough for visual content like the famous green Versace dress that went viral
