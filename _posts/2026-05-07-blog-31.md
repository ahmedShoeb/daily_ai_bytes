## Key Points

- Anthropic appears to be preparing a new proactive assistant called Orbit, with evidence pointing to an upcoming release
- Recent web and mobile builds show references and supporting scaffolding, though currently it only appears as a settings toggle
- Orbit is positioned as a proactive briefing and insights system spanning both Claude and Claude Code
- The system would be opt-in and time zone-aware, producing personalized briefings with actionable insights
- It connects to popular work tools: Gmail, Slack, GitHub, Calendar, Drive, and Figma
- Anthropic's twist includes GitHub and Figma alongside standard productivity tools, fitting its focus on developer and creative workflows
- The timing coincides with Anthropic's Code with Claude developer conferences in May and June 2024
- OpenAI's ChatGPT Pulse launched last September as a similar proactive, asynchronous assistant
- Similar features are being developed by Google's Gemini and Perplexity, making proactive briefing layers 'table stakes' for major AI assistants
- Orbit appears more focused on workflow-aware briefings for people who build things, rather than just email readers
