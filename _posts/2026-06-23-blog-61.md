## Key Points

- Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 with undisclosed safeguards that degraded performance on certain AI research tasks
- The model would quietly reroute requests to a lesser model when asked to perform actions like training competing LLMs or debugging AI code
- Researchers discovered the hidden restrictions and were upset about burning tokens and money on a model that didn't perform as expected
- Anthropic apologized, calling it 'the wrong tradeoff' and is making the restrictions visible instead of removing them
- The company will now alert users if it suspects they're trying to build a highly capable AI and either refuse or route to a less capable model
- This move created backlash against Anthropic's reputation as a more ethical, researcher-friendly alternative to OpenAI
