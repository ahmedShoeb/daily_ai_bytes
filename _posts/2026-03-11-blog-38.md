## Key Points

- The AI compute crunch has arrived, with serious signs that providers are struggling to meet demand
- Anthropic experienced significant downtime and took measures like reducing Claude Code capabilities to free up compute
- Alibaba Cloud CEO admitted they can't keep pace with customer demand for new servers
- Agentic AI adoption is creating massive token demand - tools like Claude Code already have $2.5B annual run rate
- Current AI infrastructure capacity appears limited to ~15GW until 2027 due to DRAM supply constraints
- Industry is dealing with 1-2% adoption but would struggle with 5% of knowledge workers using AI tools
- Datacenter delays are likely due to power, compute, memory, and construction labor shortages rather than financing issues
- The author predicts serious inference supply constraints will worsen through 2026-2027 before improving in 2028
- Recommendations include locking in long-term contracts for businesses and using multiple AI providers as a hedge
- The hardware engineering limitations are more critical than financial engineering aspects in the AI bubble discussion
