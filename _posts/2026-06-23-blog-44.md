## Key Points

- Apple's WWDC 2026 keynote focused on refining its software platforms to be more polished, responsive, and tightly managed across devices
- The most visible change is refinements to Liquid Glass design language introduced last year, adding uniform refraction, improved contrast, sharper icons, and a tint/transparency slider
- iOS 27 brings performance improvements including faster app launches, quicker photo loading, faster AirDrop transfers, and smoother network transitions
- Apple expanded child safety tools with enhanced child accounts, Ask to Browse, Time Allowances, Schedules, and redesigned Screen Time for parental controls
- The company emphasized the importance of digital literacy while positioning these tools as ways for parents to manage children's digital access
- Apple acknowledged these safety tools shouldn't replace parental judgment or age-appropriate education about digital spaces
- The article cautions that while tools help parents monitor online activity, they can also create a false sense of security
- The updates follow last year's controversial Liquid Glass redesign, with this year focusing more on usability improvements rather than dramatic visual changes
