## Key Points

- OpenAI is testing ads in ChatGPT where advertisements will appear as natural, organic conclusions to chat conversations rather than suggestions.
- The current trial is invite-only with selected advertisers committing to at least $200,000 each. Known companies include Adobe, Target, Albertson, and media group WPP.
- ChatGPT ads work fundamentally differently from Google Ads - instead of monetizing keywords, ChatGPT interprets conversational context to serve ads.
- The platform uses a $60 CPM (cost-per-mille) pricing model, meaning advertisers pay $60 per every 1,000 ad impressions.
- Advertisers currently receive limited metrics (campaign name, impressions, clicks) compared to Google's detailed analytics, forcing brands to rely more on storytelling than clickbait.
- The AI acts as a curator selecting which ads to show, creating algorithmic selection rather than auction-based competition like Google Ads.
- This model could potentially create an AI-controlled demand allocation system, giving the platform significant power in determining which companies get visibility.
- Success requires understanding conversational intent and how users articulate needs in dialogue, not just direct search queries.
- The current system favors large corporations with resources to optimize campaigns without detailed performance data from OpenAI.
- ChatGPT's ad model represents a structural shift from traditional search advertising to AI-mediated recommendation systems.
