## Key Points

HY-World 2.0 is a multi-modal world model framework by Tencent that generates and reconstructs 3D worlds from diverse inputs including text, images, and videos
Unlike traditional video world models that produce pixel-level videos, HY-World 2.0 creates editable, persistent 3D assets (meshes/3DGS) that can be imported into game engines like Unity, Unreal, and Blender
The system offers two core capabilities: World Generation (text/single image → 3D world) and World Reconstruction (multi-view images/video → 3D)
WorldMirror 2.0 inference code and model weights have been released as of April 16, 2026, with full HY-World 2.0 generation code and additional components coming soon
The framework follows a four-stage pipeline: HY-Pano 2.0 for panorama generation, WorldNav for trajectory planning, WorldStereo 2.0 for world expansion, and WorldMirror 2.0 for world composition
WorldMirror 2.0 can reconstruct 3D scenes from multi-view images or videos in a single forward pass, predicting depth, surface normals, camera parameters, and 3DGS attributes simultaneously
The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks like Tanks-and-Temples, MipNeRF360, and various 3D reconstruction datasets
HY-World 2.0 supports interactive character exploration with physics-based collision, allowing users to freely navigate AI-generated environments
The project is fully open-source with all model weights, code, and technical details being released to facilitate research reproducibility
The framework represents a paradigm shift from 'watching a movie' to 'building a playable game' by creating persistent, explorable 3D environments rather than temporary video content
