## Key Points

Nvidia is preparing for Computex 2026 where they'll launch their groundbreaking N1X laptop APU, packing 20 ARM cores and 6,144 CUDA cores with capabilities equivalent to an RTX 5070 GPU
The N1X chip features a 256-bit LPDDR5X bus for unified memory sharing, enabling massive VRAM allocation for running 100B+ parameter LLMs locally
Dell, Lenovo, and ASUS have all leaked or hinted at N1X laptop models, with pricing expected to exceed $3,000 for 128GB configurations
Nvidia will showcase its complete "Vera Rubin" AI factory platform at Computex, focusing on ecosystem development, supply chains, and partnership announcements
Physical and Agentic AI will be a major focus, with Nvidia highlighting Edge AI platforms like Jetson Thor for robotics and autonomous machine applications
Gaming will take a backseat at Computex 2026, with Nvidia having rolled gaming into "Edge Computing" reporting and no major gaming hardware launches expected beyond N1X
The article suggests there's controversy around DLSS 5 and that Blackwell's Super refresh has been delayed due to a RAM crisis
The launch is framed as a much-needed injection of excitement into the tech space after disappointment with CES 2026
