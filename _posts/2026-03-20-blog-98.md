## Key Points

- MoDA is a new attention mechanism that allows each attention head to attend to both sequence KV pairs at the current layer AND depth KV pairs from preceding layers
- It addresses signal degradation in deep LLMs where informative features from shallow layers get diluted by repeated residual updates
- The researchers developed a hardware-efficient algorithm that achieves 97.3% of FlashAttention-2's efficiency at 64K sequence length
- Experiments on 1.5B-parameter models show MoDA improves average perplexity by 0.2 across 10 validation benchmarks and increases downstream task performance by 2.11%
- The computational overhead is minimal at only 3.7% additional FLOPs
- The paper introduces three different Triton kernels: standard MoDA, Chunk-Visible MoDA, and Non-Causal MoDA kernels
- MoDA works particularly well with post-normalization rather than pre-normalization
- The implementation requires PyTorch >= 2.5, Triton >= 3.0, and other dependencies
- Code will be released soon, but the paper is already available on arXiv (arXiv:2603.15619)
