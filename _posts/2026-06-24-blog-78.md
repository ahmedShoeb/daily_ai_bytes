## Key Points

- The article presents a speculative thought experiment called 'Europe 2031' that warns Europe is sleepwalking into an AI disaster
- By 2031, Europe's economy is depicted as a shambles because it failed to invest in AI datacentres while the US and China forged ahead
- The scenario suggests the US monopolizes 70% of global computing power while European companies fail to adopt AI technologies
- Europe's one bargaining chip - the Dutch lithography firm ASML - is shown to be insufficient against US and Chinese AI dominance
- The authors argue Europe needs to dramatically increase datacentre construction and deregulate AI zones to remain competitive
- The piece gained traction among EU policymakers after the Trump administration blocked foreign nationals from using Anthropic's Fable AI model
- Skeptics note that many AI investment deals mentioned in the scenario have already collapsed or face uncertainty
- EU politicians warn about buying into a narrative that simply building more datacentres will solve Europe's AI competitiveness issues
- The scenario highlights concerns about Europe's technological sovereignty and dependence on foreign AI infrastructure
- The piece represents a growing genre of fictional AI doomsday scenarios that are influencing policy discussions
