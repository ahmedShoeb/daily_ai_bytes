## Key Points

Google will pay SpaceX $920 million per month under a cloud service agreement for AI compute capacity, signed on June 5
The deal covers access to approximately 110,000 NVIDIA GPUs plus related CPUs, memory, and systems from October 2026 through June 2029
SpaceX must deliver the committed GPU access by September 30, 2026, or Google can terminate after a one-month grace period or accept reduced capacity at a lower fee
Google framed the purchase as short-term bridge capacity for stronger-than-expected demand for Gemini Enterprise, its business AI platform
The contract allows Google to retain ownership of all its AI models, content, and related data while using SpaceX's infrastructure
After December 31, 2026, either side can end the arrangement with 90 days' notice, providing flexibility
Google plans massive infrastructure spending ($180-190 billion in 2026 capital expenditures), with this SpaceX deal as bridge capacity
SpaceX acquired xAI in February, gaining AI infrastructure that now serves both Google and Anthropic (which signed a separate contract in May)
The deal strengthens SpaceX's investor story ahead of a potential high-valuation IPO, targeting over $1.75 trillion valuation
Contract terms tie payments to actual hardware delivery rather than guaranteed revenue, keeping SpaceX accountable for infrastructure delivery
