## Key Points

AI agent adoption is rapidly escalating with 43% of organizations reporting more than half of their employees using AI agents daily
Shadow AI agents are appearing far sooner than expected - 54% report 1-100 unsanctioned AI agents in their environment
Critical governance gaps exist with only 15% of organizations having defined ownership for most AI agents
Scope violations pose a constant operational threat - only 8% of organizations say their agents never exceed intended permissions
Detection and response to AI agent incidents can take 5 hours or more for 58% of organizations, dangerously extending exposure
Most organizations are juggling four or more agentic platforms, intensifying the challenge of enforcing consistent policy
Only 31% of organizations have formally adopted AI agent governance policies
Current security tools leave organizations reactive rather than proactive with AI agent risk
Organizations are relying on compliance frameworks built for static software (like HIPAA and NIST AI RMF) rather than autonomous systems operating at scale
AI agent risk is no longer theoretical and requires foundational controls to govern autonomous systems at scale
