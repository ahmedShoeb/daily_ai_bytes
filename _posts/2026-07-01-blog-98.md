## Key Points

- OpenAI is launching GPT-5.6 family with three models: Sol (flagship), Terra (lower-cost), and Luna (fastest/most cost-efficient)
- Models will launch with limited preview for trusted partners before broader availability in coming weeks
- All three models are classified as High capability in Cybersecurity and Biological/Chemical risk under OpenAI's Preparedness Framework
- The models show meaningful improvements in cybersecurity capability but don't reach the highest Critical risk level
- GPT-5.6 can find vulnerabilities but cannot autonomously execute end-to-end attacks against hardened targets
- New safety technology includes: training for safety, activation classifiers that intervene during generation, real-time scanning of conversations, and automated safety systems
- Over 700,000 A100e GPU hours have been dedicated to finding universal jailbreaks, with continuous automated red teaming during deployment
- OpenAI plans to reserve the most sensitive cybersecurity and biological capabilities for trusted defenders even after broad public release
- Testing suggests GPT-5.6 is better at finding/fixing vulnerabilities than exploiting them, giving defenders an opportunity to harden systems
