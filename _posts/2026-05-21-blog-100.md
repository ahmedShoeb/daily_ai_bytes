## Key Points

Language models don't gradually mature from 'parrots' to 'intelligence' - instead they frequently and suddenly hop between these modes throughout pre-training
Researchers found models can suddenly switch from using in-context learning to memorized patterns, System 1 to System 2 thinking, or what 'sounds true' vs what's actually true
This 'mode-hopping' occurs across models of different sizes and persists even with 9× to 90× Chinchilla-optimal training budgets
Mode-hopping is locally stable - single optimization steps don't fix it, and checkpoint averaging only mitigates but doesn't resolve the issue
The behavior is interpreted as a capacity allocation problem: generalizable circuits compete with shallow patterns learned early in training
Researchers built a toy evaluation suite to track these generalization dynamics using six specific behavioral tests
The insights enabled selection of intermediate pre-training checkpoints that generalize better than final checkpoints for reasoning and alignment
Pre-training data subsets can be selected to control and stabilize generalization dynamics in specific directions
Existing 'simpler solutions generalize better' metrics were tested and showed nuanced results - generalizable solutions can be either simple or complex
The research challenges monolithic beliefs about generalization and suggests pre-training dynamics are more complex than simple phase-transition models
