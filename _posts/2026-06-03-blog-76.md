## Key Points

- The main bottleneck for enterprise AI agents isn't model performance but permissioning and security models
- Workday's Sana platform uses their existing system of record as the governance layer for AI agents
- Accuracy is critical for HR and finance applications where 'almost right is not acceptable'
- Workday addresses accuracy by using Gemini as the base reasoning layer and adding verification models that 'interrogate' outputs before execution
- Sana agents only act on behalf of authenticated users within their specific permissions
- The system maintains audit trails where interaction logs stay with Gemini while main audits remain within Workday
- Experts emphasize that permissions must live in the system of record where the data resides
- Workday expanded its partnership with Google to bring Sana to Gemini Enterprise
