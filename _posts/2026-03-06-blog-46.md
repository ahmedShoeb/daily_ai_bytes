## Key Points

- Junyang Lin, the tech lead of Alibaba's Qwen AI team, announced his departure on X with a brief post: 'me stepping down. bye my beloved qwen.'
- Lin was the public face and main communicator for the Qwen project on social media, regularly announcing releases and engaging with developers worldwide.
- Two other Qwen researchers, Kaixin Li and Binyuan Hui, also appear to have left Alibaba around the same time.
- Qwen contributor Chen Chang hinted that Lin's departure wasn't his choice, saying 'I know leaving wasn't your choice' in response to the news.
- Lin had been with Alibaba since 2019, rising to become the formal tech lead of the Qwen team from 2023.
- Before leading Qwen, Lin worked on influential Alibaba projects including M6, OFA, and Chinese-CLIP, amassing over 42,000 citations on Google Scholar.
- Under Lin's leadership, Qwen grew into one of the world's most impactful open-source AI projects with over 600 million downloads and 170,000+ derivative models on Hugging Face.
- Lin was known for candidly discussing China's AI constraints, noting US compute infrastructure exceeds China's by one to two orders of magnitude at a Tsinghua University summit.
- The departure comes just weeks after the release of Qwen3.5, though Alibaba hasn't announced who will take over Lin's public-facing role.
- Lin's next move is unclear but expected to attract significant attention given his visibility in both Chinese and international AI communities.
