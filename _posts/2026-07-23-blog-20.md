## Key Points

- Z.AI (formerly Zhipu) has completed construction of a massive 1-gigawatt data center powered entirely by Chinese-made chips
- The facility has started partial operations and provides computing capacity equivalent to powering 750,000 homes at any given moment
- The project is a milestone in China's effort to reduce reliance on restricted NVIDIA chips
- Z.AI has built or operated several computing clusters with more than 10,000 chips each as competition among Chinese AI developers intensifies
- The data center places Z.AI among China's largest AI lab facilities, though still behind giants like Alibaba and China Telecom
- Huawei, Cambricon Technologies, and Alibaba are competing as local chip suppliers to narrow the performance gap with NVIDIA
- Z.AI's expansion comes amid growing competition with Moonshot, which recently suspended new subscriptions due to infrastructure constraints
- China plans to spend around $295 billion over the next five years on data centers
- Z.AI has raised billions through a Hong Kong IPO and share sale
- The company is on track to generate $1 billion in annual recurring revenue and the data center will help strengthen its position as an AI services provider to businesses
