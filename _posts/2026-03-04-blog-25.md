## Key Points

- The article presents a future scenario where major AI labs rapidly become defense contractors, similar to how aerospace companies like Lockheed Martin evolved over 50 years, but AI labs will do it faster
- In a hypothetical February 27, 2026 timeline: President Trump orders federal agencies to cease using Anthropic's technology after they refuse to remove safety constraints for Pentagon deployment, while OpenAI signs a deal to deploy models on the Pentagon's classified network
- The FY2026 defense budget request includes $13.4 billion for AI and autonomous systems - a 7x increase from FY2025 and larger than most AI companies' revenue streams
- Defense contracts offer predictable, multi-year revenue that's politically protected, which is crucial for AI companies burning billions annually without clear profitability paths
- Security clearances create significant moats: processing takes 243+ days, only 4.2 million Americans hold active clearances, and cleared professionals command high salaries ($119K-$141K average)
- Palantir serves as a model: government as client funds and validates technology, commercial clients justify valuation; Palantir's government revenue peaked at 58.2% and they secured a $10 billion Army contract
- OpenAI is already moving in this direction with a classified Pentagon deal, appointing former NSA director to its board, and hiring Palantir's former CISO
- Major AI labs are all becoming less open: OpenAI has moved from open-source GPT-1 to closed GPT-4, Google removed weapons prohibitions from principles, Meta opened Llama to defense contractors
- Defense R&D historically generated civilian spillovers (ARPANET, GPS), but classified military research often doesn't flow back to commercial applications quickly
