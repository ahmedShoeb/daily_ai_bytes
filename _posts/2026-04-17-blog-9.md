## Key Points

- Hiro, an AI personal CFO company founded by Ethan Bloch, is being acquired by OpenAI.
- The company helped clients plan and manage over $1 billion in assets since its founding.
- Starting immediately, Hiro is no longer accepting new signups.
- The Hiro product will completely stop functioning on April 20, 2026.
- All user data will be deleted from servers on May 13, 2026, though existing users can export their data until that date.
- The acquisition will allow the Hiro team to pursue their vision of personalized financial guidance at a much larger scale through OpenAI.
- Ethan Bloch mentioned that personalized financial guidance has historically been too expensive, generic, or hard to access, but believes ChatGPT is finally changing that.
- The announcement includes appreciation for Hiro's users, team, investors (Ribbit, General Catalyst, Restive Ventures), and supporters.
- This is Ethan Bloch's second successful company acquisition, having previously founded Digit which was also acquired.
- The move reflects OpenAI's continued expansion into financial technology applications.
