## Key Points

- DeepSeek has released their V4 series with two preview models: DeepSeek-V4-Pro and DeepSeek-V4-Flash
- Both models feature 1 million token context windows and use Mixture of Experts architecture
- DeepSeek-V4-Pro has 1.6 trillion total parameters with 49 billion active, becoming the new largest open weights model
- DeepSeek-V4-Flash has 284 billion total parameters with 13 billion active and uses the MIT license
- The models are significantly more efficient than previous versions, with V4-Pro using only 27% of FLOPs and 10% of KV cache size compared to V3.2
- V4-Flash is even more efficient, using only 10% of FLOPs and 7% of KV cache size relative to V3.2
- DeepSeek offers extremely competitive pricing: $0.14/million tokens input and $0.28 output for Flash, $1.74 input and $3.48 output for Pro
- DeepSeek-V4-Flash is the cheapest among small models, beating even OpenAI's GPT-5.4 Nano
- According to DeepSeek's benchmarks, V4-Pro performs competitively with frontier models but trails state-of-the-art models by 3-6 months
- The Pro model is 865GB on Hugging Face while Flash is 160GB, potentially making Flash suitable for local deployment on high-spec machines
