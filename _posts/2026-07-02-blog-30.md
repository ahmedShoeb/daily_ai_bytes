## Key Points

- DiScoFormer is a new transformer model that estimates both density and score of distributions in a single forward pass without retraining
- Density and score are fundamental quantities: density measures where data points cluster (like a smooth histogram), score shows direction of fastest density increase
- Traditional methods like kernel density estimation (KDE) are generalizable but inaccurate in high dimensions, while neural models need retraining for each new distribution
- DiScoFormer uses transformer architecture with cross-attention, allowing it to evaluate density/score at any point, not just where data exists
- The model has a shared backbone with two output heads (one for density, one for score) that leverage their mathematical relationship for consistency
- DiScoFormer was trained on Gaussian Mixture Models because they're universal approximators with closed-form densities/scores
- In 100 dimensions, DiScoFormer outperforms KDE dramatically: 6.5x lower score error and 37x lower density error
- The model stays accurate outside its training data, handling distributions with more modes and non-Gaussian shapes
- DiScoFormer's key advantage is that score estimation is used across many fields (generative modeling, Bayesian inference, scientific computing)
- This pretrained model could serve as a plug-in estimator, eliminating the need to retrain for each new problem
