## Key Points

- GLM-5.2 is Z.ai's latest flagship model designed specifically for long-horizon tasks with a substantial leap in capability over GLM-5.1
- The model delivers robust 1M-token context support, making it the first to provide this capability for practical engineering work across large-scale implementation, research, optimization, and debugging
- GLM-5.2 ranks as the highest-performing open-source model on three key long-horizon coding benchmarks: FrontierSWE, PostTrainBench, and SWE-Marathon
- On FrontierSWE (open-ended technical projects taking hours to tens of hours), GLM-5.2 trails Claude Opus 4.8 by only 1% while edging out GPT-5.5 by 1%
- The model introduces 'effort level control' allowing users to balance capability against execution speed and computational costs, with capability roughly between Claude Opus 4.7 and 4.8 under similar token consumption
- Technical innovations include IndexShare architecture (every 4 transformer layers share a lightweight indexer) and optimized speculative decoding with MTP layers
- The model achieves significant improvements over GLM-5.1 on standard coding benchmarks: 81.0 vs. 63.5 on Terminal-Bench 2.1 and 62.1 vs. 58.4 on SWE-bench Pro
- To prevent reward hacking in coding agents, GLM-5.2 implements an anti-hack module with rule-based filtering and LLM judges to block malicious actions
- The model is available now for GLM Coding Plan subscribers with variable quota consumption (3× peak, 2× off-peak, currently 1× off-peak through September promotion)
- Model weights are publicly available on HuggingFace and ModelScope, supporting multiple inference frameworks including transformers, vLLM, and SGLang
