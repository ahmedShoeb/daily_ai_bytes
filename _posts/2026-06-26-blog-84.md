## Key Points

- Gray Swan is an AI security startup founded by Zico Kolter and Matt Fredrikson focused on making AI systems safe and secure for enterprise deployment
- AI security is fundamentally different from traditional cybersecurity because AI systems have inherent vulnerabilities and can be tricked in ways humans can be tricked
- Frontier models don't automatically become safer as they scale - safety doesn't track with capabilities and requires explicit training
- Gray Swan operates three main products: Gray Swan Arena (community red-teaming platform), Shade (automated red-teaming AI), and Cygnal (guardrail model for policy enforcement)
- Shade, their automated red-teaming AI, can now outperform human red-teamers at breaking AI models in certain scenarios
- The 'lethal trifecta' for AI security consists of: ingesting untrusted data, having access to private information, and having exfiltration capability
- Enterprises often need guardrails when they deploy agents with computer-use capabilities like Codex or Claude Code, especially when these agents can access sensitive data
- AI security is likely to become integrated with insurance and compliance frameworks, similar to how cybersecurity evolved
- The first major public prompt-injection breach is seen as inevitable and would be a 'gray swan' event - unlikely but clearly visible before it happens
