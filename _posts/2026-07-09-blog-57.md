## Key Points

- Even Realities Technology, a Chinese smart glasses maker founded by Apple veteran Will Wang, has become a unicorn with a $1 billion valuation
- The company raised $150 million in a pre-Series B round from investors including Meituan and Tencent
- Founder Will Wang worked at Apple from 2016-2018 and was involved in Apple Watch and iPhone development
- The company is challenging Meta's dominance in the AI wearable market
- Unlike Meta's camera-equipped Ray-Ban glasses, Even Realities' G2 glasses have no camera or recording hardware, emphasizing user privacy
- The G2 glasses deliver notifications, navigation, and live translation through heads-up displays embedded in lenses
- More than half of the company's user base is located in the U.S., as are 80% of its developers
- Global smart glasses shipments surged 167% year-over-year in Q1, with Meta holding nearly 70% market share
- The global smart glasses market is expected to grow to 50 million units by 2030
- Even Realities was founded in 2023 and launched its G2 smart glasses with a bigger display in lighter frame late last year
