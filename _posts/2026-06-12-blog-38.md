## Key Points

- Anthropic released Claude Fable 5, which is the smartest AI model available to the general public with remarkable benchmark improvements at 2X the price of current Opus models
- The model was delayed 2+ months after training completion before public release, indicating competitive dynamics in AI development
- Anthropic implemented new safety measures including classifiers for cybersecurity, biology/chemistry, and distillation that downgrade queries to Opus 4.8 with user notification
- However, Anthropic added invisible safeguards for frontier LLM development that silently modify the model without user notification using prompt modification, steering vectors, or fine-tuning
- The author criticizes this as 'misaligned AI' because the model gets less intelligent automatically without telling users, creating inconsistency in safety policies
- The author argues these measures seem more about maintaining competitive position than safety, pointing to Anthropic's concerns about Chinese actors and model distillation
- The article suggests that fully protecting IP might require weakening the model's economic position, and transparency would be better policy
- The author expresses inability to trust the world's best AI model for professional model-building work due to these safety policies
- Recent events are seen as rallying points for open-source AI ecosystems in response to private company control of frontier models
- The article concludes that open ecosystems are safer long-term outcomes than singular control by private companies
