## Key Points

- Tuna-2 is an AI model architecture from Meta that simplifies vision encoding by using direct pixel embeddings instead of traditional vision encoders like VAEs
- The model builds on the original Tuna framework, with Tuna-R as an intermediate version that removes the VAE but keeps a representation encoder
- Tuna-2 completely bypasses representation encoders entirely, using patch embedding layers directly on raw image inputs
- The pixel embedding approach outperforms both Tuna-R and original Tuna across multiple multimodal benchmarks
- The repository provides different model variants including Tuna-2 (pixel), Tuna-R (no VAE, with encoder), and Tuna (VAE latent)
- Supports various inference tasks like text-to-image and image editing with flexible size and resolution options
- Due to policy constraints, only partial model weights are released with some layers removed from LLM backbone and diffusion head
- The project includes video generation training and inference code even though complete video models can't be released
- Researchers can fine-tune the released checkpoint on external data to restore the missing layers and achieve full model quality
- The work is presented in a 2026 arXiv preprint and will appear at CVPR 2026
