## Key Points

Character AI is testing games within its platform to boost user engagement.
Initially, two games – Speakeasy and War of Words – are available to paid subscribers and a limited number of free users.
Games are played *with* existing characters, and a new chat is initiated to keep conversations separate.
Speakeasy challenges users to get the chatbot to say a specific word without using certain restricted words.
War of Words is a dueling game with an AI referee.
Character AI is actively positioning itself as an ‘AI entertainment company’.
The company has undergone leadership changes recently, including the departure of co-founders.
The move to include games mirrors strategies used by other platforms like YouTube and Netflix to increase time spent on their services.
Character AI has also been focusing on safety tools, particularly for teen users, following recent lawsuits.

## Key Topics Discussed

Alright folks, let's talk about Character AI! This cool startup, known for letting you chat with AI characters, is now dipping its toes into gaming. They’re rolling out two initial games, 'Speakeasy' and 'War of Words', right within their existing platform. Think of it as adding another layer of fun to your AI interactions. These games aren’t for everyone just yet – they’re prioritizing their paid subscribers and giving a limited preview to free users. What's neat is you don’t disrupt your ongoing conversations. When you want to play, it starts a *new* chat, so your backstory with the character remains intact. 

'Speakeasy' is a word game where you try to get the AI to say a specific word while avoiding certain others. 'War of Words' is more of a duel, and an AI acts as the referee. Character AI is really leaning into being an 'AI entertainment company' and sees this as a key way to achieve that. They've been through some changes at the top recently, but the vision remains. It’s a smart move, considering platforms like YouTube and Netflix have successfully used games to keep users hooked. In fact, Character AI users already spend a whopping 98 minutes a day on the app! And speaking of user experience, they've also been beefing up safety features, especially for younger users, after some previous legal challenges. So, it looks like Character AI is not only trying to entertain but also to do so responsibly.
