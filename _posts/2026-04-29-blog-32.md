## Key Points

OpenAI published a five-principle framework on Sunday outlining its approach to artificial general intelligence development
The five principles are: democratization, empowerment, universal prosperity, resilience, and adaptability
CEO Sam Altman concedes OpenAI is now materially larger than at the time of the 2018 Charter and may need to trade off some empowerment for more resilience
The framework represents OpenAI's most prominent statement of intent since the 2018 Charter
Democratization principle commits to keeping AI decisions subject to democratic processes rather than leaving them solely to AI labs
Universal prosperity principle underpins OpenAI's compute build-out, vertical integration, and worldwide data-center push
Resilience principle pledges collaboration with companies, governments, and ecosystems on bioweapon and cyber risks
The framework arrives as U.S. and European regulators tighten oversight of frontier AI labs
Publication comes after months of debate over OpenAI's Pentagon contract and surveillance concerns
Altman acknowledges OpenAI 'deserves an enormous amount of scrutiny given the weight of what we are doing'
