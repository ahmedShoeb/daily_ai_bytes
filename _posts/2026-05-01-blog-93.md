## Key Points

- Poolside AI has released two new models in the Laguna family: Laguna M.1 (225B-A23B) and Laguna XS.2 (33B-A3B)
- Laguna M.1 is their most capable model to date - a 225B total parameter Mixture of Experts model with 23B activated parameters, trained on 30T tokens using 6,144 NVIDIA Hopper GPUs
- Laguna XS.2 is their first open-weight model available under Apache 2.0 license, with 33B total parameters and 3B activated parameters
- Both models are 'agentic coding models' designed for long-horizon work and software creation tasks
- Laguna M.1 achieves 46.9% on SWE-bench Pro and 40.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0
- Laguna XS.2 achieves 44.5% on SWE-bench Pro and 30.1% on Terminal-Bench 2.0
- The company has also released their agent harness (pool) which is the same environment they use internally for agent RL training and evaluation
- Models were trained using the Muon optimizer and feature advanced synthetic data generation (4.4T+ synthetic tokens)
- Both models are free to use for a limited time via API and on OpenRouter
- The company emphasizes their focus on enabling agents that can write and execute code as a more expressive interface than traditional tool-calling approaches
