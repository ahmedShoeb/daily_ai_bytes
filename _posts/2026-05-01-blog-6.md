## Key Points

- The article explores whether traditional search APIs could be replaced by AI agents that understand user requests and retrieve information directly
- Experimental results show agents with basic search tools (BM25 and embedding models) outperform traditional search APIs significantly
- On Amazon ESCI dataset, agents achieved NDCG scores up to 0.453 compared to baseline scores of 0.289/0.314 for traditional approaches
- Agents typically issue search queries only once or twice, but can intelligently refine searches when results are unsatisfactory
- Keyword search seems to naturally encourage agents to try more queries and refine their search strategies
- Small improvements were seen when requiring agents to make more tool calls (4+) and use diverse retrieval queries
- Specialized agentic search models like SID-1 are emerging that are specifically optimized for retrieval tasks
- The approach works best for 'finding things' (products, jobs) but less so for 'deep research' where the agent lacks knowledge
- The article suggests a potential future where search is built with agents rather than traditional APIs
