## Key Points

- London-based AI lab Inherent raised $50 million in seed funding from Index Ventures, Radical Ventures, and Nvidia's NVentures
- The company is building Faraday, a platform that pairs human researchers with self-improving AI agents for scientific discovery
- Inherent aims to create 'AI-native science' that can identify which scientific questions are worth asking, not just answer existing questions
- The founding team comes from DeepMind, Microsoft, Reka AI, and includes former White House AI policy staffers
- Company is structured as a public benefit corporation, which is unusual for venture-backed AI labs
- Matt Clifford, former UK AI tsar and co-founder of Entrepreneurs First, serves as an advisor to Inherent
- The platform is designed for open-ended scientific exploration that could lead to discoveries human researchers might not find alone
- The investment represents one of Europe's largest AI stealth-to-launch rounds in 2026
- Team combines DeepMind research credentials with White House policy experience for unique positioning
- The goal is to enable exceptional scientific outcomes through a new paradigm of human-AI collaboration
