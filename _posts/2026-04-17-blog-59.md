## Key Points

Google is preparing a major expansion of NotebookLM with a new Canvas feature that adds visual and interactive layers on top of source material
The Canvas feature could transform source content into interactive timelines, web pages, games, or visualizers for guided exploration
A new Connectors option suggests NotebookLM will integrate with other Google services, moving beyond manual file uploads
This shift positions NotebookLM as a central research layer across Google's ecosystem rather than a standalone notebook tool
Source organization improvements include labeling capabilities and an Auto Label feature for better categorization of large source libraries
These changes align with Google's strategy of grounding AI tools in user-provided context and workspace data
The updates build on recent notebook customization features like custom banners and editable summaries rolled out in March
These developments signal Google's evolution of NotebookLM from a research assistant to a workspace for building structured, visual experiences
