## Key Points

- IBM Research discovered that model routing systems that treat model selection as a classification problem are fundamentally flawed
- Cost is more than just model pricing - caching effects can dramatically change which model is cheaper to run, as shown with Claude Sonnet beating GPT-4.1 on cost despite higher base pricing
- Complexity involves more than task difficulty - invisible task complexity, compliance requirements, and governance constraints force routers to juggle multiple optimization dimensions simultaneously
- Latency is more than model speed - routing overhead, infrastructure conditions, and granularity of routing decisions all impact the end-to-end user experience
- The solution was to treat routing as an optimization problem rather than classification, optimizing cost, quality, and latency simultaneously
- Their optimization-based router created a cost-accuracy frontier with multiple operating points, achieving a 21% cost reduction and 9% latency reduction compared to using Opus alone
- The key insight: routing is about optimizing entire systems, not just choosing the 'best' model for a given task
- Model routing involves balancing caching behavior, infrastructure state, compliance constraints, and workload patterns alongside model selection
