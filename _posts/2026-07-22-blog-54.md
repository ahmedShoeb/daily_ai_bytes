## Key Points

- Apple has sent legal preservation letters to about 40 former Apple employees now working at OpenAI
- This follows Apple's trade secret lawsuit against OpenAI filed last week
- Apple alleges OpenAI recruited key Apple engineers and benefited from proprietary designs, manufacturing processes, and trade secrets
- Among the former employees targeted are Tang Tan (OpenAI's Chief Hardware Officer and 24-year Apple veteran) and Chang Liu (on OpenAI's hardware team)
- Apple claims more than 400 former Apple employees now work at OpenAI
- OpenAI has denied the allegations, stating it's 'not aware of any evidence that this complaint has merit'
- Apple believes the alleged misconduct may be broader than isolated actions by individual employees
- Apple is seeking an injunction to stop OpenAI from using Apple information in AI hardware development, plus damages
- The company believes current evidence may only be the 'tip of the iceberg'
- Apple is suing key former employees for breach of contract for violating employment agreements
