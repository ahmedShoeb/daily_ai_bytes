## Key Points

- AI models can now analyze social media burner accounts to identify pseudonymous users across platforms with surprisingly high accuracy
- Researchers achieved recall rates as high as 68% and precision rates up to 90% in deanonymization experiments
- This technology threatens pseudonymity, a common privacy measure used by many for sensitive discussions
- The findings invalidate the assumption that targeted deanonymization requires extensive manual effort
- Researchers used datasets from Hacker News, LinkedIn, Netflix, and Reddit to test their techniques
- The ability to cheaply identify people behind pseudonymous accounts opens them to doxxing, stalking, and detailed marketing profiling
- Researchers collected data from public social media sites while preserving speaker privacy by stripping identifying references
- The Netflix prize attack technique was used to identify users from preferences and transaction records
- LLMs make it possible to cheaply and quickly deanonymize users who previously relied on pseudonymity for protection
