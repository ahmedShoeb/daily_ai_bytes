## Key Points

A California jury took less than two hours to dismiss all claims in Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI CEO Sam Altman and co-founder Greg Brockman
The jury unanimously found Musk waited beyond the statute of limitations (over 3 years) to file his 2024 lawsuit
Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers agreed with the jury's decision and tossed out the case
Musk had accused Altman and Brockman of 'breach of charitable trust' by allegedly abandoning OpenAI's founding nonprofit mission and profiting from the decision
OpenAI's lawyer called the lawsuit 'a hypocritical attempt to sabotage a competitor' given Musk launched his own for-profit AI company (xAI) 18 months before suing
The case revealed OpenAI was founded as a nonprofit in 2015 but created a for-profit arm in 2017 to raise funds and attract researchers, which Musk opposed
Musk left OpenAI's board in 2018 after disagreeing about control of the company
Musk also sued Microsoft for its $13 billion investments in OpenAI, but that claim was also dismissed
If Musk had won, OpenAI and Microsoft could have faced up to $150 billion in damages and Altman/Brockman could have been dismissed
Musk's attorney indicated they plan to appeal the decision, arguing the case was decided on 'a calendar technicality' rather than the merits
