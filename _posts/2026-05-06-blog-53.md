## Key Points

- **Synthetic Computers at Scale** is a methodology for creating realistic computer environments with folder hierarchies and content-rich artifacts (documents, spreadsheets, presentations)
- The approach enables **long-horizon productivity simulations** where one agent creates productivity objectives and another agent acts as the user to complete them over extended periods
- Simulations require **over 8 hours of agent runtime** and average **more than 2,000 turns**, simulating about a month of human work productivity
- Initial experiments created **1,000 synthetic computers** and produced **rich experiential learning signals** validated by significant agent performance improvements
- The methodology can theoretically **scale to millions or billions of synthetic user worlds**, covering diverse professions, roles, and contexts with sufficient compute
- Applications include providing a **foundational substrate for agent self-improvement** and **agentic reinforcement learning** in productivity scenarios
- Realistic productivity work depends heavily on user-specific computer environments storing work context through directory structures and professional artifacts
- Agents navigate file systems for grounding, coordinate with simulated collaborators, and produce professional deliverables during simulations
