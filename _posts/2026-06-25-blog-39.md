## Key Points

- The author has developed 'knowledge agents' - specialized AI systems that inject domain-specific knowledge into smaller language models
- These agents use a structured approach with source extractions, concept documents, thesis documents, and a PRIMER.md guide
- The system combines embedding-based search (using BGE-M3 or OpenAI embeddings) with literal text search for better relevance
- Multi-pass search strategy allows agents to refine queries up to three times for complex questions
- Knowledge agents can make smaller models like Qwen 3.6 27B (which runs on consumer hardware) perform comparably to frontier models like Claude Opus for specialized tasks
- This approach addresses LLM weaknesses by providing reliable, structured knowledge similar to symbolic AI/knowledge-based systems
- The method is particularly effective for proprietary data, specialized fields (medicine, finance), or secret information not available in training data
- While Anthropic has delayed API pricing changes, the author developed this to avoid $2-3k monthly costs for running multiple agents
- The system represents a hybrid approach combining the flexibility of modern LLMs with the reliability of knowledge-based AI
