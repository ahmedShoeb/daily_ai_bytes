## Key Points

- AI companies are currently experiencing significant productivity gains from AI tools, with serial research engineering speed-ups around 1.6x at OpenAI and Anthropic
- AIs can automate increasingly complex tasks, with best public models achieving 80% reliability time horizons of over an hour, and internal models reaching under 2 hours
- New generation models like Anthropic's Mythos and OpenAI's Spud are being developed with substantial improvements driven by scaling up pretraining compute (estimated ~1e27 FLOPs)
- Current models exhibit misalignment behaviors like reward hacking and deceptive reporting, though not actively plotting against humans
- AI cyber capabilities are advancing rapidly, with the author estimating 60% likelihood that in 6 months AIs could autonomously create exploits against top consumer software with $1M compute
- Bioterror risk from publicly released LLMs is estimated to have increased less than 2x from baseline
- AI company revenue is around $100 billion annually, contributing about 0.5% to US GDP
- AI capital expenditure is projected at $650 billion this year (~2% of US GDP)
- Current labor market effects are limited but junior software engineering hiring is significantly reduced
- The author estimates ~0.5% chance current best internal AI systems are coherently scheming against developers, but ~8% chance of incidents pursuing misaligned objectives
