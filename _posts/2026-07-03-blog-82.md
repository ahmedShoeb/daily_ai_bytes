## Key Points

- Etched, an AI chip startup competing with Nvidia, has reached a $5 billion valuation and booked $1 billion in contract orders
- The company has raised $800 million total, including a $500 million round in December at the $5B valuation
- Etched's chips are being manufactured by TSMC and are currently being tested with customers as 'frontier inference clusters'
- The startup was founded in 2022 by Harvard dropouts Gavin Uberti and Robert Wachen who became Thiel fellows
- Early on, Etched struggled to get investor interest and operated month-to-month despite having a 30-page memo about AI needing specialized chips
- Investors now include VentureTech Alliance, Jane Street, Hudson River Trading, Two Sigma, Ribbit Capital, Stripes, and AI heavyweights like Andrej Karpathy and Geoffrey Hinton
- The company focuses on solving AI inference bottlenecks, which is currently the biggest cost center for AI companies trying to serve customers at scale
- Etched's announcement comes amid a hot AI chip market with competitors like Cerebras (which recently IPO'd) and Groq (raising $650M)
- Major hyperscalers like Amazon, Google, and Microsoft are building their own in-house AI chips, and OpenAI just announced its first custom chip
