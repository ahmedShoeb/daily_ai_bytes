## Key Points

a blogging competition received over 600 essay submissions answering big questions about AI
First place went to Jassi Pannu, who argued for using AI Foundation resources to end airborne transmission through physical infrastructure like far-UVC lamps
Second place winner Ege Erdil advised countries outside AI supply chain to focus on stable policies, capital formation, and getting out of the way of AI automation
Third place winner Michael Li drew analogy between AI labs and Hong Kong's MTR system, suggesting labs should capture value through complementary assets rather than just API fees
The competition explored practical approaches to AI governance and deployment rather than theoretical scenarios
The blog post includes full text of all three winning essays with author backgrounds and reasoning
