## Key Points

- Meta's new Applied AI team, created three months ago, is facing internal revolt with engineers describing it as a 'soul-crushing gulag'
- The unit comprises roughly 6,500 engineers and product managers drafted to support Meta's AI research ambitions
- Employees were moved into the group through surprise emails with no choice but to join or quit, leading many to call themselves 'draftees'
- Their assigned work involves generating puzzles and coding problems to train AI models, which many find tedious and demeaning
- The drama escalated when someone hijacked a livestreamed employee presentation with an expletive-laden meltdown targeting a senior Meta AI executive
- Mark Zuckerberg drafted internal employees rather than contractors, believing Meta employees have 'significantly higher' intelligence than third-party contractors
- Over 1,600 Meta employees company-wide have signed a petition protesting a program that monitors their clicks and keystrokes for AI training data
- Meta's chief product officer Chris Cox acknowledged the 'brutal' environment on a call with employees
- Mark Zuckerberg addressed the situation in an internal memo, admitting recent changes had 'caused distress' and that the company made mistakes
- The Applied AI team is led by Maher Saba, a 12-year Meta veteran who previously worked in the $83 billion Reality Labs division
