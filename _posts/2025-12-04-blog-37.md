## Key Points

- John Giannandrea, Apple's AI chief, is stepping down from his position.
- This change occurs as Apple faces challenges with its AI-powered Siri, including a delayed launch of a more personalized version.
- Amar Subramanya, who previously held AI leadership roles at Microsoft and Google, is replacing Giannandrea as Apple’s vice president of AI.
- Subramanya will be responsible for overseeing the development of Apple’s AI models, machine learning research, and AI safety.
- Giannandrea will transition to an advisory role before his retirement in spring 2026.
- Apple anticipates launching its upgraded Siri next spring, with reports suggesting it might be powered by a custom version of Google’s Gemini AI model.

## Key Topics Discussed

Hey everyone, we've got some big news coming out of Apple today regarding their AI division. John Giannandrea, who has been leading Apple's AI efforts, is stepping down from his role. This move comes at a time when Apple has been grappling with some significant setbacks concerning their AI-powered assistant, Siri, including a delayed launch of a more personalized version that was pushed back earlier this year. It seems there's been some internal restructuring to get their AI strategy back on track. 

Taking over from Giannandrea is Amar Subramanya, a seasoned professional with a strong background in AI from both Google and Microsoft. Subramanya will be stepping in as Apple’s vice president of AI, and he'll be overseeing the development of Apple’s AI models, their machine learning research, and crucially, AI safety. This is a pretty significant shift, as Subramanya's experience in integrating AI research into products is highlighted as being vital for Apple's future innovations, especially with upcoming Apple Intelligence features. 

While Giannandrea is stepping down from his leadership position, he won't be leaving Apple immediately; he'll continue to serve as an advisor before officially retiring in the spring of 2026. This leadership change signals Apple's commitment to advancing its AI capabilities. In fact, Apple is looking to launch its upgraded Siri next spring, and there are even whispers that some of the assistant's new features could be powered by a custom version of Google’s Gemini AI model. 

Apple CEO Tim Cook has publicly welcomed Subramanya, emphasizing that AI has always been central to Apple's strategy and expressing confidence in Subramanya's expertise to drive their AI efforts forward, particularly with the goal of bringing a more personalized Siri to users next year. It's clear Apple is making some strategic moves to strengthen its position in the rapidly evolving AI landscape.
