## Key Points

- The author has updated towards substantially shorter AI timelines, with a 2x higher probability of full AI R&D automation by end of 2028 (now ~30% vs. previously ~15%)
- AI systems now show strong performance on massive easy-to-verify software engineering (ES) tasks, especially those that don't require much novel ideation (ESNI tasks)
- By end of 2026, the author expects AI to have a 50% reliability time horizon of years to decades on ESNI tasks, while 90% reliability time horizons will be much shorter (hours or days)
- Key capability: AI can autonomously develop test suites/benchmarks and iteratively optimize solutions against them, enabling progress on large software tasks even with occasional mistakes
- The gap between 50% and 90% reliability time horizons is much larger than expected, suggesting some tasks contain 'traps' that AI struggles with
- AI performance is especially strong on software replication tasks where they can create drop-in replacements for existing software
- The author reports AI systems successfully completing 3-12 months of useful work in SWE projects, including beating or coming close to beating complex closed-source software
- Poor AI 'taste' and judgment remains a bottleneck, especially for tasks requiring research insight or creative ideation
- AI can accelerate AI R&D mostly through engineering and infrastructure tasks, but research taste improvements are needed for full automation
- Updated forecasts: AI R&D parity (2028 median), AI stack + conflict parity (2034 median), Top Expert Dominating AI (2035 median)
