## Key Points

- OpenAI trained CriticGPT, a model based on GPT-4, to catch errors in ChatGPT's code output
- When people get help from CriticGPT to review ChatGPT code, they outperform those without help 60% of the time
- CriticGPT is being integrated into OpenAI's RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) labeling pipeline
- As ChatGPT becomes more accurate, its mistakes become more subtle, making them harder for human trainers to spot
- CriticGPT critiques are preferred by trainers over ChatGPT critiques in 63% of cases on naturally occurring bugs
- The model produces fewer 'nitpicks' (small unhelpful complaints) and hallucinates problems less often than ChatGPT
- OpenAI used additional test-time search to generate longer and more comprehensive critiques
- Current limitations: CriticGPT was trained on short ChatGPT answers, models still hallucinate, and can't handle errors spread across multiple parts of an answer
- The research shows applying RLHF to GPT-4 helps humans produce better RLHF data for GPT-4
- This work is a step toward evaluating outputs from advanced AI systems that are difficult for people to rate without better tools
