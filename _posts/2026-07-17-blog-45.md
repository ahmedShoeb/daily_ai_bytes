## Key Points

- Perplexity has released WANDR (Wide ANd Deep Research), a new benchmark with 500 realistic data-collection tasks for evaluating research agents
- WANDR focuses on 'wide-and-deep' research where agents must discover large sets of qualifying entities AND provide specific evidence for each one
- The benchmark uses a flexible qualification key hierarchy that can represent various real-world research patterns like competitive mapping, due diligence, and market analysis
- Grading is reference-free and evidence-verified - agents are graded against the evidence they cite rather than pre-defined answer keys
- In evaluations, Perplexity's Search as Code system led with 0.363 soft F1 and 0.133 hard F1, followed by Anthropic at 0.249 and 0.072
- The median task asks for 50 members, four records per member, and 245 records overall across the 500 tasks
- Key finding: Even the strongest systems struggle with complete coverage - Perplexity drops from 0.363 soft F1 to just 0.133 hard F1
- Larger target volumes and deeper hierarchies significantly reduce performance across all systems tested
- The benchmark shows that finding usable pages is relatively easy, but providing complete, correct evidence for each record is much harder
