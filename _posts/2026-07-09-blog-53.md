## Key Points

- TeraWulf (NASDAQ:WULF) shares rose 13% on Monday after announcing a long-term AI infrastructure lease with Anthropic
- The 20-year lease agreement with Anthropic is expected to generate approximately $19 billion in contracted revenue over the initial term
- The lease covers capacity at TeraWulf's Justified Data Campus in Hawesville, Kentucky, which is designed to support around 401 megawatts of critical IT load
- Initial capacity is expected to come online in the second half of 2027, with full buildout targeted for early 2028
- TeraWulf CEO Paul Prager called this a "landmark partnership" that validates the company's strategy of securing long-duration customer commitments
- TeraWulf also announced selling its 50.1% ownership interest in the Abernathy Joint Venture to an investor group led by Fluidstack
- The transaction values TeraWulf's investment at approximately $450 million, representing a premium to invested capital
- The Abernathy divestment allows TeraWulf to redeploy capital into wholly owned AI infrastructure projects while retaining operational control
- The Abernathy joint venture was developing a 168 MW AI data center campus in Texas, which Fluidstack will continue to lead
