## Key Points

- ELT (Elastic Looped Transformers) is a new parameter-efficient class of visual generative models based on recurrent transformer architecture
- Unlike conventional generative models that use deep stacks of unique transformer layers, ELT employs iterative, weight-shared transformer blocks to drastically reduce parameter counts
- The approach uses Intra-Loop Self Distillation (ILSD) where student configurations are distilled from teacher configurations to ensure consistency across model depth
- ELT enables Any-Time inference capability with dynamic trade-offs between computational cost and generation quality using the same parameter count
- The framework yields a family of elastic models from a single training run
- ELT achieves a 4× reduction in parameter count under iso-inference-compute settings while maintaining competitive performance
- On class-conditional ImageNet 256×256, ELT achieves FID of 2.0
- On class-conditional UCF-101, ELT achieves FVD of 72.8
- ELT significantly shifts the efficiency frontier for visual synthesis
- The paper was submitted in April 2026 with revisions, representing cutting-edge research in computer vision and pattern recognition
