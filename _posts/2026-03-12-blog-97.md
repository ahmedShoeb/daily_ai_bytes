## Key Points

ChatGPT remains the dominant consumer AI product with 2.7x more web traffic than #2 Gemini and 2.5x more mobile users, reaching 900 million weekly active users
The AI market is splintering into three distinct geographic ecosystems: Western tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), China's homegrown products (DeepSeek, Doubao), and Russia's local offerings
Creative tools are evolving with video generation showing the most movement - Chinese models like Kling AI lead in quality, while US models like Veo 3 are catching up
Agentic AI is becoming mainstream with products like OpenClaw gaining rapid adoption, acquired by OpenAI in February 2026, and other agents like Manus and Genspark appearing
AI is moving beyond traditional browsers and apps into browsers themselves (Atlas, Comet), desktop tools (Claude Code reaching $1B ARR), and embedded in existing software like Notion and Google Workspace
Consumer AI products now include established tools where AI is core to the experience like Canva, Notion, Grammarly, and CapCut, not just AI-native companies
The 'race for default AI' is intensifying with platforms building their own ecosystems - ChatGPT has 220+ apps while Claude focuses on professional tools like financial terminals and developer infrastructure
Singapore leads per capita AI adoption globally, followed by UAE and Hong Kong, while the US ranks 20th despite producing most leading AI products
Sora spent 20 days at the top of the US App Store with rapid initial growth but downloads decreased as it struggled to maintain viral social momentum
Methodology is becoming less effective at capturing AI usage as it moves from destinations to features embedded in tools people already use daily
