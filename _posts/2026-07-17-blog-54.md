## Key Points

- DeepSeek, the Chinese large language model developer, is reportedly preparing for a 2027 IPO debut, which could happen as early as end of 2026
- The company is looking to raise around $1.5 billion in new funds at approximately a $71 billion valuation
- This comes just a month after DeepSeek raised $7 billion in its first-ever outside funding round at around a $50 billion valuation
- The China-based startup, founded in 2023, gained attention last year for releasing AI technology that was more efficient and cost-effective than U.S. model makers
- In June 2026, DeepSeek accounted for nearly 23% of all tokens processed by enterprise AI gateway Vercel, compared to Anthropic's 32%
- DeepSeek continues to demonstrate how Chinese open source models are performing close to top U.S. AI labs despite U.S. export controls on chips
- The company's cloud service runs on chips made by Chinese company Huawei Technologies
- Major investors in DeepSeek include Tencent and Beijing's National Artificial Intelligence Industry Investment Fund
