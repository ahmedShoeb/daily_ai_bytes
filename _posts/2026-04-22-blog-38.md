## Key Points

Anthropic published updates to Claude Opus's system prompt between versions 4.6 (Feb 5, 2026) and 4.7 (Apr 16, 2026)
Key changes include: the developer platform is now called the "Claude Platform", new tools like Claude in Powerpoint are mentioned, and expanded child safety instructions with a new <critical_child_safety_instructions> tag
Claude 4.7 tries to be less pushy and respects users who want to end conversations rather than trying to keep them engaged
New guidance encourages Claude to act rather than clarify minor details, preferring to use available tools to resolve ambiguities instead of asking users
A new <acting_vs_clarifying> section emphasizes completing tasks rather than stopping partway through
Claude 4.7 has a tool_search mechanism to check for available capabilities before claiming it lacks access to something
The model is encouraged to be less verbose and keep responses concise to avoid overwhelming users
New guardrails against simple yes/no answers to complex or controversial questions to avoid screenshot attacks
Removed sections from 4.6 that warned against using emotes and certain words like "genuinely" and "honestly", suggesting the model no longer misbehaves in those ways
Updated political knowledge cutoff - removed specific language about Donald Trump being president, reflecting the model's new reliable knowledge cutoff date of January 2026
