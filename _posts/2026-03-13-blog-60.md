## Key Points

OpenAI introduces IH-Challenge, a training dataset that strengthens instruction hierarchy, safety steerability, and prompt injection robustness in AI models.
AI systems receive instructions from multiple sources: system messages (safety policies), developers, users, and online data - creating potential conflicts that need proper prioritization.
OpenAI's models follow a clear instruction hierarchy: System > Developer > User > Tool, where higher-priority instructions are more trusted.
When instructions conflict, models must prioritize correctly - for example, refusing user requests that violate system safety policies, or ignoring malicious instructions in tool outputs.
Three pitfalls of naive reinforcement learning for instruction hierarchy are identified: complex instructions that cause failures, subjective/nuanced conflicts, and models learning useless shortcuts like overrefusal.
IH-Challenge tasks are designed to be instruction-following-simple, objectively-gradable with Python scripts, and have no trivial shortcuts for high reward.
Testing on GPT-5 Mini-R shows improved performance on instruction hierarchy benchmarks, generalization to new attacks, and maintains usefulness without overrefusal.
Safety steerability improves - models better resolve conflicts with safety specifications present, achieving higher refusal rates on unsafe requests without decreasing helpfulness overall.
Prompt injection resistance improves substantially, with the IH-trained model showing better performance on both academic benchmarks and internal prompt injection evaluations.
OpenAI is releasing the IH-Challenge dataset publicly to support further research in this crucial area of AI safety and reliability.
