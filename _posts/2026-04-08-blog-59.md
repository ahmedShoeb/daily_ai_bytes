## Key Points

- The system proposes a novel approach where LLMs systematically build and maintain a persistent wiki rather than using traditional RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
- Unlike RAG where knowledge is rediscovered on every query, this system compiles knowledge once and keeps it updated, allowing for compounding knowledge accumulation
- The architecture has three layers: raw sources (immutable documents), the wiki (LLM-owned markdown files), and a schema file that defines the wiki structure and workflows
- The LLM handles all maintenance tasks like updating cross-references, flagging contradictions, and keeping summaries current across potentially dozens of pages in one pass
- The human's role is to curate sources, ask good questions, and guide analysis while the LLM handles the tedious bookkeeping that causes humans to abandon wikis
- Systems include two key navigation files: index.md (content catalog) and log.md (chronological record of ingests and queries)
- The approach is inspired by Vannevar Bush's 1945 concept of Memex - a personal knowledge store with associative trails between documents
- Recommended tools include Obsidian for browsing, Obsidian Web Clipper for acquiring sources, qmd for search functionality, and Dataview for dynamic content queries
- The pattern can apply to personal tracking, research, book analysis, business wikis, competitive analysis, and various other knowledge-intensive domains
- The document is intentionally abstract to allow users and their LLM agents to collaboratively create specific implementations tailored to their needs
