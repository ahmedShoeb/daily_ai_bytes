## Key Points

- Qualcomm CEO Cristiano Amon revealed the company is working on over 40 different AI wearable device designs, including jewelry, earbuds with cameras, pins, and watches
- The company announced two new platforms: Snapdragon Reality Elite for mixed-reality glasses and START (Scalable Turnkey AI-Ready Toolkit) for AI devices
- Snapdragon Reality Elite delivers major performance improvements: up to 60% GPU boost, 30% CPU boost, and 160% NPU improvement over previous XR platform
- The new platform can run a 3-billion-parameter language model at 45 tokens per second for responsive AI interactions
- Snapdragon Reality Elite supports 4.4K per-eye resolution at 90 fps for sharper visuals and reduced motion sickness
- Qualcomm is positioning itself as the foundational silicon layer for the post-smartphone computing era
- START includes a white-label program with three reference designs: audio+camera setup, monocular display, and binocular display
- Initial partners include eyewear manufacturers Inspecs and O'Neill (owned by TitanFlex)
- First devices to use Snapdragon Reality Elite include XREAL Project Aura and an upcoming device from Play for Dream
- Amon believes new hardware form factors will emerge as companies seek to gather more real-world data to power AI agents
