## Key Points

The article explores the limitations of current RL (Reinforcement Learning) and RLVR (Reinforcement Learning from Verifiable Rewards) training paradigms, arguing they're insufficient for achieving true AGI
Current AI training focuses on verifiable tasks in deterministic, replayable environments, but many real-world skills require learning from sparse, ambiguous data in complex, non-stationary environments
The author introduces 'grindability' as a crucial factor - the ability to run parallel rollouts in deterministic simulators, which works for coding/math but fails for domains like computer use, business building, or politics
Continual learning (on-the-job weight updates) is essential but faces sample inefficiency problems - gradient updates require massive data while real-world deployment provides sparse signals
Proposes On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD) as a solution to distill session experience back into weights with dense supervision, more efficient than standard RL or SFT
Suggests 'dreaming' as a speculative technique where AIs build simulations to rehearse skills internally, creating more training data from limited real-world experience
Envisions a 2027 future where AIs primarily improve through continual learning from deployment experience rather than pre-release training, with shared learning across all users
Identifies economic and technical barriers: enterprise privacy concerns, batched inference costs, and the challenge of learning from sparse user feedback like simple thumbs up/down
