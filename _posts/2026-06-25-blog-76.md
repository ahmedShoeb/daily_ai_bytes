## Key Points

- Tencent is testing an AI assistant called 'Xiaowei' within WeChat (Weixin) on a small scale in China
- Users can interact with Xiaowei via text or voice, communicate with friends, and launch mini-programs within WeChat
- This is Tencent's first integration of AI into WeChat, leveraging the app's massive user base of over 1.4 billion monthly active users
- Experts note that an assistant wired into WeChat can complete tasks rather than just provide answers, giving Tencent a unique advantage
- The move is part of Tencent's broader strategy to challenge rivals Alibaba, DeepSeek, and Zhipu in China's competitive AI market
- Tencent has poached an OpenAI researcher as its chief AI scientist this year and develops its own Hunyuan AI models
- Tech companies are focusing on 'AI agents' - digital assistants that can carry out complex tasks across apps
- Tencent executives have been considering AI integration into WeChat since last year as a potential new revenue stream
