## Key Points

- Cognition introduced FrontierCode, a new benchmark that measures code quality and mergeability rather than just correctness
- Unlike existing benchmarks that only test functional correctness, FrontierCode evaluates whether code would actually be merged by maintainers
- The benchmark was developed in collaboration with 20+ world-class open-source maintainers who invested 40+ hours per task
- FrontierCode has three difficulty levels: Diamond (50 hardest tasks), Main (100 hardest), and Extended (150 tasks)
- Current top models struggle significantly, with Claude Opus 4.8 achieving only 13.4% on the Diamond level
- The benchmark employs novel grading techniques including reverse-classical tests, code scope analysis, and adaptive classical grading
- FrontierCode claims an 81% lower false positive rate compared to SWE-Bench Pro
- The evaluation considers multiple quality dimensions: correctness, test quality, scope discipline, style, and adherence to codebase standards
- Tasks are designed with human-like, concise prompts (one-third the length of existing benchmarks)
- The benchmark focuses on evaluating whether AI models can write production-ready, maintainable code that respects subjective quality standards
