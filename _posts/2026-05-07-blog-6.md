## Key Points

LLMs are used by over a billion people globally primarily for writing assistance, but they significantly distort the meaning of written content
LLMs change the conclusions and stance of writing, moving essays toward more neutral positions and altering argument types
Human users report a paradox of preferences - they're satisfied with LLM output while acknowledging significant loss of personal voice and creativity
LLMs introduce larger semantic shifts than human editors, even when prompted only for grammar corrections
LLM-generated writing forms tight clusters in semantic space, indicating homogenization compared to diverse human-written content
LLMs systematically restructure grammar toward less personal, more formal styles, removing first-person experience-based argumentation
LLMs increase emotional language usage across both positive and negative emotions, and increase analytical/statistical language while decreasing personal experience arguments
In scientific peer review (ICLR 2026), LLMs gave scores 10% higher than humans and focused on different evaluation criteria like reproducibility and scalability
Human-written scientific reviews were more likely to comment on clarity and relevance, while LLM reviews focused more on technical criteria
The study suggests AI-generated content is subtly distorting written language across cultural institutions including politics, science, and personal communication
