## Key Points

Perceptron Inc. released its flagship video analysis reasoning model, Mk1, priced 80-90% cheaper than major competitors like Anthropic's Claude Sonnet 4.5, OpenAI's GPT-5, and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro
Mk1 costs $0.15 per million input tokens and $1.50 per million output tokens, compared to approximately $2.00 for GPT-5 and $3.00 for Gemini 3.1 Pro
The model achieved superior performance on industry benchmarks, scoring 85.1 on EmbSpatialBench (beating Google's Robotics-ER 1.5) and 72.4 on RefSpatialBench (massively outperforming GPT-5m and Sonnet 4.5)
Mk1 processes native video at up to 2 frames per second across a 32K token context window with temporal continuity, maintaining object identity through occlusions
The model features 'Physical Reasoning' capabilities allowing it to understand object dynamics, cause-and-effect, and the laws of physics in real-world settings
Perceptron uses a dual licensing strategy: closed-source Mk1 via API for enterprise use, and open-weights Isaac series models for edge and low-latency deployments
Founded by former Meta FAIR researchers Armen Aghajanyan and Akshat Shrivastava, the company spent 16 months developing a 'multi-modal recipe' from the ground up
Early applications include auto-clipping sports highlights, robotics teleoperation, manufacturing quality control, and wearable assistants on smart glasses
The model demonstrates strong general world knowledge, correctly identifying and dating vintage footage from 1906 NYC skyscraper construction
Perceptron provides a developer platform with SDK features including Focus, Counting, and In-Context Learning functions for specialized applications
