## Key Points

- Article introduces BenchBench, a new benchmark that tests AI models' ability to create their own evaluation benchmarks for other models
- The benchmark assesses both creators (models making benchmarks) and solvers (models solving benchmarks) with surprising results about model capabilities
- GPT-5.2 emerged as the only clear winner, successfully creating useful benchmarks that other models struggled to solve
- Other top models like GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.6 performed poorly as benchmark creators, either making too-easy tasks or unsolvable puzzles
- Gemini models showed creativity in benchmark creation but produced brittle tasks, with Gemini 3.1 Pro being described as 'by far the most creative'
- The benchmark revealed an interesting divergence between Creator and Solver capabilities - some models are better solvers than creators and vice versa
- BenchBench tests creativity, self-awareness of model capabilities, and provides novel insights not highly correlated with existing benchmarks
- The benchmark aims to identify AI's ability to understand its own strengths/weaknesses and create evaluations that push frontier models further
- BenchBench is described as 'the next major benchmark' that could help automate and improve AI evaluation processes moving forward
