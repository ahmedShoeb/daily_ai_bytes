## Key Points

Xiaomi Robotics introduces Xiaomi-Robotics-1, a foundation model that addresses robotics' data scarcity problem through large-scale embodiment-free pre-training
The model uses 100,000 hours of UMI (Universal Manipulation Interface) trajectories across 1,700+ scenarios for pre-training
A key innovation is an automatic annotation pipeline powered by vision-language models that converts video clips into language descriptions of scene state transitions
The training follows a two-stage approach similar to LLMs: large-scale pre-training for breadth followed by post-training for embodiment and instruction alignment
Results show clean scaling behavior - as pre-training data and model size increase, real-robot success rates improve steadily without saturation
After post-training, Xiaomi-Robotics-1 achieves state-of-the-art results on four major simulation benchmarks, with relative gains ranging from 2.6% to 58.3%
The model demonstrates impressive data efficiency, learning new complex tasks like phone packing and laundry loading with under 10 hours of demonstrations per task
For efficient adaptation to new tasks, the model achieves 75% overall success rate with under 10 hours/task and 85% with under 40 hours/task
This represents a practical solution to robotics' scaling problem by leveraging embodiment-free data to break the robot data bottleneck
The approach suggests that robotics can now follow similar scaling laws to language and vision models
