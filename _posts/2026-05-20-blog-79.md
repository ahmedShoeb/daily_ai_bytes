## Key Points

- Runway is an AI video-generation startup with three founders who met at NYU's Tisch School of Arts rather than having traditional Silicon Valley pedigrees
- The company has pivoted from simply creating video generation tools (Gen-4.5) to pursuing 'world models'—AI systems that learn directly from observational data of how the world works
- Runway believes the next frontier of AI won't be built from text but from video and world models that understand reality directly, not just human descriptions of it
- The company is now valued at $5.3 billion and added $40 million in annual recurring revenue in Q2 2026
- Runway launched its first world model in December and plans another this year, targeting applications in interactive entertainment, gaming, robotics training, drug discovery, and climate modeling
- Despite raising $860 million (including $315 million recently from AMD Ventures and Nvidia), Runway faces competition from Google's Veo video model and Genie world model
- Google is seen as Runway's biggest threat, competing in both video generation and the race toward world models
- Experts note that no one has yet proven the jump from video intelligence to generalized reasoning via world models, and compute resources remain a critical challenge
- The company has 155 employees across global offices and has been used in productions like 'Everything Everywhere All At Once'
- Runway's founders believe their non-Silicon Valley background and scrappy revenue-focused approach gives them an advantage over well-funded competitors
