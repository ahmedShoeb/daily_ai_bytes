## Key Points

- OpenAI signed a deal with Amazon Web Services (AWS) to sell its AI products to the U.S. government for classified and unclassified work
- The partnership follows OpenAI's earlier deal with the Pentagon to allow military use of its AI models on classified networks
- This move positions OpenAI to compete directly with Anthropic, which has deep AWS integration and has been Amazon's AI investment partner
- AWS will distribute OpenAI products across its public-sector customer base including government cloud environments like AWS GovCloud and AWS Classified Regions
- OpenAI retains control over its technology, deciding which models are made available and requiring AWS to provide notice before enabling sensitive government/intelligence customers
- OpenAI can require additional safeguards for particular deployments and will coordinate directly with customers on deployment terms
- The deal could unlock more enterprise contracts as government contracts are often seen as a stamp of trust and reliability
- This expansion comes amid controversy as Anthropic was recently named a supply-chain risk by the DOD and is suing the Pentagon over surveillance and autonomous weapons concerns
