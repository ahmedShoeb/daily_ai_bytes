## Key Points

- Google DeepMind's AlphaProof Nexus autonomously solved 9 out of 353 open Erdős problems, including two questions unanswered for 56 years
- The system proved 44 out of 492 open conjectures from the Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences and settled a 15-year-old question about Hilbert functions in algebraic geometry
- Inference costs were just a few hundred dollars per problem, making it surprisingly cost-effective
- The system combines Gemini 3.1 Pro for proof generation with Lean's formal language compiler for verification and error feedback
- It uses four agent variants ranging from simple LLM+compiler feedback to complex evolutionary systems with population sharing and rating
- The simplest agent (just LLM + compiler) could also solve all nine Erdős problems, though at higher cost on difficult ones
- Mathematicians found value even in failed proof attempts, which helped deepen understanding of problems
- The system is already being used in ongoing quantum optics and graph theory research
- OpenAI recently solved Erdős problems using pure natural language approaches, while DeepMind focuses on systematic, scalable tools for everyday research
- AI's overall success rate on Erdős problems remains low at 1-2%, concentrated on easier tasks
