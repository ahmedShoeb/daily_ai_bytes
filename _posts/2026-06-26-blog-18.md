## Key Points

- Krea 2 is a series of foundation models focused on creative exploration in image generation, designed to expose a broad visual space for creative navigation rather than producing a single polished default style
- The models use a multi-stage training pipeline including pretraining, midtraining, supervised finetuning, preference optimization, and reinforcement learning, with architectural improvements like grouped-query attention and sigmoid-gated attention
- Training infrastructure was built from scratch using PyTorch with FSDP2 and tensor parallelism, featuring custom data systems and observability tools for large-scale training stability
- Key innovations include a prompt expander that transforms simple user prompts into richer visual descriptions, and a style-reference system allowing users to guide generation with reference images while controlling style strength and mixing
- The team intentionally avoided using AI-generated images in pretraining to prevent model biases, instead focusing on carefully curated datasets with broad domain coverage
- Architectural choices include using Qwen 3 VL as text encoder, FLUX 2 VAE, and techniques like iREPA for faster convergence, with thorough ablations across attention mechanisms and conditioning methods
- Reinforcement learning uses multi-reward optimization including aesthetic scoring, prompt following, text rendering, and artifact detection, with careful prompt curation to prevent reward hacking
- The models rank among top performers on text-to-image leaderboards, scoring 2nd place among models from independent labs
- Future work includes scaling to MoE architectures, multi-teacher distillation, architectural simplification to unify components, and expanding capabilities to editing and native 2K/4K generation
