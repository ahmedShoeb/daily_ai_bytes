## Key Points

- 'Doom loops' are repetitive degeneration patterns in AI models where they emit the same span repeatedly, exhausting context windows, especially common in small reasoning models on hard problems.
- Liquid AI developed 'Antidoom' using Final Token Preference Optimization (FTPO) to target specific loop-starting tokens and train models to prefer coherent alternatives.
- Three mechanisms cause doom loops: overtrained tokens + uncertainty (common words like 'Wait', 'So', 'the'), prior context reinforcement, and greedy sampling at low temperatures.
- The method identifies the first token of repetitive loops, generates alternative plausible tokens, and trains models using a specialized FTPO algorithm that differs from DPO.
- Results show significant improvements: early LFM2.5-2.6B checkpoint reduced doom-looping from 10.2% to 1.4%, and Qwen3.5-4B dropped from 22.9% to 1% under greedy sampling.
- The training approach uses LoRA with high ranks (128-256) and optimal learning rates around 4e-6 to 2e-5, with early stopping to prevent over-training.
- Multiple rounds of Antidoom can be beneficial as fixing initial loops can expose new failure points in the distribution.
- The code and training pipeline are available open-source at github.com/Liquid4All/antidoom.
