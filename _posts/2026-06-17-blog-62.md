## Key Points

- iOS 27 developer beta contains built-in Extensions framework that would allow users to choose between ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini within Siri
- Apple didn't announce this feature at WWDC despite having the system ready and discussing with AI providers
- Three main reasons for the omission: EU regulatory standoff over Digital Markets Act, OpenAI's potential legal action over their partnership, and Apple wanting Siri AI to stand on its own first
- Apple confirmed Siri AI won't launch in the EU due to unresolved negotiations over third-party access concerns
- OpenAI is reportedly preparing legal action against Apple over their current ChatGPT integration deal
- Apple rebuilt Siri from scratch after scrapping an earlier version that didn't meet their vision
- Bloomberg's Mark Gurman found Siri AI in beta to be buggy with slow responses and limited capabilities
- The Extensions framework could turn Siri into a platform layer allowing different AI models for different tasks
- Claude and Gemini would gain native access to over 1.5 billion Apple devices through Extensions
- iOS 27 beta code also reveals references to foldable devices and touch-screen MacBooks
