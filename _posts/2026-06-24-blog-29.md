## Key Points

- John Jumper, a Nobel Prize-winning scientist, announced he's leaving Google DeepMind after nearly 9 years to join rival company Anthropic
- Jumper shared the news in a post on X, expressing gratitude to DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis for taking a chance on him
- He was a key member of the AlphaFold team that won the 2024 Nobel Prize in chemistry for predicting protein 3D structures
- Jumper praised DeepMind as 'a special place' and said he'll continue to be excited about their future discoveries
- Bloomberg reports Jumper was instrumental in developing Google's coding tools, which the company has struggled to sell to businesses
- This follows Character AI co-founder Noam Shazeer also leaving DeepMind this week, though he's joining OpenAI instead
