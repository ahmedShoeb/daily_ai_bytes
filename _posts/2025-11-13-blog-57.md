## Key Points

- OpenAI is preparing to release a family of GPT-5.1 models: GPT-5.1, GPT-5.1 Reasoning, and GPT-5.1 Pro.
- Enterprise customers will have the option to opt out of experimental model releases.
- A general availability date of November 24th is referenced for the GPT-5.1 family, likely for enterprise accounts.
- Individual Plus and Pro users might gain earlier access to the new models, potentially within the upcoming week.
- One of the models, possibly GPT-5.1 Thinking, is already being tested as Polaris Alpha on OpenRouter.
- Community feedback suggests high performance in creative writing and benchmark tests.
- OpenAI's strategy involves segmenting models for specialized use cases and customer segments.
- The timing aligns with increased competition from models like Kimi K2 Thinking and the anticipated Gemini 3.

## Key Topics Discussed

Hey podcast listeners, have you heard the latest from OpenAI? They're gearing up to launch a whole new family of GPT-5.1 models, including GPT-5.1, GPT-5.1 Reasoning, and GPT-5.1 Pro! It looks like they're sticking to their strategy of clear versioning, similar to what we saw with GPT-5. Now, for the big enterprise players, there's a cool feature where they can opt out of these new, potentially experimental releases, which is great for managing those big production workloads. The code even hints at a general availability date of November 24th for the GPT-5.1 family, likely for those enterprise accounts. But here's the exciting part for individual Plus and Pro users: you might get your hands on these new models even sooner, possibly within the next week! This timing is super interesting, especially with competitors like Kimi K2 Thinking and Google's anticipated Gemini 3 also making waves. And get this, one of these models, possibly GPT-5.1 Thinking, is already being tested under the name Polaris Alpha on platforms like OpenRouter. The community is buzzing about its high performance in creative writing and benchmark tests, which really makes you wonder if public access is just around the corner. This staged rollout from OpenAI really shows their focus on tailoring models for specific uses and customer needs, continuing their pattern of improving their core models to meet the ever-growing demands from both businesses and the AI research community.
