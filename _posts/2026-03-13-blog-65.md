## Key Points

- Data and AI agents have struggled in deployments because they lack proper context to understand business definitions, data sources, and organizational knowledge
- The initial focus on 'text to SQL' capabilities wasn't enough - agents need context about business definitions like 'revenue growth' and 'quarters' which vary across organizations
- Modern data stacks centralized data but created new challenges as data agents couldn't navigate messy, disparate enterprise data sources and outdated definitions
- A 'context layer' is emerging as a solution - a repository that ties together business logic, data sources, tribal knowledge, and governance rules for agents
- Context layers differ from traditional semantic layers by including more elements like canonical entities, identity resolution, tribal knowledge, and governance guidance
- Building a modern context layer involves: accessing all relevant data, automated context construction using LLMs, human refinement of tribal knowledge, agent connection via APIs, and self-updating flows
- The market is evolving with three approaches: data gravity platforms (Databricks, Snowflake), existing AI data analyst companies adding context features, and new dedicated context layer companies
- Companies like OpenAI and Palantir have demonstrated successful internal implementations, showing the complexity and effort required for effective data agents
- The industry is still early in solving the context problem, with open questions about where context layers will live and whether they'll be standalone products
- Effective data agents require blending technical infrastructure challenges with human operational challenges of tribal knowledge collection
