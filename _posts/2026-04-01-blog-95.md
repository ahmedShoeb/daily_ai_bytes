## Key Points

- Meta's next-gen AI model codenamed 'Avocado' has been delayed from March to at least May 2026 after internal testing showed it falls short of leading systems from Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic
- Meta is testing multiple Avocado variants including Avocado 9B (9 billion parameters), Avocado Mango (multimodal with image generation capabilities), Avocado TOMM (Tool of many models), Avocado Thinking 5.6, and Paricado (text-only conversational)
- Evidence shows Meta is routing some user requests through Google's Gemini models to fill capability gaps while Avocado matures
- Meta's AI leadership has reportedly discussed temporarily licensing Google's Gemini technology, confirming strategic discussions about external partnerships
- Avocado represents a shift from Meta's open-source tradition as it's expected to be proprietary, unlike previous Llama models
- The model has access to various internal tools and can solve complex math problems that earlier Llama models couldn't, though competitors solved them months earlier
- Despite delays, Avocado would still represent a meaningful upgrade for Meta's hundreds of millions of users across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp
