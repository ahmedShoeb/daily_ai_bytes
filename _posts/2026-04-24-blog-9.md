## Key Points

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman criticized Anthropic's new cybersecurity model Mythos, accusing the company of using fear-based marketing tactics to make the product sound more impressive than it actually is
Anthropic released Mythos earlier this month to a small cohort of enterprise customers, claiming it's too powerful for public release due to concerns about cybercriminals weaponizing it
Altman suggested this rhetoric serves to keep AI technology in the hands of a small, exclusive elite group of people
The OpenAI CEO used the analogy: 'We have built a bomb, we are about to drop it on your head. We will sell you a bomb shelter for $100 million' to describe Anthropic's approach
Altman implied that fear-based marketing helps maintain AI control within a limited group, stating 'You can justify that in a lot of different ways'
The article notes that fear-based marketing isn't unique to Anthropic, with much of the AI industry leveraging scare tactics and hyperbole
Critics have called Anthropic's rhetoric about Mythos being too powerful for public release 'overblown'
The article acknowledges that apocalyptic AI rhetoric has come not just from activists but also from the tech companies selling the technology, including Altman himself
