## Key Points

Clem Delangue, CEO of Hugging Face, argues that comparing open-weight models to closed APIs is like comparing 'an engine to a car' - they're fundamentally different systems with different advantages
He predicts the number of AI builders will explode from low millions today to potentially 100 million people who can train, fine-tune, and optimize models themselves
Hugging Face sold nearly 10,000 Reachy Mini open-source desktop robots, which Delangue sees as crucial for changing public perception of AI by enabling hands-on building experiences
He counters cybersecurity arguments against open source, pointing out that open systems are patched faster and give more defenders capabilities than attackers
Delangue warns about lobbying in Washington DC against open source AI, arguing it would hurt US AI leadership and concentrate power in a few companies
He believes local AI (running models on device) will become dominant for many use cases due to cost savings, privacy, and control, even if models aren't frontier-level
The Hugging Face platform is adapting for agents, which Delangue predicts may outnumber human users on the platform by the end of this year
He sees AI literacy evolving beyond just reading/writing to include skills like model training, dataset building, and local deployment
Delangue cites Camus' 'The Myth of Sisyphus' as an important philosophical influence for founders in AI, emphasizing finding joy in the journey of building
