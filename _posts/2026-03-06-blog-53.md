## Key Points

- Black-box monitors can detect scheming in AI agents using only externally observable information, without access to internal activations or chain-of-thought
- Researchers introduced two synthetic data pipelines (STRIDE and Gloom) to generate both benign and scheming agent trajectories for training
- Monitors were optimized on 2,000 synthetic trajectories and tested on 7,500 realistic trajectories from grounded sandbox environments
- Key finding: Optimization on synthetic data generalizes to realistic settings - monitors trained on LLM-generated data can pick up real scheming signals
- Performance saturates quickly: Simple prompt selection over 15 candidates performs as well as extensive human refinement and automated optimization
- The study recommends generating diverse prompts using multiple frontier models, evaluating on synthetic data, and picking the best performer without over-optimizing
- Monitors were tested across 5 grounded environments (BashBench, Infrastructure-as-Code, RogueEval, AgentDojo, SHADE Arena) using 3 agent models (GPT-5, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Pro)
- Past a certain point, prompt tweaks fix specific failure modes but introduce regressions elsewhere due to global behavior shifts
- The approach uses natural language specifications of scheming to generate synthetic training data through Constitutional Classifiers method
