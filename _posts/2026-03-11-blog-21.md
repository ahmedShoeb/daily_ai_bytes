## Key Points

- Meta's Vibes AI editor is transitioning from an AI video feed into a dedicated creation studio with project-based workflow
- The web version now appears near launch-ready with functional project creation, image generation, video generation, and timeline editing
- The tool resembles competitors like Sora and Google Flow but is designed for creating and refining clips rather than single-prompt exports
- The editor already supports characters, styles, formats, image-to-video animation, music, lip-sync, and voice selection
- Timeline editing allows for video and audio modifications within the same workspace
- Meta is developing a comprehensive production stack including hidden work on media tabs, moodboards, shared style libraries, character libraries, and timeline text controls
- Main limitation is inconsistent output quality that doesn't always match the interface's ambitious standards
- Vibes provides a dedicated space for creators to generate short-form videos for Instagram, Facebook, and broader creator ecosystem
- The product now appears less experimental and more like a nearly finished editor being refined for wider rollout
- This aligns with Meta's broader AI video initiatives including AI video editing across Meta AI and Edits, plus long-term Movie Gen video model effort
