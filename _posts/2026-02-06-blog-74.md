## Key Points

- This project investigates whether Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning, already successful in language models for tasks like math, can be adapted to improve *autoregressive image generation*.
- Three CoT techniques are explored: **test-time computation scaling** (ORM, PARM, PARM++), **Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)**, and **combining these techniques** for better results.
- **PARM** (Potential Assessment Reward Model) dynamically evaluates each step of image generation, blending strengths from existing reward models.
- **PARM++** adds a *self-correction mechanism* to help generative models fix earlier mistakes in image generation.
- The research was accepted for presentation at **CVPR 2025**, a top-tier computer vision conference, and the paper is available on arXiv.
- The team has open-sourced training code, datasets, and checkpoints for models like **ORM, PARM, and DPO**, making it accessible for further experimentation.
- A baseline model (**Show-o**) is provided, along with fine-tuned and zero-shot versions of ORM, PARM, and DPO-based approaches.
- The repository includes detailed instructions for setting up the environment, downloading models, and running fine-tuning or training scripts.
- If you use this work, the team kindly asks you to cite their paper using the provided BibTeX entry.
- The project also links to additional research in **3D vision, mathematical visual reasoning, and multi-modal search engines** by the same team.
