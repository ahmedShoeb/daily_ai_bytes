## Key Points

- Microsoft researchers introduce a self-evolving 'ResearchAgent' that can plan, execute, and evaluate research projects autonomously
- ResearchAgent performed better than expert human researchers in a benchmark test involving AI model architecture design
- The system works with text and code, using language models to plan multi-step research projects
- ResearchAgent successfully discovered an improved transformer variant architecture in testing
- Researchers are concerned about potential large-scale labor displacement if similar systems become widely adopted
- Google's research team continues to make progress on automated scientific discovery and lab automation systems
- The development of research automation systems could radically change how scientific knowledge is produced and who produces it
