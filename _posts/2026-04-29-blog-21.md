## Key Points

- This research paper presents a framework for building dynamic language models that can adapt to language evolution over time.
- The study focuses on Spanish language evolution as a case study to demonstrate the framework's effectiveness.
- The framework addresses the problem of language model obsolescence as languages naturally change and evolve.
- Researchers created a method to track and incorporate linguistic changes into language models continuously.
- The approach maintains model performance while accommodating new vocabulary, semantic shifts, and grammatical changes.
- The study shows how dynamic models outperform static models when dealing with evolving language patterns.
- The framework uses temporal analysis to identify significant linguistic changes and update models accordingly.
- This work has important implications for developing more resilient NLP systems that don't become outdated as quickly.
- The methodology could be applied to other languages beyond Spanish to create adaptive language processing systems.
