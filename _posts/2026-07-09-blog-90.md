## Key Points

- PyTorch Monarch has been successfully ported to AMD GPUs using ROCm, expanding fault-tolerant distributed training beyond CUDA environments
- Traditional checkpoint-based fault tolerance wastes computation when failures occur, but Monarch enables dynamic recovery without halting entire training jobs
- Monarch uses an actor-based runtime with process mesh abstraction and supervision trees for hierarchical fault handling
- The port required significant engineering work including collective communications conversion using hipify_torch, GPU memory management extensions, and RDMA integration
- The system demonstrated successful fault-tolerant training on AMD MI300 and MI355 clusters with 128-256 GPUs, maintaining steady loss convergence despite injected failures
- Key achievement: AMD GPUs now support advanced fault-tolerance mechanisms previously limited to CUDA environments
- The architecture combines Monarch (orchestration), TorchFT (fault tolerance), and TorchTitan (training engine) for resilient training stacks
- Dynamic recovery workflow includes local restart of failed nodes and peer checkpoint transfer without global checkpoint reloads
- The approach works seamlessly across both SLURM (HPC) and Kubernetes (cloud native) environments
- Future directions include expanding NIC support, improving runtime performance, and supporting more training frameworks on ROCm
