## Key Points

- The article draws parallels between the development of nuclear weapons and current AI development, specifically comparing the historical Manhattan Project to modern AI companies
- It references the historical meeting between Oppenheimer and President Truman where Oppenheimer expressed guilt over the atomic bomb, while Truman emphasized the government's role in deciding its use
- The author presents a hypothetical scenario: what if a private corporation (called 'McBombalds Corporation') rather than the government had developed nuclear weapons and wanted to control their use
- This analogy directly addresses Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei's position that AI is akin to a nuclear weapon and his company's reluctance to provide unlimited government access
- The article argues that when a technology has profound national security implications, the fundamental question is who should control it - private entities or governments
- It notes that the current debate over AI access happened alongside American military involvement aimed at preventing nuclear proliferation in Iran, highlighting the contradiction
- The core argument: If AI represents an existential technology similar to nuclear weapons, then questions about private vs. government control become paramount
- The author suggests critics of government AI access must justify why private entities deserve control over such powerful technology
- The piece concludes with a pragmatic view that governments (specifically America) may be the most responsible stewards for technologies with civilization-altering potential
