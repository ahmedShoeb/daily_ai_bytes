## Key Points

OpenAI is scaling up its Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program to thousands of verified individual defenders and hundreds of teams responsible for critical software defense.
The company is introducing GPT‑5.4‑Cyber, a variant of GPT‑5.4 specifically fine-tuned for defensive cybersecurity use cases with fewer capability restrictions.
GPT‑5.4‑Cyber includes binary reverse engineering capabilities, allowing security professionals to analyze compiled software for malware potential and vulnerabilities without source code access.
OpenAI's approach is guided by three principles: democratized access, iterative deployment, and investing in ecosystem resilience.
The company has been building its cyber defense program since 2023, starting with the Cybersecurity Grant Program and evolving through the Preparedness Framework.
Codex Security, launched earlier this year, has contributed to over 3,000 critical and high fixed vulnerabilities across the ecosystem.
Individual users can verify identity at chatgpt.com/cyber to gain access to TAC, while enterprises can request access through OpenAI representatives.
OpenAI recognizes cyber risk as an accelerating threat and believes that defenses must scale alongside model capabilities.
The company aims to expand access based on evidence and real signals of trust rather than relying on manual decisions.
OpenAI expects current safeguards to be sufficient for upcoming models but anticipates the need for more expansive defenses for future, more powerful models.
