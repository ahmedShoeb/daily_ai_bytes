## Key Points

- Anthropic collaborated with Mozilla to test Claude AI's ability to find security vulnerabilities in Firefox
- Claude Opus 4.6 discovered 22 vulnerabilities in Firefox over two weeks, with 14 classified as high-severity
- These findings represent almost a fifth of all high-severity Firefox vulnerabilities fixed in 2025
- The AI was particularly effective at finding Use After Free vulnerabilities in the JavaScript engine
- Mozilla fixed most issues in Firefox 148.0, protecting hundreds of millions of users
- While Claude could find vulnerabilities efficiently, it was less effective at creating exploits - succeeding only twice in hundreds of attempts
- The cost of finding vulnerabilities was an order of magnitude cheaper than creating exploits
- Anthropic developed 'task verifiers' to help Claude check its own work when analyzing code
- Frontier language models are now world-class vulnerability researchers but defenders currently have an advantage
- The collaboration provides a model for how AI-enabled security researchers and maintainers can work together
