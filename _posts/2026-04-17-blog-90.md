## Key Points

Fluidstack, a startup building specialized AI data centers, is in talks to raise $1 billion at an $18 billion valuation, potentially led by Jane Street
This would more than double the company's valuation from just months ago when it was reportedly raising around $700 million at a $7.5 billion valuation
In December 2025, the previous round was led by AGI-focused fund Situational Awareness and backed by Stripe's Collison brothers, former GitHub CEO Nat Friedman, and investor Daniel Gross
Google was considering contributing $100 million to the round as recently as February, according to reports
The startup gained significant credibility with a landmark $50 billion deal with Anthropic in November to build custom AI data centers in Texas and New York
Fluidstack specializes in AI-specific infrastructure rather than general-purpose cloud computing like AWS or Google Cloud
The Anthropic deal prompted Fluidstack to relocate its headquarters from the UK to New York and pull out of a €10 billion AI project in France to focus on U.S. opportunities
In addition to Anthropic, Fluidstack counts Meta, Poolside, Black Forest Labs, and Mistral among its customers
Anthropic's rapid growth has created demand for more infrastructure capacity beyond their existing AWS and Google Cloud partnerships
