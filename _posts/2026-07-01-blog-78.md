## Key Points

- Anthropic updated its data pipeline to track Claude usage patterns more granularly, including hourly usage patterns and new artifact classification
- Usage patterns mirror work-life rhythms: personal use spikes on weekends (from 35% to nearly 50%), work tasks drop while creative/entrepreneurial activities increase
- Tax-related queries surged 8x around April 15 (US tax deadline), showing how external deadlines drive AI usage
- 93% of Claude conversations produce concrete artifacts like documents, explanations, code, or creative work
- Higher-wage occupations use more compute-intensive features and get more valuable outputs from Claude
- Claude Code shows significantly more AI autonomy than chat/Cowork - users delegate entire tasks with single prompts vs. iterative collaboration
- Claude typically responds at a higher comprehension level than prompts by about one year of education
- Survey of 9,700 users shows 35% expect AI to handle most of their work within a year
- Early-career workers report highest AI exposure but heaviest automation users are most optimistic about job security and pay impacts
- Women use Claude differently - less automated usage, more iterative collaboration, and less use of Claude Code
- Most users hope for AI-augmented work that preserves meaningful tasks while automating drudgery
