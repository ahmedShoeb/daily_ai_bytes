## Key Points

- Anthropic has signed a new agreement with Google and Broadcom for multiple gigawatts of next-generation TPU capacity expected to come online starting in 2027
- This expansion will power Anthropic's frontier Claude models and help serve extraordinary demand from customers worldwide
- Anthropic CFO Krishna Rao described this as 'the most significant compute commitment to date' to keep pace with unprecedented growth
- Anthropic's run-rate revenue has now surpassed $30 billion, up from approximately $9 billion at the end of 2025
- The number of business customers spending over $1 million annually has doubled to over 1,000 in less than two months
- The vast majority of new compute will be sited in the United States, expanding their November 2025 commitment to invest $50 billion in American computing infrastructure
- Anthropic trains and runs Claude on multiple AI hardware platforms including AWS Trainium, Google TPUs, and NVIDIA GPUs
- Claude remains the only frontier AI model available on all three major cloud platforms: Amazon Web Services (Bedrock), Google Cloud (Vertex AI), and Microsoft Azure (Foundry)
- Amazon remains Anthropic's primary cloud provider and training partner, with continued work on Project Rainier
- This partnership builds on previous TPU capacity announcements and deepens existing relationships with Google Cloud and Broadcom
