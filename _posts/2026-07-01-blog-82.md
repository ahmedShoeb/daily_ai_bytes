## Key Points

- Paul Meade, Apple vice president in charge of the Vision Pro headset, is reportedly leaving Apple to join OpenAI's hardware team
- Meade also led development of Apple's upcoming AI-powered smart glasses, which Apple hopes will compete with Meta's wearables
- The Vision Pro headset was not commercially successful despite being a high-cost product
- This departure is reportedly connected to John Ternus' upcoming elevation to Apple CEO and his hardware engineering team reorganization
- Some Apple vice presidents reportedly felt demoted after Ternus' team shakeup
- OpenAI is already collaborating with Apple's former chief design officer Jony Ive on an AI device
- CEO Sam Altman described OpenAI's planned device as 'more peaceful and calm than an iPhone'
- Despite this collaboration, reports last fall indicated OpenAI was struggling with getting device details right
- TechCrunch has contacted both Apple and OpenAI for official comment on these reports
