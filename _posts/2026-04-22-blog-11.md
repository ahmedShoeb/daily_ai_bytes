## Key Points

- A study of 500 companies using Cursor AI shows that better AI models lead to greater AI demand, with a 44% increase in weekly messages per user during an 8-month period
- The study covered the releases of Opus 4.5 and GPT-5.2, two models that delivered significant advances in AI coding capability
- Media and advertising saw the largest increase in AI usage (+54%), followed by software/developer tools (+47%) and finance/fintech (+45%)
- Initially, developers used better models for more of the same work, but after 4-6 weeks they began using AI for more complex tasks
- High-complexity AI messages grew 68% during the study period compared to 22% for low-complexity messages
- The largest increases in task categories were documentation (+62%), architecture (+52%), code review (+51%), and learning (+50%)
- The data suggests developers are shifting from writing code to managing AI-generated code, with more focus on documentation, review, and architectural work
- The findings show AI doesn't just replace existing work but expands economic activity by enabling developers to tackle more ambitious projects
