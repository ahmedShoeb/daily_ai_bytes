## Key Points

Google DeepMind released a new paper titled 'Measuring Progress Toward AGI: A Cognitive Taxonomy' that presents a scientific framework for evaluating AI systems' progress toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)
The framework draws on decades of research from psychology, neuroscience, and cognitive science to identify 10 key cognitive abilities important for general intelligence in AI systems
The 10 cognitive abilities are: Perception, Generation, Attention, Learning, Memory, Reasoning, Metacognition, Executive functions, Problem solving, and Social cognition
Google proposes a three-stage evaluation protocol that benchmarks AI performance against human capabilities across these cognitive abilities
Google is partnering with Kaggle to launch a hackathon called 'Measuring progress toward AGI: Cognitive abilities' to develop evaluations for the five cognitive abilities with the largest evaluation gaps: learning, metacognition, attention, executive functions, and social cognition
The hackathon offers a total prize pool of $200,000 with $10,000 awards for top submissions in each of the five tracks and $25,000 grand prizes for the best overall submissions
Submissions for the hackathon are open from March 17 through April 16, with results announced on June 1
Participants will use Kaggle's Community Benchmarks platform to build and test their evaluations against frontier models
The framework aims to address the lack of empirical tools for evaluating systems' general intelligence and tracking progress toward AGI
Google believes cognitive science provides an important piece of the puzzle for measuring AGI progress, alongside other methods and approaches
