## Key Points

- Stanford DAM project provides an interactive dataset tracking memory and storage prices over time in the spirit of John C. McCallum's classic dataset
- The visualization shows price per gigabyte over time for DRAM, NAND flash, and HBM memory types on a log scale with both nominal and inflation-adjusted dollars
- DRAM pricing is broken out by generation including Pre-DDR (SDRAM), DDR, DDR2, DDR3, DDR4, and DDR5
- Includes accelerator cost breakdown estimates from Epoch AI showing quarterly costs across Nvidia, AMD, Google (TPU), and Amazon (Trainium) by component
- HBM pricing by generation (HBM2e through HBM4) with projections, though these are sparse industry-analyst estimates as HBM has no public spot market
- Data sources include: McCallum memory-price dataset (1957-2024), Amazon retail price history via Keepa for modern data, and Epoch AI modeling for HBM
- Methodology notes that $/GB defaults to cheapest retail prices in nominal USD, with inflation-adjusted option available using US CPI-U
- The dataset is regularly updated with DRAM and NAND $/GB refreshing monthly and HBM data updating quarterly
- Important caveats: Prices reflect cheapest retail, not contract or average; data may track end-of-life generation clearance; and HBM figures are modeled estimates
- The project is compiled and maintained by David Shim at Stanford DAM project with downloadable CSV data available
