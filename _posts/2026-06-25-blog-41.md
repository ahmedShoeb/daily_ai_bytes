## Key Points

- The article predicts AI model scaling from 2023 to 2031, focusing on compute scaling, sparsity, and hardware constraints.
- Compute scaling between 2026 and 2028 models is estimated at 15x (from 1.3e27 to 2e28 FLOPs), with sparsity increasing from 8x to 30x for additional effective compute boost.
- Models are increasingly constrained by pretraining data shortages, with 2028 models facing a 4.5x data shortfall and 2031 models facing a 15x shortfall.
- Model parameters scale dramatically: 10T total params in 2026 (8x sparsity), 240T in 2028 (30x sparsity), to 1.4 quadrillion in 2031 (30x sparsity).
- Hardware constraints evolve significantly: HBM read times decrease from 20ms (H100) to 14ms (Feynman chips), enabling larger models to be served efficiently.
- Token generation speed targets (80 tokens/s) constrain pipeline length, affecting maximum model sizes that can be served practically.
- 2024 was identified as the most constrained year for serving compute-optimal models, while 2028 onward sees constraints shifting primarily to pretraining compute.
- Different sparsity levels affect training efficiency: 8x sparsity gives 3x compute multiplier, 30x gives 6x multiplier, driving up data requirements.
- Infrastructure scaling predicts AI companies having 3-4 GW of compute by end of 2026, growing to 10 GW by end of 2027, 40 GW by 2030.
- The analysis includes detailed calculations of pipeline constraints, HBM specifications, and the interplay between model size, sparsity, and hardware limitations.
