## Key Points

- Z.ai (formerly Zhipu) is projected to become the first independent Chinese AI firm with about $1bn in annual sales
- The company is achieving commercial success while open-sourcing its most capable models like GLM-5.2
- Z.ai's 2025 revenue was approximately $100 million, up 132% year-over-year
- Much of its revenue comes from on-premises deployments for state-owned enterprises and financial institutions
- Annualized recurring revenue from its open platform reached $234 million, growing sixtyfold in one year
- The company employs an unconventional business model: free open-source models drive adoption, which then sells cloud services, support, and on-premises deployments
- Despite rapid revenue growth, Z.ai remains lossmaking with projections suggesting profitability by 2028
- The company's valuation has surged to roughly $112 billion after a rally of over 1,000% since its January listing
- The success demonstrates China's strength in commercializing AI technology rather than pure subsidies
- The $1bn milestone would be significant for Chinese AI, showing the industry can start paying for itself rather than just burning cash
