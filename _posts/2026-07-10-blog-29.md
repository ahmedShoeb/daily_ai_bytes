## Key Points

.### Key Points about DeepSeek's Chip Plans
- DeepSeek, a Chinese AI startup competitive with OpenAI and Anthropic, is planning to enter the silicon business
- The company has been working on this move for about a year, meeting with hardware partners and hiring engineers
- The focus is specifically on developing data center chips optimized for AI inference (not training)
- One major motivation is to reduce reliance on both Huawei (which controls half of China's data center chip market) and Nvidia
- US export controls preventing Nvidia from selling advanced chips to China are a key driver for this initiative
- DeepSeek isn't alone - other Chinese tech giants like Alibaba and Baidu are also pursuing chip development
- Interestingly, US AI companies are making similar moves, with OpenAI recently announcing its Jalapeño chip with Broadcom
- OpenAI's chip strategy aims to reduce Nvidia dependence and gain Apple-like control over their tech stack
- Control over silicon and data center infrastructure provides competitive advantages in an AI market where compute access is increasingly constrained
