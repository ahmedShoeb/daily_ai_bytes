## Key Points

- Google is reportedly in discussions with Marvell Technology to develop two new AI chips - a memory processing unit and an inference-optimized Tensor Processing Unit (TPU)
- The talks have not yet produced a signed contract, but would position Marvell as Google's third design partner alongside existing partners Broadcom and MediaTek
- This reflects Google's strategic shift toward inference (serving AI models to users) as the dominant compute cost, rather than training models
- Google recently renewed its TPU agreement with Broadcom through 2031, indicating Marvell would supplement rather replace existing partners
- The seventh-generation Ironwood TPU, launched this month, is Google's first TPU specifically designed for inference workloads
- Inference has become the primary expense for AI companies as models serve billions of queries daily, creating demand for purpose-built inference silicon
- Marvell has been growing its custom silicon business, working with Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, and Google on various AI chips
- TrendForce projects custom chip sales will grow 45% in 2026 compared to 16% growth in GPU shipments, with the custom AI accelerator market expected to reach $118 billion by 2033
- Google's multi-supplier strategy (Broadcom, MediaTek, Marvell, TSMC) aims to avoid dependence on a single chip supplier and reduce costs
- This diversification approach mirrors automotive industry practices where multiple suppliers handle different components to maintain competitive leverage
