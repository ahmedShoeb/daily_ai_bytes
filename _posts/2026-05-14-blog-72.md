## Key Points

- Thinking Machines is announcing a research preview of interaction models that handle interaction natively rather than through external scaffolding
- These models are designed to collaborate with humans in real time, continuously taking in audio, video, and text while thinking, responding, and acting simultaneously
- The approach addresses the limitations of current turn-based AI models that create a narrow channel for human-AI collaboration
- Interaction models use a multi-stream, micro-turn design with 200ms chunks of input/output processing for near real-time responsiveness
- The system includes both an interaction model for real-time presence and a background model for sustained reasoning and tool use
- Key capabilities include seamless dialog management, verbal/visual interjections, simultaneous speech, time-awareness, and concurrent tool calls
- The TML-Interaction-Small model achieves state-of-the-art performance on both intelligence and interactivity benchmarks
- The architecture features encoder-free early fusion of audio and video inputs with minimal preprocessing
- Future work includes scaling model size beyond the current 276B parameter MoE, improving long session handling, and advancing alignment/safety for real-time interfaces
- Thinking Machines plans to open a limited research preview and invites community feedback on this new approach to human-AI collaboration
