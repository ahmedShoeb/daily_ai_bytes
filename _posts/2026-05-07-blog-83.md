## Key Points

- The article examines Y Combinator's ownership stake in OpenAI, which Sam Altman previously led as president before becoming OpenAI's CEO
- OpenAI was originally seeded by YC Research in 2016 when Altman was running Y Combinator
- According to sources, Y Combinator owns approximately 0.6% of OpenAI
- At OpenAI's $852 billion valuation, that stake is worth over $5 billion
- Paul Graham, Y Combinator co-founder, was quoted multiple times in a New Yorker investigation of Altman but never disclosed his financial stake
- The article questions whether Graham's multi-billion dollar stake in OpenAI might influence his public opinions about Sam Altman's trustworthiness
- Gary Marcus previously noted that while Altman holds no direct equity in OpenAI, he has an indirect stake through his ownership in Y Combinator
