## Key Points

- OpenAI has reached an agreement with the U.S. Department of War for deploying advanced AI systems in classified environments with strong guardrails
- The agreement includes explicit language prohibiting the use of OpenAI tools for domestic surveillance of U.S. persons, including through commercially acquired personal information
- The Department of War affirmed that OpenAI services will not be used by intelligence agencies like the NSA without a new agreement
- OpenAI has three main red lines: no mass domestic surveillance, no direction of autonomous weapons systems, and no high-stakes automated decisions like 'social credit' systems
- The deployment will be cloud-only, with OpenAI retaining control over its safety stack and having cleared personnel in the loop to monitor usage
- The agreement includes multi-layered protections: deployment architecture, contractual language, and AI expert involvement
- OpenAI believes this agreement has more guardrails than previous agreements for classified AI deployments, including Anthropic's
- The company states they will not deploy without safety guardrails and will not provide 'guardrails off' or non-safety trained models
- OpenAI requested that the same terms be made available to all AI companies and hopes this creates a pathway for collaboration between labs and government
- If the government violates terms, OpenAI reserves the right to terminate the contract, and the agreement references current laws and policies even if they change in the future
