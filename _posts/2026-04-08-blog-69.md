## Key Points

Apple celebrated its 50th anniversary as Nasdaq brought opening bell festivities to Apple Park, marking a pivotal moment as the company faces AI challenges
Apple has struck a multiyear deal to use Google's Gemini AI for Siri - a surprising move that reverses the payment relationship from Google paying Apple for search placement to Apple paying Google for AI technology
The deal raises privacy concerns as Apple's core philosophy under Steve Jobs has been keeping user data private rather than using it for advertising
Apple has been slow to AI compared to rivals, with Siri updates delayed but still promised by year-end, despite launching Apple Intelligence in 2024 with mixed consumer response
Apple has avoided massive AI infrastructure investments unlike Amazon, Microsoft, Alphabet and Meta, which are collectively spending hundreds of billions annually
Apple's 'fork in the road' situation stems from misreading the AI market and the speed of technological change, according to analysts
Siri launched in 2011 and had a 5-year lead on competitors, but stagnated after Steve Jobs' death according to co-founders who say the original vision was far more ambitious
Apple's bet is that AI will move from cloud to device as models shrink, addressing privacy concerns by processing queries locally on AI-capable silicon integrated since 2017
Apple faces competition from OpenAI's collaboration with former Apple design chief Jony Ive, who is reportedly working on screenless AI devices that could challenge Apple's hardware advantage
The future may involve AI-enabled accessories that work alongside phones rather than replacing them, potentially playing to Apple's strengths if the iPhone remains central to computing
