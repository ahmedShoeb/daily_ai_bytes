## Key Points

- Inception Labs introduced Mercury 2, claiming it's the world's fastest reasoning language model with generation speeds of roughly 1,000 tokens per second
- Mercury 2 significantly outperformed Google's DiffusionGemma on the AIME 2026 benchmark, scoring 90% vs. DiffusionGemma's 69.1%
- Both models use diffusion techniques instead of traditional sequential text generation, allowing parallel processing similar to image generators like Stable Diffusion
- Augment Code reported an 82% drop in latency and 90% cost reduction when swapping Mercury 2 for Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 while maintaining output quality
- Mercury 2 is a paid, closed-weight API model, while DiffusionGemma is free and open-weight on Hugging Face
- The model excels in speed-sensitive, high-volume workflows like real-time coding, multi-agent systems, voice interfaces, and latency-sensitive autocomplete
- Inception Labs was founded by Stanford professor Stefano Ermon and raised $50 million from investors including Nvidia, Andrew Ng, and Andrej Karpathy
- Diffusion models create a 'flow' experience where AI feels like it's keeping pace with users rather than making them wait between thoughts
- These models enable complex AI systems to use specialized sub-agents more efficiently by making utility calls cheap and fast
