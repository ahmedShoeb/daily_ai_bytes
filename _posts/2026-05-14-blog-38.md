## Key Points

AARD (Agentic Auto-Regressive Diffusion) is a novel architecture that solves the fundamental challenge of semantic drift and narrative collapse in long video synthesis
The system formulates long video synthesis as a closed-loop process using a Retrieve–Synthesize–Refine–Update cycle that works segment-by-segment
It features three core components: Multimodal Video Memory for tracking progression, Adaptive Segment Generation for switching generation modes, and Hierarchical Test-Time Self-Improvement
AARD introduces LVBench-C, a challenging benchmark with non-linear entity and environment transitions to stress-test long-horizon consistency
The system is training-free and built on existing video diffusion models, enabling videos from 1-10 minutes in length
AARD outperforms state-of-the-art baselines by up to 30% in consistency and 20% in narrative coherence
It uses flexible video segments that can span one or multiple shots or scenes, with segment context dictating narrative and actions
The system supports both extrapolation (synthesizing forward from a beginning frame) and interpolation (connecting fixed beginning and ending frames)
Examples include single-scene videos like 'A stylish woman walks down a Tokyo street' and complex multi-scene narratives like 'The Great Museum Heist' (10 minutes)
The architecture is particularly effective at handling complex scenarios where entities and environments appear, disappear, and reappear across long horizons
