## Key Points

- Mistral AI introduced Forge, a system that enables enterprises to build frontier-grade AI models trained on their proprietary knowledge
- Forge addresses the gap between generic AI models (trained on public data) and enterprise needs by allowing organizations to train models on internal documentation, codebases, and operational records
- The system allows models to learn enterprise-specific vocabulary, reasoning patterns, and constraints, making them understand internal terminology and workflows
- Key customers already using Forge include ASML, DSO National Laboratories Singapore, Ericsson, European Space Agency, and Reply
- Forge supports multiple training approaches: pre-training on large internal datasets, post-training refinement for specific tasks, and reinforcement learning for alignment with internal policies
- Organizations maintain full control over models, data, and intellectual property, with models governed by internal policies and compliance requirements
- The system is agent-first by design, built for autonomous agents like Mistral Vibe to fine-tune models, optimize hyperparameters, and manage training processes
- Forge supports both dense and mixture-of-experts (MoE) architectures, plus multimodal inputs for text, images, and other data formats
- The platform enables continuous improvement through reinforcement learning and evaluation frameworks against internal benchmarks
- Potential applications span government agencies, financial institutions, software teams, manufacturers, and large enterprises for domain-specific AI solutions
