## Key Points

- AI is making faster progress in mathematics than in any other field, providing insights into how AI will transform other domains
- Mathematics shows a 'jagged landscape' where AI excels in some areas (geometry) but struggles in others (combinatorics)
- Three potential paths for AI solving major math problems: brute force computation, connecting disparate fields (like Montgomery-Dyson connection), or building entirely new theoretical frameworks
- The verification challenge: Some conceptual breakthroughs (like Galois's group theory) take a century or more to be recognized as valuable
- AI's mathematical progress raises questions about whether we'll understand AI-generated proofs of major problems like the Riemann hypothesis
- AI's advantage in mathematics stems from both verifiability AND grindability - you can run thousands of parallel attempts without real-world constraints
- Writing presents different challenges than mathematics because it requires theory of mind and understanding reader psychology, not just verifiable correctness
- The role of mathematicians may shift toward curation and explanation as AI handles theorem-proving and potentially even problem formulation
- AI could accelerate mathematics 10x-100x but economic applications may be limited to certain fields like PDE-solving for engineering
- Students interested in mathematics should focus on understanding how value is created in their field rather than just chasing existing benchmarks
