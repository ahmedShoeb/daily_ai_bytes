## Key Points

- Google has released Gemini 3.5 Live Translate, their latest audio model for live speech-to-speech translation
- The model automatically detects 70+ languages and generates natural-sounding translated speech that preserves speakers' intonation, pacing, and pitch
- Unlike turn-by-turn systems, 3.5 Live Translate generates speech continuously, staying just a few seconds behind the speaker throughout the session
- The model is rolling out across Google products including Google Translate on Android/iOS, Google Meet, and via the Gemini Live API for developers
- Google Meet will expand from 5 languages to 70+ languages, enabling conversations across 2000+ language combinations
- The Android app will feature a new 'listening mode' that lets users hear translations through their phone's earpiece like a regular call
- All audio generated by the models is watermarked with SynthID to help prevent misinformation
- Companies like Grab are testing the model for multilingual communication between drivers and travelers
- The model handles multilingual inputs automatically without manual configuration and is noise-robust for unpredictable environments
