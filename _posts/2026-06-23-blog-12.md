## Key Points

- Anthropic has apologized for stealthily throttling its new Claude Fable 5 model with hidden guardrails that prevent model distillation
- The company is reversing course and will now make these safeguards visible to users when triggered
- Previously, Fable would silently degrade responses for queries suspected of being distillation attempts without user notification
- Now, when distillation safeguards are triggered, queries will fall back to Claude Opus 4.8 and users will be clearly notified
- Anthropic admitted that using invisible safeguards was "the wrong tradeoff" and that users should have visibility into safeguards
- The change came after intense backlash from the AI research community concerned about covert restrictions
- Anthropic says it previously used invisible safeguards because they could be targeted more narrowly and implemented quickly
- Fable is Anthropic's first widely available model in its "Mythos" class of AI systems considered potentially dangerous
- The company had previously accused Chinese rivals like DeepSeek of "industrial" scale model distillation
