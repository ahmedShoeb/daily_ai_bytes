## Key Points

- AI risk has shifted from text/output-based risks to system-level execution risks as models now trigger workflows and interact with live systems
- Only 36% of security leaders believe their security keeps pace with AI, while 90% of companies think they cannot defend against AI-driven threats
- Model Context Protocol (MCP) is the critical execution layer where AI outputs become system actions, handling tool selection, data transfer, and system interactions
- 1 in 35 generative AI prompts carries high risk of sensitive data leakage, affecting 87% of organizations deploying GenAI at scale
- MCP servers often run on developer laptops or containers with live credentials and no centralized control, creating 'Shadow AI' that escapes traditional security visibility
- 99% of organizations have exposed files accessible to AI tools without formal safeguards in place
- 63% of organizations have no formal AI governance policy, creating a critical ownership gap for the execution layer
- MCP provides a natural control point for AI risk since it follows defined paths through known servers and systems
- The MCPTotal platform is positioned as a solution that consolidates agent activity into a managed environment with policy enforcement and real-time visibility
- AI risk becomes 'real' when outputs lead to actions, and MCP is where this transition occurs
