## Key Points

- OpenAI acquired voice-cloning startup Weights.gg earlier this year, acquiring the six-person team and intellectual property
- Weights.gg's Replay catalog hosted unauthorized celebrity voice models including Taylor Swift, Samuel L. Jackson, President Trump, and dozens of other public figures
- The startup shut down its hosted services on March 31, 2026, several weeks before the acquisition was publicly reported
- OpenAI dispersed the team across multiple groups rather than keeping them together to build a follow-on product
- The acquisition appears to be more about removing unauthorized celebrity voice models ahead of OpenAI's planned IPO rather than technology acquisition
- Taylor Swift filed trademark applications for her voice and likeness in April 2026, while Samuel Jackson has publicly objected to his voice being cloned
- OpenAI maintains Voice Engine in limited preview on safety grounds and requires explicit consent for voice impersonation in its Realtime API
- OpenAI is preparing for a public listing by the end of 2026, which would require disclosure of material acquisitions like this one
- The company had already hired Instagram's former celebrity partnerships lead to manage Hollywood relationships earlier this year
- Multiple other companies including xAI and ElevenLabs offer voice cloning, making the technology a commodity rather than a unique capability
