## Key Points

Researchers introduce Edit-R1, a framework for applying verifier-based reinforcement learning to image editing tasks
The approach moves from simple scoring models to reasoning verifiers using chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning
Edit-RRM (Reasoning Reward Model) breaks editing instructions into distinct principles and evaluates images against each principle
The framework uses supervised fine-tuning as a 'cold-start' to generate reward trajectories
Group Contrastive Preference Optimization (GCPO) reinforcement learning leverages human pairwise preference data
The method outperforms powerful vision-language models like Seed-1.5-VL and Seed-1.6-VL as editing-specific reward models
Experiments show clear scaling trends with performance improving from 3B to 7B parameters
Edit-R1 enhances editing models like FLUX.1-kontext, demonstrating practical effectiveness
Provides interpretable, fine-grained rewards for image editing rather than overall scores
Addresses the bottleneck of lacking robust general reward models for all editing tasks
