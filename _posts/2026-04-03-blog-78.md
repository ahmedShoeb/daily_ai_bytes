## Key Points

Current chatbot interfaces create cognitive load that overwhelms users and offsets AI productivity gains, with disorganized conversations compounding the problem
Recent research shows less experienced workers are hurt most by poor AI interfaces, despite being those who could benefit most from AI assistance
Anthropic's Claude Cowork with Dispatch allows AI to work on your desktop and be controlled via phone, creating a personal assistant-like experience
Specialized interfaces like Claude Code exist for programmers, but most knowledge workers lack AI tools optimized for their professions
Google is experimenting with specialized interfaces like Stitch (AI-native design), Pomelli (marketing campaigns), and NotebookLM (research)
Open-source AI agent OpenClaw became wildly popular by letting users interact with AI through familiar messaging apps like WhatsApp and Telegram
The future may involve AI generating customized interfaces on-demand rather than requiring pre-built specialized interfaces
Claude now can generate interactive visualizations directly in conversations that users can adjust and modify
The article argues AI capability has outpaced accessibility, with chatbot interfaces being largely to blame for 'AI disappointment'
As interfaces improve, more people will be able to leverage what AI is truly capable of, making AI feel more capable even without model improvements
