## Key Points

This article presents a guide to evaluating multi-agent AI systems using macro evaluations (macro-evals) that look across populations of agent runs rather than individual interactions
The approach addresses a key challenge: agentic system failures often involve patterns across multiple runs, such as repeated handoff timing issues or missed signals by specialist agents
The methodology uses a simulated electric vehicle (EV) order workflow with specialist agents handling pricing, compliance, supply chain, factory routing, scheduling, and release decisions
The workflow separates evaluation into two levels: lower-level evals that grade individual agents and handoffs, and macro evals that discover recurring behavior patterns across many runs
The article demonstrates how to turn agent traces into compact documents for clustering, discover recurring patterns, and drill into high-impact patterns to identify where human inspection is needed
The approach uses a BERTopic-inspired method to cluster trace documents and identify behavior patterns, then applies AgentTrace-style analysis to find root causes
Key metrics include impact scores that combine pattern prevalence with severity to prioritize which issues to investigate first
The method helps teams move from thousands of agent events to a small number of understandable patterns that can be analyzed by both technical and business stakeholders
The article includes practical next steps for both AI engineering teams (improving regression suites, tracking patterns by version) and business stakeholders (validating risk scenarios, prioritizing policy design)
