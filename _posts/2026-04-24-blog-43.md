## Key Points

DNL reveals how neural networks can be catastrophically disabled by flipping just a few sign bits in model parameters with no training data or optimization required
The attack only needs write access to stored weights, making it practical through firmware exploits, rootkits, DMA attacks, or Rowhammer vulnerabilities
ResNet-50 collapses with 2 sign flips (99.8% accuracy drop), while vision models like Mask R-CNN and YOLOv8-seg fail with 1-2 flips in early layers
Large language models like Qwen3-30B and Nemotron 8B experience reasoning collapse to near-zero accuracy with just 2-32 flips targeting early blocks
Early-layer high-magnitude weights are universally vulnerable across CNNs, Transformers, and MoE architectures, causing feature map corruption
The method bypasses common defenses like weight quantization, pruning, and simple checksumming schemes
Selective hardening of just 0.1-1% most vulnerable weights provides practical defense without major performance overhead
Sign-bit flips are especially destructive as they instantly negate weights and are hardware-feasible through physical attacks
Mixture-of-Experts models are particularly vulnerable when attackers target different experts, compromising all token routing paths
The data-free nature makes detection and attribution exceptionally challenging for defenders
