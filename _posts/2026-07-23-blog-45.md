## Key Points

. Moonshot's upcoming Kimi K3 model (releasing July 27th) will be the largest open weights model ever with 2.8 trillion parameters, 1M token context window, native multimodality, and benchmarks rivaling Opus 4.8
The most important number is 16: K3 activates only 16 of 896 experts per token - under 2% of its expert weights per forward pass, representing the most consistent architectural trend in open models
Total parameters have grown ~20x since Mixtral, but active parameters have remained in a 17-49B band for 27 months, showing that labs are inflating total capacity while keeping per-token compute flat
Sparsity allows models to learn more from the same FLOPs, moving the cost from compute (rationed) to storage (cheaper) - 'the cheapest way to buy intelligence is to spend capacity'
Attention compression techniques are collapsing KV cache sizes (V4-Pro's cache is 10% of its predecessor's), working alongside sparse experts to reduce bandwidth demands
Sparsity democratizes compute costs for frontier inference (per-token costs similar to mid-size dense models) but does nothing for capacity - K3 weights alone are ~1.4TB at MXFP4, requiring 10+ H200s just to load
Two serving regimes emerge: low-batch environments can tier experts into cheap DRAM, while hyperscale serving pools everything in rack-level HBM across 64+ accelerators
The entire point of downloading open checkpoints is running them outside hyperscalers, and sparsity is the only reason self-hosting exists for trillion-scale models
Future improvement potential lies in smarter routers with deliberate locality - if labs train routers to create 'popular experts' and predictable paths, true tiering becomes viable even at high batch
