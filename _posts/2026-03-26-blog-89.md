## Key Points

Public markets have repriced the software sector, indicating that traditional terminal value assumptions are no longer valid
Companies must choose one of two paths: accelerate revenue growth by 10+ percentage points through new AI-native products within 12-18 months, OR rebuild for 40%+ true operating margins (including stock-based compensation)
The "comfortable middle" is over - companies stuck between high growth and high profits will face growth pressure, persistent dilution, and multiple compression
AI adoption must be transformative, not just bolted-on chatbots or copilot interfaces attached to existing products
For growth path: companies need to identify and empower key talent, create new AI-native products with 50% of R&D, and evolve to token/consumption-based pricing models
For profitability path: companies need radical cost discipline, flatten management layers, standardize implementation, and treat stock comp as a real expense
Both paths require rebuilding leadership teams and creating "refounding moments" for companies
The Broadcom/Vmware case study demonstrates that radical cost discipline and 61% EBITDA margins are achievable
Companies must move away from seat-based pricing toward token/consumption models as AI-driven workflows become more autonomous
Founders must explicitly choose a path and communicate this strategy clearly to investors and boards
