## Key Points

- Becoming a successful AI researcher requires both reading/learning and building/practicing - you can't just do one
- Research success often depends more on temperament and persistence than raw talent, with most days yielding no breakthroughs
- When starting out, focus on fundamental concepts that have endured for decades rather than chasing temporary trends
- Modern AI researchers often benefit from being relatively young and less attached to outdated methodologies
- Inspiration often strikes during non-work activities like walks, so it's important to step away from the keyboard
- Approach experiments with equanimity - both positive and negative results provide valuable information
- Don't get overly excited about good results initially, as they're often due to measurement errors or bugs
- Many successful projects involve extensive behind-the-scenes gruntwork before breakthroughs appear
- Researchers must be paranoid about bugs in complex deep learning stacks and investigate unexpected metrics
- Fast experimental feedback loops are crucial for productive research, despite some experiments taking weeks
