## Key Points

- The article tests frontier AI models (GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Opus 4.6) on real financial tasks using actual documents like earnings reports, investor presentations, and regulatory fee schedules
- Models perform much worse when working with visual documents (images, charts) compared to clean extracted text - accuracy drops by 16-20 percentage points
- When models had to answer purely from parametric knowledge (no documents), they scored 0-4% correct, showing the tasks genuinely test document reasoning
- Two main failure modes identified: 1) Visual extraction errors (reading wrong values from dense charts), 2) Reasoning failures (applying wrong financial operations even with correct values)
- Real financial work involves messy documents with nested tables, multi-panel charts, and footnotes, while AI benchmarks use cleaner layouts
- The gap between benchmark performance and real-world financial document analysis suggests AI displacement of financial analysts may be premature
