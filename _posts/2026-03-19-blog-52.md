## Key Points

OpenAI is in advanced talks with private equity firms including TPG, Advent International, Bain Capital, and Brookfield Asset Management to form a joint venture
The proposed deal has a pre-money valuation of about $10 billion
Private equity investors would commit about $4 billion and receive equity stakes in the venture
TPG would serve as the anchor investor, committing the most capital
All four firms would secure board seats in the joint venture, though no final decision has been made
The joint venture would distribute OpenAI's enterprise products across the PE firms' portfolio companies and beyond
The deal could give OpenAI faster access to corporate adoption while providing PE firms with AI solutions for their portfolio companies
Anthropic is also in discussions with private equity firms including Blackstone, Permira, and Hellman & Friedman for a similar joint venture worth about $1 billion
Both AI companies are aggressively courting private equity as they race to go public, possibly as soon as this year
The arrangement would give PE firms early access to OpenAI's enterprise tools and influence over how the technology is deployed across their portfolios
