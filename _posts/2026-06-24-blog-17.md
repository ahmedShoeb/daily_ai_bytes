## Key Points

- Author shares personal job search experience after a 6-year PhD in NLP at University of Washington, applying for Research Scientist/Member of Technical Staff roles
- Interviewed at 11 companies over 57 interviews, plus 46 recruiter calls and 16 post-offer chats
- Interview types include ML coding (most common), general coding, technical discussions, research discussions, behavioral interviews, math questions, and job talks
- Found interview preparation to be like being back in undergrad - took notes, studied fundamentals, and required full-time dedication
- Preparation included watching Stanford's Language Modeling from Scratch course and practicing implementation/debugging transformers
- Negotiation phase was surprisingly challenging - initial offers intentionally leave room for negotiation and putting in energy here can be equivalent to years of work
- Emphasizes the importance of managing emotions during job search process, which can be stressful and overwhelming
- Encourages PhD students to cherish their time while also preparing for industry job search
