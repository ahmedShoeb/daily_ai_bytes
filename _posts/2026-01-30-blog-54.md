## Key Points

HP announced new AI and gaming computers at CES 2025, highlighting the growing integration of AI in computing.
The HP EliteBook Ultra G1i notebook boasts a slim design and a next-gen AI processor capable of 48 TOPS, aiming for superior video conferencing.
The HP Z2 Mini G1a Workstation Desktop PC delivers transformative AI performance in a mini form factor, supporting 3D design, rendering, and local LLM processing.
HP is utilizing AMD Ryzen AI Max Pro processors in some machines, providing up to 16 CPU cores and the ability to assign significant RAM to the GPU.
HP also introduced new peripherals, including a quiet wireless mouse, a Voyager Legend series, and a rechargeable keyboard and mouse combo.

## Key Topics Discussed

Hey everyone, so HP really came to play at CES this year! They unveiled a whole bunch of new AI-powered computers, both laptops and desktops. This is a big signal that AI is moving beyond just being a buzzword and is actually being built into the hardware we use every day. 

Let's start with the laptops. The HP EliteBook Ultra G1i is really interesting. It's super slim and packed with a next-gen AI processor capable of 48 TOPS – that's a measure of how quickly it can handle AI tasks. HP is positioning this as a video conferencing powerhouse with a new 9MP camera and amazing audio features. They've also included a cool haptic touchpad that gives you customizable feedback. HP is also releasing a series of other AI laptops geared towards business clients.

But it's not just laptops! They also have the HP Z2 Mini G1a Workstation. This thing is tiny, but it's a serious workstation. It can handle things like 3D design, rendering complex graphics, and even running large language models locally. It’s using AMD's new Ryzen AI Max Pro processor which is designed specifically for AI workloads. It can also assign up to 96GB of RAM to the GPU! Plus, it’s built with a lot of recycled materials, which is awesome. The HP ZBook Ultra 14-inch mobile workstation is another standout. It’s thin, power-efficient, and also uses the AMD Ryzen AI Max Pro processor. 

And of course, they didn’t forget about accessories! New mice, keyboards, and headsets were also announced. Finally, HP also unveiled the HP OmniDesk Desktop PCs and the HP OmniStudio X Next-Gen AI PC, a 27-inch all-in-one that’s also jumping on the AI bandwagon.

Overall, HP is making a big bet on AI, and it looks like we're going to see a lot more AI-powered features in our computers going forward.
