## Key Points

- Apple filed a lawsuit against OpenAI alleging trade secret theft and breach of contract
- The lawsuit accuses OpenAI's Chief Hardware Officer Tang Tan (former 24-year Apple veteran) of using Apple's confidential project code names during recruiting
- OpenAI allegedly asked job candidates to bring Apple hardware components to interviews and coached departing Apple employees on evading security procedures
- Another employee, Chang Liu, is accused of keeping an Apple-issued laptop and downloading confidential technical documents about unannounced technologies
- The lawsuit claims OpenAI used Apple's proprietary metal finishing technique by misleading a partner about having Apple's permission
- Apple believes this is part of OpenAI's strategy to extract confidential information as it develops its own hardware product
- Industry analyst Ming-Chi Kuo suggests OpenAI could be developing an AI-powered smartphone that would compete with iPhone
- Apple wants the court to bar OpenAI from using its trade secrets and return confidential materials
- Apple says it raised concerns with OpenAI in February but received no response
- OpenAI responded with a public statement saying they have no interest in other companies' trade secrets
