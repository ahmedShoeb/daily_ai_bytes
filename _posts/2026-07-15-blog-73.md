## Key Points

- Johannes Heidecke, OpenAI's head of safety systems, is leaving the company as part of a reorganization
- Heidecke started at OpenAI in 2021 and informed staff via memo about his departure
- Saachi Jain, who previously led OpenAI's safety teams, will become interim head of safety systems
- Mia Glaese will become vice president of research and safety, overseeing safety teams reporting to her
- OpenAI is restructuring to integrate safety work more closely with frontier-model development
- Chief research officer Mark Chen emphasized safety should have 'earlier and more direct role' in model and product decisions
- The reorganization follows OpenAI's recent release of GPT-5.6, which was approved by the US government
- OpenAI still maintains a Head of Preparedness role hired earlier this year for severe risk mitigation
