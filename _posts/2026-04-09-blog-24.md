## Key Points

Google has quietly released a free offline-first AI dictation app called 'Google AI Edge Eloquent' on iOS
The app uses Gemma-based automatic speech recognition (ASR) models that are downloaded to work offline
It automatically filters out filler words like 'um' and 'ah' and polishes text after dictation pauses
Offers text transformation options including 'Key points', 'Formal', 'Short', and 'Long'
Can work in local-only mode or use cloud-based Gemini models when cloud mode is enabled
Imports keywords, names, and jargon from Gmail accounts and allows adding custom words
Provides transcription history tracking, words-per-minute speed measurement, and word count stats
Currently available only on iOS but App Store description references Android version coming soon
On Android, it can be set as default keyboard with system-wide access across text fields
Google is testing this experimental app to compete with popular dictation apps like Wispr Flow, SuperWhisper, and Willow
