## Key Points

- Caitlin Kalinowski, OpenAI's hardware team lead, resigned in response to the company's controversial agreement with the Pentagon
- Kalinowski criticized the deal as being rushed without proper guardrails defined for governance concerns
- She expressed concerns about surveillance of Americans without judicial oversight and lethal autonomy without human authorization
- OpenAI defended the agreement, stating it creates a responsible path for national security uses while maintaining red lines against domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons
- The controversy has damaged OpenAI's reputation with consumers, causing ChatGPT uninstalls to surge 295% while Anthropic's Claude app climbed to top of App Store charts
- OpenAI's deal with the Pentagon came after Anthropic negotiations fell through over safeguards against mass surveillance and autonomous weapons
- Kalinowski previously led Meta's augmented reality glasses team and joined OpenAI in November 2024
- She emphasized her decision was about principle rather than people and expressed respect for CEO Sam Altman and the OpenAI team
