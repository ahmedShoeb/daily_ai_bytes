## Key Points

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman is in ongoing talks with the White House about a potential government stake in the artificial intelligence company
Discussions have been ongoing for over a year, with Altman first sharing the idea with the Trump administration in 2025
As part of a potential agreement, OpenAI could donate equity to seed a 'Public Wealth Fund' that would enable citizens to participate in AI growth
President Trump confirmed the talks, stating concepts where 'the American public essentially becomes a partner' with AI companies
No official investment terms have been decided yet and details are still subject to change
OpenAI is valued at over $850 billion by private investors and is preparing for a potential IPO as soon as this year
The Trump administration has already taken stakes in Intel, IBM, and other quantum and critical mineral companies
Trump recently signed directives to accelerate AI adoption and asked AI companies to voluntarily provide government access to models 30 days before release
Altman has voiced support for the administration's approach to AI regulation and safety
Senator Bernie Sanders also discussed the sovereign wealth fund concept with Altman during a recent meeting
