## Key Points

Google has released Gemini 3.5 Live Translate, a new audio model for live speech-to-speech translation
The model automatically detects 70+ languages and generates natural-sounding translated speech that preserves speaker intonation, pacing, and pitch
Unlike traditional systems that wait for speakers to finish, Gemini 3.5 Live Translate generates speech continuously with only a few seconds delay
The model is rolling out across Google products: for developers via Gemini Live API and Google AI Studio, for enterprises in Google Meet, and for everyone via Google Translate apps
Google Meet will soon use 3.5 Live Translate, expanding from 5 languages to 70+ languages and enabling over 2000+ language combinations in meetings
A new 'listening mode' on Android lets users hear translations directly through their phone's earpiece, similar to a regular phone call
Companies like Grab are testing the model to enable multilingual communication between drivers and travelers for over 10 million voice calls per month
The model handles noisy environments and multilingual inputs without manual configuration, making it suitable for calls, meetings, lessons, and broadcasts
All audio generated is watermarked with SynthID to help prevent misinformation and ensure AI-generated content remains detectable
Google's translation technology now processes over a trillion words monthly for billions of users across its products
