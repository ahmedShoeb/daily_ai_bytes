## Key Points

- AI coding agents have become the first AI product people are paying for at volume because they directly speed up work for developers
- AI lab revenue is growing at unprecedented rates: Anthropic is up 3x since the start of the year, with projections showing continued explosive growth
- Despite massive revenue growth, AI labs face profitability challenges due to high research investments and infrastructure costs
- The AI industry faces persistent bottlenecks that shift upstream each year: GPU shortages (2023), HBM memory shortages (2024), datacenter power supply limitations (2025), and now US grid capacity constraints (2026)
- Memory costs are becoming critical as models require more HBM (High Bandwidth Memory), controlled by just two companies (SK Hynix and Samsung) commanding 90% of the market
- Datacenter construction is limited by power availability and skilled labor shortages, particularly electricians
- Semiconductor production is bottlenecked by TSMC factory capacity and ASML's lithography machines, with only ~50 produced annually at $350M each
- Compute demand is growing faster than infrastructure can be built out, creating a 'cash flow gap' between current revenue and future infrastructure investments
- Companies like Anthropic face capacity limits despite wanting to grow, forced to use premium 'scrap' capacity from various providers
- Users should diversify across AI models and prepare for potential price increases as AI labs try to become profitable
