## Key Points

- Meta's superintelligence chief Alexandr Wang reportedly told employees that Meta's upcoming model 'Watermelon' has 'caught up' with OpenAI's GPT-5.5 on internal AI benchmarks
- Wang stated Watermelon is still in training and uses 'an order of magnitude more compute' than Meta's previous model Muse Spark (internally codenamed Avocado)
- The report comes from Business Insider citing anonymous sources, and neither Meta nor OpenAI has confirmed the benchmark claims
- It's unclear which specific benchmarks Wang was referring to in the internal comparison
- Meta released Muse Spark in April 2026, which performed well on some benchmarks but still trailed leading rival models overall
- The article cautions practitioners to treat this as a leading indicator rather than verified results, since internal benchmark claims lack published methodology or third-party replication
- Meta has not provided a release timeline for the Watermelon model
- The main takeaway is Meta's aggressive compute scaling strategy, with Wang describing Watermelon using far more training resources than previous models
- Independent evaluations and published benchmark data are needed before making deployment decisions based on these claims
